21 Cuba

Cuba’s location gives access to and potential control of the Florida Straits and the Yucatán Channel in the Gulf of Mexico, which is the exit and entry route for the port of New Orleans and therefore a potential threat to the USA if under the control of another major power. In 1898, the USA declared war on Spain, which controlled Cuba at the time. In 1962 the Cuban Missile Crisis was a major incident during the Cold War with the Soviet Union.

22 The Destroyers for Bases Agreement

During the Second World War Britain agreed to swap the valuable bases it had accrued around the world for the USA’s warships – and the ability to stay in the war, effectively handing its naval power to the Americans.

42 c) The Canadian Shield

The Canadian Shield is a huge area of mostly volcanic rock with a thin layer of soil, covering almost half the country, from the Great Lakes all the way up to the Arctic Ocean. Although the inhospitable terrain limits the amount of inhabitable land, it contains one of the world’s richest areas for mining nickel, copper, silver, gold, aluminium and cobalt.
