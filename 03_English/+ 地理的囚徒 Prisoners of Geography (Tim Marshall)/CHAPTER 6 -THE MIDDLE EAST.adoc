=== 中东地理

It also contains the fertile region known as Mesopotamia, the ‘land between the rivers’ (the Euphrates and Tigris). However, the most dominant feature is the vast Arabian Desert and scrubland in its centre which touches parts of Israel, Jordan, Syria, Iraq, Kuwait, Oman, Yemen and most of Saudi Arabia including the Rub’ al Khali or ‘Empty Quarter’. This is the largest continuous sand desert in the world, incorporating an area the size of France. It is due to this feature not only that the majority of the inhabitants of the region live on its periphery, but also that until European colonisation most of the people within it did not think in terms of nation states and legally fixed borders.

[.my2]
它还包含被称为"美索不达米亚"的肥沃地区，即“两河之间”（幼发拉底河和底格里斯河之间）的土地。 +
然而，**其最显著的特征, 是位于中心的广袤阿拉伯沙漠和灌木丛，**这片沙漠与以色列、约旦、叙利亚、伊拉克、科威特、阿曼、也门, 以及沙特阿拉伯的大部分地区接壤，其中包括鲁卜哈利沙漠，又称“空域”。这是世界上最大的连续沙漠，面积相当于法国。正是由于这一特征，**该地区的大多数居民都生活在沙漠边缘，**而且**在欧洲殖民之前，这里的大多数人, 都没有"民族国家和法律规定的边界"观念。**

=== 中东的国家形成

The Ottoman Empire (1299–1922) was ruled from Istanbul. At its height it stretched from the gates of Vienna, across Anatolia and down through Arabia to the Indian Ocean. From west to east it took in what are now Algeria, Libya, Egypt, Israel/Palestine, Syria, Jordan, Iraq and parts of Iran.  +
It had never bothered to make up names for most of these regions; in 1867 it simply divided them into administrative areas known as ‘Vilayets’, which were usually based on where certain tribes lived, be they the Kurds in present-day Northern Iraq, or the tribal federations in what is now part of Syria and part of Iraq.

[.my2]
奥斯曼帝国（1299–1922）的统治中心, 位于伊斯坦布尔。鼎盛时期，其疆域从维也纳城门, 延伸至安纳托利亚，横跨阿拉伯半岛，直至印度洋。从西到东，它囊括了如今的阿尔及利亚、利比亚、埃及、以色列/巴勒斯坦、叙利亚、约旦、伊拉克以及伊朗的部分地区。 +
**奥斯曼帝国从未费心为这些地区中的大多数命名；1867 年，它只是简单地将它们划分为行政区域，**称为“维拉耶特”（Vilayets），**这些行政区域的划分, 通常基于特定部落的居住地，**例如居住在如今伊拉克北部的库尔德人，或是居住在如今叙利亚和伊拉克部分地区的部落联盟。

==== Sykes–Picot Agreement -> 英法划分"奥斯曼原领土"的势力范围

When the Ottoman Empire began to collapse, the British and French had a different idea. In 1916 the British diplomat Colonel Sir Mark Sykes took a chinagraph pencil and drew a crude line across a map of the Middle East. It ran from Haifa on the Mediterranean in what is now Israel to Kirkuk (now in Iraq) in the north-east. It became the basis of his secret agreement with his French counterpart François Georges-Picot to divide the region into two spheres of influence should the Triple Entente defeat the Ottoman Empire in the First World War. North of the line was to be under French control, south of it under British hegemony.

[.my2]
**当奥斯曼帝国开始瓦解时，英国和法国却另有打算。**1916 年，**英国外交官**马克·赛克斯爵士, 用油性铅笔**在中东地图上画了一条粗略的线。**这条线从"地中海"沿岸的"海法"（今以色列境内）, 一直延伸到东北部的"基尔库克"（今伊拉克境内）。**这条线, 成为他与法国外交官**弗朗索瓦·乔治·皮科秘密协议**的基础：如果协约国在第一次世界大战中击败奥斯曼帝国，就将该地区划分为两个势力范围。线以北由法国控制，线以南由英国统治。**







The term ‘Sykes-Picot’ has become shorthand for the various decisions made in the first third of the twentieth century which betrayed promises given to tribal leaders and which partially explain the unrest and extremism of today. This explanation can be overstated, though: there was violence and extremism before the Europeans arrived. Nevertheless, as we saw in Africa, arbitrarily creating ‘nation states’ out of people unused to living together in one region is not a recipe for justice, equality and stability.

[.my2]
**“赛克斯-皮科协定”一词, 已成为二十世纪前三分之一时期, 一系列决策的代名词. 这些决策背弃了对部落首领的承诺，并在一定程度上解释了当今的动荡和极端主义。**然而，这种解释可能有些夸大：在欧洲人到来之前，暴力和极端主义就已经存在。尽管如此，正如我们在非洲所见，随意地将原本不习惯在同一地区共同生活的人们划分成“民族国家”，并不能带来公正、平等和稳定。

Prior to Sykes-Picot (in its wider sense), there was no state of Syria, no Lebanon, nor were there Jordan, Iraq, Saudi Arabia, Kuwait, Israel or Palestine. Modern maps show the borders and the names of nation states, but they are young and they are fragile.

[.my2]
**在《赛克斯-皮科协定》（广义上）颁布之前，叙利亚、黎巴嫩、约旦、伊拉克、沙特阿拉伯、科威特、以色列和巴勒斯坦这些国家都不存在。**现代地图上标示着各个国家的边界和名称，但这些国家都很年轻，也很脆弱。

[.my1]

With the partitioning of the Ottoman Empire, its Kurdish-majority territories were divided between the newly formed states of Turkey, Iraq, and Syria, making Kurds a significant ethnic minority in each state. Kurdish nationalist movements have long been suppressed by Turkey and in the states of Iran, Iraq, and Syria.[1]

早期库尔德民族主义起源于奥斯曼帝国 ，**库尔德人是奥斯曼帝国的一个重要民族。随着奥斯曼帝国的分裂 ，其库尔德人占多数的领土被新成立的土耳其、伊拉克和叙利亚瓜分，使得库尔德人在每个国家都成为重要的少数民族。长期以来，库尔德民族主义运动一直受到土耳其以及伊朗、伊拉克和叙利亚的镇压。**


Since the 1970s, Iraqi Kurds have pursued the goal of greater autonomy and even outright independence against the Iraqi nationalist Ba'ath Party regimes, which responded with brutal repression, including the massacre of 50,000-100,000 Kurds in the Anfal campaign. The Kurdish–Turkish conflict, where Kurdish armed groups have fought against the state, has been ongoing since 1984. After the 1991 uprisings in Iraq, the United Nations enforced the Iraqi no-fly zones under Security Council Resolution 688, which included much of Iraqi Kurdistan, facilitating autonomy and self-government outside the control of the Iraqi government. After the 2003 invasion of Iraq that ousted Saddam Hussein, the Kurdistan Regional Government was established, enjoying a great measure of self-governance but stopping short of full independence.

自 20 世纪 70 年代以来， **伊拉克库尔德人一直追求更大的自治权乃至完全独立，**反对伊拉克民族主义复兴党政权。复兴党政权以残酷镇压回应，包括在安法尔战役中屠杀了 5 万至 10 万库尔德人。

库尔德武装团体, 与土耳其政府之间的库尔德-土耳其冲突, 自 1984 年以来持续不断 。

1991 年伊拉克爆发起义后， 联合国根据安理会第 688 号决议, 设立了伊拉克禁飞区 ，其中包括伊拉克"库尔德斯坦"的大部分地区，从而促进了该地区在伊拉克政府控制之外的自治和自主管理。 2003 年伊拉克战争推翻萨达姆·侯赛因政权后， *"库尔德斯坦"地区政府成立，享有高度自治权，但尚未完全独立。*

The more serious potential threat comes from Lebanon’s bigger neighbour Syria. Historically, Damascus wants and needs direct access to the coast. It has always regarded Lebanon as part of Syria (as indeed it was) and remains bitter about its troops having been forced to leave in 2005. If that route to the sea is blocked, the alternative is to cross the Golan Heights and descend to the hilly region around the Sea of Galilee en route to the Mediterranean. But the Heights were seized by Israel after Syria attacked it in the 1973 war, and it would take an enormous onslaught by a Syrian army to break through to the coastal plain leading to the major Israeli population centres. This cannot be discounted at some future point, but in the medium term it remains extremely unlikely, and – as long as the Syrian civil war continues – impossible.

Iran is a non-Arabic, majority Farsi-speaking giant. It is bigger than France, Germany and the UK combined, but while the populations of those countries amount to 200 million people, Iran has only 78 million. With limited habitable space, most live in the mountains; the great deserts and salt plains of the interior of Iran are no place for human habitation. Just driving through them can subdue the human spirit, and living in them is a struggle few undertake.

[.my2]
**伊朗是一个非阿拉伯语、以波斯语为主的巨型国家。**它的面积比法国、德国和英国的总和还要大，但尽管这三个国家的人口加起来有2亿，伊朗却只有7800万。由于适宜居住的土地有限，大多数人居住在山区；伊朗内陆广袤的沙漠和盐碱地并不适合人类居住。仅仅是驱车穿过这些地区就足以令人心力交瘁，而生活在其中更是一种艰辛的挑战，很少有人愿意尝试。

There are two huge mountain ranges in Iran: the Zagros and the Elburz. The Zagros runs from the north, 900 miles down along Iran’s borders with Turkey and Iraq, ending almost at the Strait of Hormuz in the Gulf. In the southern half of the range there is a plain to the west where the Shatt al-Arab divides Iran and Iraq. This is also where the major Iranian oilfields are, the others being in the north and centre. Together they are thought to comprise the world’s third-largest reserves. Despite this Iran remains relatively poor due to mismanagement, corruption, mountainous topography that hinders transport connections and economic sanctions which have, in part, prevented certain sections of industry from modernising.

[.my2]
伊朗境内有两大山脉：扎格罗斯山脉, 和厄尔布尔士山脉。扎格罗斯山脉自北向南绵延 900 英里，沿着伊朗与土耳其和伊拉克的边界线延伸，几乎止于波斯湾的霍尔木兹海峡。山脉南段西侧是一片平原，阿拉伯河在此将伊朗和伊拉克分隔开来。伊朗的主要油田也位于此地，其他油田则分布在北部和中部。据估计，这些油田的总储量位居世界第三。 尽管如此，由于管理不善、腐败、地形崎岖阻碍交通以及经济制裁等原因，伊朗仍然相对贫穷，经济制裁在一定程度上阻碍了某些行业的现代化进程。

The Elburz range also begins in the north, but along the border with Armenia. It runs the whole length of the Caspian Sea’s south shore and on to the border with Turkmenistan before descending as it reaches Afghanistan. This is the mountain range you can see from the capital, Tehran, towering above the city to its north. It provides spectacular views, and also a better-kept secret than the Iranian nuclear project: the skiing conditions are excellent for several months each year.

By the time of the Second Gulf War in 2003 even the USA, the greatest fighting force the world has seen, thought better than to take a right turn once it had entered Iraq from the south, knowing that even with its superior firepower Iran was not a country to invade. In 1980, when the Iran–Iraq War broke out, the Iraqis used six divisions to cross the Shatt al-Arab in an attempt to annex the Iranian province of Khuzestan. They never even made it off the swamp-ridden plains, let alone entered the foothills of the Zagros. The war dragged on for eight years, taking at least a million lives.

[.my2]
1980年"两伊战争"爆发时，伊拉克军队动用六个师试图渡过阿拉伯河，吞并伊朗胡齐斯坦省。然而，他们甚至没能离开沼泽遍布的平原，更遑论进入扎格罗斯山脉的山麓。这场战争持续了八年，夺去了至少一百万人的生命。

The mountainous terrain of Iran means that it is difficult to create an interconnected economy, and that it has many minority groups each with keenly defined characteristics. Khuzestan, for example, is ethnically majority Arab, and elsewhere there are Kurds, Azeri, Turkmen and Georgians, among others. At most 60 per cent of the country speaks Farsi, the language of the dominant Persian majority. As a result of this diversity, Iran has traditionally centralised power and used force and a fearsome intelligence network to maintain internal stability. Tehran knows that no one is about to invade Iran, but also that hostile powers can use its minorities to try and stir dissent and thus endanger its Islamic revolution.

[.my2]
**伊朗多山的地形, 使得建立互联互通的经济体系十分困难，也造就了其众多各具鲜明特征的少数民族群体。**例如，胡齐斯坦省以阿拉伯人为主，其他地区则居住着库尔德人、阿塞拜疆人、土库曼人和格鲁吉亚人等。**全国最多只有 60%的人口讲波斯语，**这是占主导地位的波斯族群的语言。由于这种多样性，伊朗历来实行权力集中制，并依靠武力和强大的情报网络来维护国内稳定。德黑兰深知目前没有人会入侵伊朗，但也明白敌对势力可能会利用其少数民族煽动异议，从而危及伊朗的伊斯兰革命。

Iran also has a nuclear industry which many countries, particularly Israel, believe is being used to prepare for the construction of nuclear weapons, increasing tensions in the region. The Israelis feel threatened by the prospect of Iranian nuclear weapons. It is not just Iran’s potential to rival their own arsenal and wipe out Israel with just one bomb: if Iran were to get the bomb, then the Arab countries would probably panic and attempt to get theirs as well. The Saudis, for example, fear that the Ayatollahs want to dominate the region, bring all the Shia Arabs under their guidance, and even have designs on controlling the holy cities of Mecca and Medina. A nuclear-armed Iran would be the regional superpower par excellence, and to counter this danger the Saudis would probably try to buy nuclear weapons from Pakistan (with whom they have close ties). Egypt and Turkey might follow suit.

The Bosporus or Bosphorus Strait  is a natural strait and an internationally significant waterway located in Turkey which is straddled by the city of Istanbul. The Bosporus connects the Black Sea to the Sea of Marmara and forms one of the continental boundaries between Asia and Europe. It also divides Turkey by separating Asia Minor from Thrace. It is the world's narrowest strait used for international navigation.

博斯普鲁斯海峡 , 构成亚欧大陆的分界线之一。博斯普鲁斯海峡连接黑海和马尔马拉海 ，**它也将土耳其一分为二，分隔了小亚细亚和色雷斯地区 。**它是世界上最狭窄的国际航行海峡。

image:/img/Bosporus Strait 2.jpg[,100%]

image:/img/Bosporus Strait.png[,100%]

As a maritime waterway, the Bosporus connects the Black Sea to the Sea of Marmara and thence to the Aegean and Mediterranean seas via the Dardanelles. It also connects various seas along the Eastern Mediterranean, the Balkans, the Near East, and Western Eurasia. Thus, the Bosporus allows maritime connections from the Black Sea all the way to the Mediterranean Sea and the Atlantic Ocean via Gibraltar, and to the Indian Ocean through the Suez Canal, making it a crucial international waterway, in particular for the passage of goods coming from Russia.

作为一条海上航道，博斯普鲁斯海峡连接黑海和马尔马拉海，并通过达达尼尔海峡, 连接爱琴海和地中海。它还连接东地中海 、 巴尔干半岛 、 近东和西欧亚大陆沿岸的各个海域。因此，*博斯普鲁斯海峡使得黑海能够经由直布罗陀海峡, 通往地中海和大西洋，并通过苏伊士运河通往印度洋，使其成为一条至关重要的国际航道，尤其对于来自俄罗斯的货物运输而言更是如此。*

The Bosporus is the only way for Bulgaria, Georgia, Romania, Russia (south-western part) and Ukraine to reach the Mediterranean Sea and other seas. Thus sovereignty over the straits is an important issue for these countries, as well as Turkey, the state the Bosporus actually flows through.

##**博斯普鲁斯海峡, 是保加利亚 、 格鲁吉亚 、 罗马尼亚 、 俄罗斯 （西南部）和乌克兰, 通往地中海及其他海域的唯一通道。**##因此，海峡主权对于这些国家, 以及"博斯普鲁斯海峡"实际流经的土耳其而言, 都是一个重要问题。



At its peak between the 16th and 18th centuries, the Ottoman Empire was able to use the strategic importance of the Bosporus to wrest control of the entire Black Sea area, which they regarded as an "Ottoman lake", from which Russian warships were prohibited.

在 16 至 18 世纪的鼎盛时期，奥斯曼帝国利用博斯普鲁斯海峡的战略重要性，夺取了整个黑海地区的控制权，并将黑海视为“奥斯曼湖”，禁止俄罗斯战舰进入。

Following the First World War, the 1920 Treaty of Sèvres demilitarised the strait and made it an international territory under the control of the League of Nations. This was amended by the Treaty of Lausanne (1923), which restored the straits to Turkey but allowed all foreign warships and commercial shipping to traverse the straits freely. Turkey eventually rejected the terms of that treaty, and remilitarised the straits area. The reversion was formalised under the Montreux Convention Regarding the Regime of the Turkish Straits of 20 July 1936. That convention – which is still in force – treats the straits as an international shipping lane except that Turkey retains the right to restrict the naval traffic of non–Black Sea states.

第一次世界大战后，1920 年的《塞夫尔条约》 使海峡非军事化，并将其置于国际联盟的管辖之下。** 1923 年的《洛桑条约》 对此进行了修正，将##海峡归还土耳其，##但允许所有外国军舰和商船自由通行。**土耳其最终拒绝接受该条约的条款，并重新将海峡地区军事化。*1936 年 7 月 20 日签署的《 关于土耳其海峡制度的蒙特勒公约》 正式确立了这一归还行为。#该公约至今仍然有效，它将海峡视为国际航道，但土耳其保留限制非黑海沿岸国家海上交通的权利。#*

Turkey was neutral in the Second World War until February 1945, and the straits were closed to the warships of belligerent nations during this time, although some German auxiliary vessels were permitted to transit. In diplomatic conferences, Soviet representatives had expressed an interest in obtaining naval bases on the straits. This, together with Stalin's demands for the restitution of the Turkish provinces of Kars, Artvin and Ardahan to the Soviet Union (which were lost by Turkey in the Russo-Turkish War of 1877–1878, but were regained with the Treaty of Kars in 1921), was a consideration in Turkey's decision to abandon neutrality in foreign affairs. Turkey declared war against Germany in February 1945, but did not engage in offensive actions.

**土耳其在第二次世界大战中保持中立，直至 1945 年 2 月。在此期间，海峡对交战国的军舰关闭，但允许一些德国辅助舰艇通过。**在外交会议上， 苏联代表曾表示有意在海峡沿岸建立海军基地。此外，斯大林要求土耳其归还卡尔斯 、 阿尔特温和阿尔达汉三个省份（这些省份在 1877-1878 年的俄土战争中被土耳其割让，后于 1921 年通过 《卡尔斯条约》 重新获得），这些因素促使土耳其决定放弃外交中立 。土耳其于 1945 年 2 月对德宣战，但并未采取进攻行动。

Turkey joined NATO in 1952, thus affording the straits even more strategic importance as a commercial and military waterway.

*土耳其于 1952 年加入北约 ，这使得海峡作为商业和军事水道更具战略重要性。*

During the early 21st century, the Turkish Straits have become particularly important for the oil industry. Russian oil, from ports such as Novorossiysk, was exported by tankers primarily to the U.S. via the Bosporus and the Dardanelles straits.

21 世纪初， 土耳其海峡对石油工业变得尤为重要。俄罗斯石油从新罗西斯克等港口出口，主要通过博斯普鲁斯海峡, 和达达尼尔海峡, 由油轮运往美国。

The Bosporus is fairly deep and there is no definite limit on the depth and length of a ship, but ships over 150 metres long or 10 metres deep must pre-book their passage. Those over 300 metres long must follow a special clearance procedure. There is an air draft limit of 57 metres.


博斯普鲁斯海峡水深较深，对船舶的深度和长度没有明确的限制，但**长度超过 150 米, 或吃水超过 10 米的船舶必须提前预订通行。**长度超过 300 米的船舶必须遵守特殊的通行程序。*船舶的净空高度限制为 57 米。(因为海峡上有桥存在.)*


In 2011, Turkey proposed to build a 50 km (31 mi) canal west of the Bosporus, suggesting that it would reduce the risk presented to the Bosporus by oil tankers and other cargo ships.[36][37] The project proved highly controversial and, as of 2022, work had not been started on building the canal even though a route for it had been established.[38]

2011 年，*土耳其提议在博斯普鲁斯海峡西侧, 修建一条 50 公里（31 英里）长的运河 ( Kanal İstanbul 伊斯坦布尔运河 ) ，认为这将降低油轮和其他货船对"博斯普鲁斯海峡"构成的风险。* [ 36 ] [ 37 ] 该项目引发了极大的争议，截至 2022 年，尽管运河路线已经确定，但尚未开工建设。

The Istanbul Canal (Turkish: Kanal İstanbul pronounced [kɑnɑɫ isˈtɑnbuɫ]) is a project for an artificial sea-level waterway planned by Turkey in East Thrace, connecting the Black Sea to the Sea of Marmara, and thus to the Aegean and Mediterranean seas.
