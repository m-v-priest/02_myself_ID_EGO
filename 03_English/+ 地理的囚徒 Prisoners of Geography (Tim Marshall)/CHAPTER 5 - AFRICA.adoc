=== 非洲地理常识

==== "苏伊士运河"开通的重要性

The world’s idea of African geography is flawed. Few people realise just how big it is. This is because most of us use the standard Mercator world map. This, as do other maps, depicts a sphere on a flat surface and thus distorts shapes.
We know Africa is a massive land mass, but the maps rarely tell us how massive. You could fit the USA, Greenland, India, China, Spain, France, Germany and the UK into Africa and still have room for most of Eastern Europe.

[.my2]
世界对非洲地理的认知存在缺陷。很少有人真正意识到非洲究竟有多大。这是因为我们大多数人使用的是标准的"墨卡托投影"世界地图。这种地图和其他地图一样，将球体投影到平面上，因此会扭曲形状。
你可以把美国、格陵兰岛、印度、中国、西班牙、法国、德国和英国都塞进非洲，甚至还能容纳东欧的大部分地区。我们知道非洲是一块巨大的陆地，但地图很少会告诉我们它到底有多大。你可以把美国、格陵兰岛、印度、中国、西班牙、法国、德国和英国都塞进非洲，甚至还能容纳东欧的大部分地区。

Africa is far, far longer than usually portrayed, which explains what an achievement it was to round the Cape of Good Hope, and is a reminder of the importance of the Suez Canal to world trade. Making it around the Cape was a momentous achievement, but once it became unnecessary to do so, the sea journey from Western Europe to India was reduced by 6,000 miles.

[.my2]
**非洲的实际长度远比通常描绘的要长得多，这也解释了绕过"好望角"是多么了不起的成就 ，并提醒我们"苏伊士运河"对世界贸易的重要性。**绕过"好望角"是一项意义非凡的成就，但**一旦不再需要绕道，从西欧到印度的海上航程就缩短了 6000 英里。**

In the days of the British Empire, controlling South Africa meant controlling the Cape of Good Hope and thus the sea lanes between the Atlantic and Indian oceans.

[.my2]
在英国殖民帝国时期，控制南非, 就意味着控制了"好望角"，进而能控制大西洋和印度洋之间的海上航线。

==== 南北地理差异

The geography of this immense continent can be explained in several ways, but the most basic is to think of Africa in terms of the top third and bottom two-thirds.

[.my2]
可以用多种方式来解释这片广袤大陆的地理特征，但**最基本的解释, 是将非洲分为北部的三分之一, 和南部的三分之二。**

===== -> 北部

The top third begins on the Mediterranean coastlines of the North African Arabic-speaking countries.

- The coastal plains quickly become the Sahara, the world’s largest dry desert, which is almost as big as the USA.
- Directly below the Sahara is the Sahel region, a semi-arid, rock-strewn, sandy strip of land measuring more than 3,000 miles at its widest points and stretching from Gambia on the Atlantic coast through Niger, Chad and right across to Eritrea on the Red Sea.  +
The word Sahel comes from the Arabic sahil, which means coast, and is how the people living in the region think of it – as the shoreline of the vast sand sea of the Sahara.  +
It is another sort of shore, one where the influence of Islam diminishes. From the Sahel to the Mediterranean the vast majority of people are Muslims. South of it there is far more diversity in religion.

[.my2]
====
北部三分之一的区域, 始于北非阿拉伯语国家的地中海沿岸。

- 沿海平原迅速过渡到撒哈拉沙漠，这是世界上最大的干旱沙漠，面积几乎与美国相当。 +
- **撒哈拉沙漠正下方是"萨赫勒"地区，**这是一片半干旱、岩石遍布的沙质地带，最宽处超过 3000 英里. 从大西洋沿岸的"冈比亚"延伸，途经尼日尔、乍得，一直到红海沿岸的厄立特里亚。 +
萨赫勒一词源于阿拉伯语“ sahil” ，意为海岸，生活在该地区的人们, 正是这样理解这片土地——撒哈拉沙漠这片广袤沙海的海岸线。  +
这里是另一种意义上的海岸，*伊斯兰教的影响力在这里逐渐减弱。从"萨赫勒"到"地中海沿岸"，绝大多数居民信奉伊斯兰教。而在萨赫勒以南，宗教信仰则更加多元化。*
====


===== -> 南部


Indeed, south of the Sahel, in the bottom two-thirds of Africa, there is more diversity in most things.

- The land becomes more temperate and green vegetation appears, which becomes jungle as we approach Congo and the Central African Republic.
- Towards the east coast are the great lakes in Uganda and Tanzania, while across to the west more deserts appear in Angola and Namibia.
- By the time we reach the tip of South Africa the climate is again ‘Mediterranean’, even though we have travelled almost 5,000 miles from the northernmost point in Tunisia on the Mediterranean coast.

[.my2]
====
的确，在"萨赫勒地区"以南，也就是非洲大陆南部三分之二的地区，大多数物种都更加多样化。气候变得更加温和，植被也开始出现.  +

- 随着我们接近"刚果"和"中非共和国"，植被逐渐过渡到丛林。 +
- 东海岸是乌干达和坦桑尼亚的大湖区，而西边则是安哥拉和纳米比亚的沙漠。 +
- 当我们到达"南非"最南端时，气候又回到了“地中海”气候，尽管我们已经从地中海沿岸最北端的"突尼斯"出发，跋涉了近 5000 英里。
====

=== 问题 /地理对经济的约束


==== 对外交流 ->  与欧亚隔绝, 交流太闭塞, 科技发展缓慢

Africa, being a huge continent, has always consisted of different regions, climates and cultures, but what they all had in common was their isolation from each other and the outside world.  +
Almost the entire continent developed in isolation from the Eurasian land mass, where ideas and technology were exchanged from east to west, and west to east, but not north to south.

[.my2]
非洲幅员辽阔，历来由不同的地区、气候和文化组成，但它们共同的特点是彼此隔绝，也与外界隔绝。 +
*几乎整个非洲大陆的发展, 都与欧亚大陆隔绝. 而欧亚大陆的思想和技术交流, 是东西向的，南北向的交流却很少。*

Even had technologically productive nation states arisen, much of the continent would still have struggled to connect to the rest of the world because the bulk of the land mass is framed by the Indian and Atlantic oceans and the Sahara Desert. The exchange of ideas and technology barely touched sub-Saharan Africa for thousands of years.

[.my2]
即使出现了技术发达的民族国家，**非洲大陆的大部分地区, **仍然**难以与世界其他地区连接，因为其大部分陆地, 被印度洋、大西洋和撒哈拉沙漠所环绕。数千年来，思想和技术的交流, 几乎未曾触及撒哈拉以南非洲。**


Despite this, several African empires and city states did arise after about the sixth century CE: for example the Mali Empire (thirteenth–sixteenth century), and the city state of Great Zimbabwe (eleventh–fifteenth century), the latter in land between the Zambezi and Limpopo rivers. However, these and others were isolated to relatively small regional blocs, and although the myriad cultures which did emerge across the continent may have been politically sophisticated, the physical landscape remained a barrier to technological development: by the time the outside world arrived in force, most had yet to develop writing, paper, gunpowder or the wheel.


[.my2]
尽管如此， 公元 6 世纪之后，非洲确实出现了一些帝国和城邦，例如马里帝国（13 至 16 世纪）, 和位于赞比西河和林波波河之间的大津巴布韦城邦（11 至 15 世纪）。*然而，这些帝国和城邦, 大多局限于相对较小的区域集团内。* +
尽管非洲大陆上涌现出的众多文化, 可能在政治上较为成熟，但自然环境仍然是技术发展的障碍：*当外部世界的文明大规模涌入非洲时，该州大多数地区尚未发明文字、纸张、火药或车轮。*

==== 农业->  南方的地理, 不适合种植农业

But back south there were few plants willing to be domesticated, and even fewer animals. Much of the land consists of jungle, swamp, desert or steep-sided plateau, none of which lend themselves to the growing of wheat or rice, or sustaining herds of sheep. Africa’s rhinos, gazelles and giraffes stubbornly refused to be beasts of burden.

[.my2]
但**往南走，可供驯化的植物寥寥无几，动物更是屈指可数。那里大部分土地都是丛林、沼泽、沙漠或陡峭的高原，这些地方都不适合种植小麦或水稻，也不适合放牧绵羊。**非洲的犀牛、瞪羚和长颈鹿固执地拒绝成为驮畜.

==== 航道 ->  缺乏良港, 其河流不适合运输

AFRICA’S COASTLINE? GREAT BEACHES, REALLY, REALLY LOVELY beaches, but terrible natural harbours. Rivers? Amazing rivers, but most of them are rubbish for actually transporting anything, given that every few miles you go over a waterfall. These are just two in a long list of problems which help explain why Africa isn’t technologically or politically as successful as Western Europe or North America.

[.my2]
**非洲的海岸线？**拥有绝美的海滩，真的非常非常漂亮 ，但**天然港口却很糟糕。**河流？河流壮丽，但**大多数河流都不适合运输任何东西，因为每隔几英里就会遇到一个瀑布。**这只是众多问题中的两个，这些问题也解释了为什么非洲在科技和政治上不如西欧或北美成功。

When the Europeans finally made it down the west coast in the fifteenth century they found few natural harbours for their ships. Unlike Europe or North America, where the jagged coastlines give rise to deep natural harbours, much of the African coastline is smooth. And once they did make land they struggled to penetrate any further inland than about 100 miles due to the difficulty of navigating the rivers, as well as the challenges of the climate and disease.

[.my2]
*十五世纪，当欧洲人最终抵达西海岸时，他们发现几乎没有天然良港。与欧洲或北美崎岖的海岸线造就"深水天然港湾"不同，非洲的大部分海岸线都较为平缓。即使登陆，由于河流航行困难，加上气候和疾病的挑战，他们也很难深入内陆超过约100英里。*

Most of the continent’s rivers also pose a problem, as they begin in high land and descend in abrupt drops which thwart navigation. For example, the mighty Zambezi may be Africa’s fourth-longest river, running for 1,600 miles, and may be a stunning tourist attraction with its white-water rapids and the Victoria Falls, but as a trade route it is of little use. It flows through six countries, dropping from 4,900 feet to sea level when it reaches the Indian Ocean in Mozambique. Parts of it are navigable by shallow boats, but these parts do not interconnect, thus limiting the transportation of cargo.

[.my2]
**非洲大陆的大多数河流也存在问题，因为它们发源于高地，然后陡然下降，阻碍了航行。**例如，奔腾的**赞比西河虽然是非洲第四长河，** 全长 1600 英里，以其湍急的白水和"维多利亚瀑布"而闻名，是令人叹为观止的旅游胜地，**但作为贸易通道却作用甚微。**"赞比西河"流经六个国家，在莫桑比克注入印度洋时，水位从 4900 英尺骤降至海平面。*虽然部分河段浅水船可以通航，但这些河段彼此不相连，因此限制了货物的运输。*

Unlike in Europe, which has the Danube and the Rhine, this drawback has hindered contact and trade between regions – which in turn affected economic development, and hindered the formation of large trading regions.

[.my2]
与拥有多瑙河和莱茵河的欧洲不同，非洲大陆的**这一缺陷, 阻碍了各地区之间的交流和贸易，进而影响了经济发展，并阻碍了大型贸易区域的形成。**

==== 国家形成 ->  没有通用的语言, 缺乏共同文化

The continent’s great rivers, the Niger, the Congo, the Zambezi, the Nile and others, don’t connect and this disconnection has a human factor. Whereas huge areas of Russia, China and the USA speak a unifying language which helps trade, in Africa thousands of languages exist and no one culture emerged to dominate areas of similar size. Europe, on the other hand, was small enough to have a ‘lingua franca’ through which to communicate, and a landscape that encouraged interaction.

[.my2]
**非洲大陆的主要河流，如尼日尔河、刚果河、赞比西河、尼罗河等，彼此并不相连. 而这种不连通性, 也是与"人为因素"密切相关的。**俄罗斯、中国和美国的大部分地区都使用一种通用语言，这有助于贸易往来；而**在非洲，却存在着数千种语言，也没有一种文化能够主导面积相近的区域。而欧洲, 面积较小，因此拥有一种通用语，便于交流，而且其地理环境也有利于互动。**

==== 疾病 ->  因气候炎热, 而有很多致命疾病

But Africa’s head start in our mutual story did allow it more time to develop something else which to this day holds it back: a virulent set of diseases, such as malaria and yellow fever, brought on by the heat and now complicated by crowded living conditions and poor healthcare infrastructure. This is true of other regions – the subcontinent and South America, for example – but sub-Saharan Africa has been especially hard hit, for example by the HIV virus, and has a particular problem because of the prevalence of the mosquito and the Tsetse fly.

[.my2]
然而，非洲在我们共同的历史进程中起步较早，也让它有更多的时间发展出另**一种至今仍阻碍其发展的东西：一系列致命的疾病，例如疟疾和黄热病，这些疾病是由炎热气候引起的，**如今又因拥挤的居住环境, 和落后的医疗基础设施, 而变得更加复杂。其他地区的情况也是如此——例如次大陆和南美洲——但撒哈拉以南非洲受到的打击尤为严重，例如**艾滋病毒，而且由于蚊子和采采蝇的盛行，该地区面临着特殊的问题。**

=== 问题 / 人为造成的 or 国家间竞争


==== 种族冲突 <- 由"非自然形成的国境线"带来

Back in the great capital cities of London, Paris, Brussels and Lisbon, the Europeans then took maps of the contours of Africa’s geography and drew lines on them – or, to take a more aggressive approach, lies. In between these lines they wrote words such as Middle Congo or Upper Volta and called them countries. These lines were more about how far which power’s explorers, military forces and businessmen had advanced on the map than what the people living between the lines felt themselves to be, or how they wanted to organise themselves.

[.my2]
回到伦敦、巴黎、布鲁塞尔, 和里斯本这些伟大的首都城市， *欧洲人拿着非洲地理轮廓的地图，在上面画线*——或者更确切地说，是谎言。**他们在这些线之间写下诸如“中刚果”或“上沃尔特”之类的字眼，并称之为国家。这些线与其说是反映了生活在这些线之间的人们的自我认同, 或他们想要如何组织起来，不如说是反映了哪个强权的探险家、军队和商人, 在地图上推进了多远。**

There are now fifty-six countries in Africa.  However, many encompass the same divisions they did when first drawn, and those formal divisions are some of the many legacies colonialism bequeathed the continent.

[.my2]
非洲现在共有56个国家。然而，许多边界仍然沿用最初划定时的划分，而这些正式的划分, 正是殖民主义留给非洲大陆的众多遗产之一。

The ethnic conflicts within Sudan, Somalia, Kenya, Angola, the Democratic Republic of the Congo, Nigeria, Mali and elsewhere are evidence that the European idea of geography did not fit the reality of Africa’s demographics. There may have always been conflict: the Zulus and Xhosas had their differences long before they had ever set eyes on a European. But colonialism forced those differences to be resolved within an artificial structure – the European concept of a nation state. The modern civil wars are now partially because the colonialists told different nations that they were one nation in one state, and then after the colonialists were chased out a dominant people emerged within the state who wanted to rule it all, thus ensuring violence.

[.my2]
苏丹、索马里、肯尼亚、安哥拉、刚果民主共和国、尼日利亚、马里等地的种族冲突表明，欧洲的地理观念并不符合非洲的人口现实。冲突或许一直存在：祖鲁人和科萨人早在见到欧洲人之前就存在分歧。但**殖民主义强迫这些分歧, 在人为构建的框架内得到解决——即欧洲的"民族国家"概念。现代内战的部分原因在于，殖民者告诉不同的民族，他们同属一个国家，而殖民者被驱逐后，国家内部出现了一个想要统治一切的强势群体，从而导致了暴力冲突。**


==== 案例/ 刚果民主共和国 <- 任意划定边界的悲剧

The DRC is an illustration of why the catch-all term ‘developing world’ is far too broad-brush a way to describe countries which are not part of the modern industrialised world. The DRC is not developing, nor does it show any signs of so doing. The DRC should never have been put together; it has fallen apart and is the most under-reported war zone in the world, despite the fact that six million people have died there during wars which have been fought since the late 1990s.

[.my2]
刚果民主共和国的例子, 充分说明了为什么用“发展中国家”这个包罗万象的术语, 来描述那些不属于现代工业化世界的国家, 过于笼统。刚果民主共和国既不是发展中国家，也没有任何发展迹象。刚果民主共和国本就不应该存在 ；它已经分崩离析. 并且是世界上报道最少的战区，尽管自 20 世纪 90 年代末以来，已有 600 万人死于战火。

The DRC is neither democratic, nor a republic. It is the second-largest country in Africa with a population of about 75 million, although due to the situation there it is difficult to find accurate figures.

[.my2]
刚果民主共和国既非"民主国家"，也非"共和国"。它是非洲第二大国家，人口约7500万，但由于当地局势，很难获得准确的人口统计数据。

The people are divided into more than 200 ethnic groups, of which the biggest are the Bantu. There are several hundred languages, but the widespread use of French bridges that gap to a degree.  +
The French comes from the DRC’s years as a Belgian colony (1908–60) and before that when King Leopold of the Belgians used it as his personal property from which to steal its natural resources to line his pockets. Belgian colonial rule made the British and French versions look positively benign and was ruthlessly brutal from start to finish, with few attempts to build any sort of infrastructure to help the inhabitants. When the Belgians went in 1960 they left behind little chance of the country holding together.

[.my2]
刚果民主共和国人民分为200多个民族，其中最大的民族是班图族。该国语言数百种，但法语的广泛使用在一定程度上弥合了语言之间的鸿沟。 +
法语的传播, 源于刚果民主共和国作为比利时殖民地时期（1908-1960年），以及更早之前, 比利时国王利奥波德二世将刚果民主共和国视为其私人领地，肆意掠夺自然资源以中饱私囊。*比利时的殖民统治, 令英国和法国的统治相形见绌，从始至终都残酷无情，几乎没有为当地居民建设任何基础设施。1960年比利时人撤离时，这个国家几乎没有任何统一的希望。*

Because it is so resource-rich and so large, everyone wants a bite out of the DRC, which, as it lacks a substantive central authority, cannot really bite back. +
The region is also bordered by nine countries. They have all played a role in the DRC’s agony, which is one reason why the Congo wars are also known as ‘Africa’s world war’. To the south is Angola, to the north the Republic of the Congo and the Central Africa Republic, to the east Uganda, Rwanda, Burundi, Tanzania and Zambia.  +
The roots of the wars go back decades, but the worst of times was triggered by the disaster that hit Rwanda in 1994 and swept westward in its aftermath.

[.my2]
由于刚果民主共和国资源丰富, 且幅员辽阔，每个人都想从中分一杯羹，但由于缺乏实质性的中央权威，刚果民主共和国实际上无法反击。 +
该地区与九个国家接壤。这些国家都曾参与造成刚果民主共和国的苦难，这也是刚果战争被称为“非洲世界大战”的原因之一。南部是安哥拉，北部是刚果共和国和中非共和国，东部是乌干达、卢旺达、布隆迪、坦桑尼亚和赞比亚。 +
战争的根源可以追溯到几十年前，但最严重的时期是由1994年袭击卢旺达的灾难引发的，这场灾难随后向西蔓延。

The wars have killed, at a low estimate, tens of thousands of people, and have resulted in the deaths of another six million due to disease and malnutrition. The UN estimates that almost 50 per cent of the victims have been children aged under five.

[.my2]
战争已造成数万人死亡（保守估计），另有六百万人死于疾病和营养不良。*联合国估计，近一半的受害者是五岁以下的儿童。*


In 2014 the United Nations Human Development Index placed the DRC 186th out of 187 countries it measured. The bottom eighteen countries in that list are all in Africa.

[.my2]
**2014 年，联合国"人类发展指数"将刚果民主共和国, 在 187 个国家中排名第 186 位。**该榜单排名垫底的 18 个国家, 全部位于非洲。

In recent years the fighting has died down, but the DRC is home to the world’s most deadly conflict since the Second World War and still requires the UN’s largest peacekeeping mission to prevent full-scale war from breaking out again. Now the job is not to put Humpty Dumpty together again, because the DRC was never whole. It is simply to keep the pieces apart until a way can be found to join them sensibly and peacefully. The European colonialist created an egg without a chicken, a logical absurdity repeated across the continent and one that continues to haunt it.

[.my2]

.an egg without a chicken 没有母鸡的蛋

“蛋” (The Egg) = 国家的空壳. +
这里的“蛋”指的是外部形式上的国家结构。1884年柏林会议上，欧洲殖民者拿着尺子在地图上画出的国界线。这块地区被冠以“刚果”这个统一的名字。

“母鸡” (The Chicken) = 有机的民族认同 +
这里的“母鸡”指的是产生这个国家、并能维持其生命的内在力量。 +

自然演进： 在欧洲，国家通常是先有共同的语言、文化、历史（母鸡），经过长期融合，最后才形成了政治上的国家边界（蛋）。 +
民族认同： 国境线内的人民觉得自己属于同一个民族，拥有共同的利益和效忠对象。

在正常的自然逻辑中，是先有鸡才有蛋（或者说鸡生了蛋）。但在刚果（DRC），欧洲殖民者把逻辑颠倒了：

- 强行拼凑： 他们制造了一个巨大的“蛋壳”（一个比西欧还要大的领土），但壳里面塞进去了 200多个完全不同的民族，他们没有共同语言，甚至历史上彼此敌对。

- 缺乏核心： 这个国家没有一个自然的、能够凝聚所有人的内核。因为它不是由内部生长出来的，而是由外部强加的。

[.my3]
[options="autowidth" cols="1a,1a,1a"]
|===
| 维度 | 正常的“有鸡之蛋”模式 | 刚果的“无鸡之蛋”模式
| **形成过程** | 文化/民族认同  政治边界。 | 殖民者划线  强行塞入多元民族。
| **凝聚力** | 国民有共同的归属感，“我首先是XX国人”。 | 人民忠于自己的部族，“我首先是XX部族人”。
| **中央控制** | 政府能有效管理全国每一寸土地。 | 只有首都和矿区有控制力，边远地区军阀割据。
| **崩溃后果** | 即使政府倒台，民族认同依然能维系国家。 | 一旦强力统治消失，蛋壳就会碎成无数片（Humpty Dumpty）。
|===

文中提到这“持续困扰着非洲”，是因为这种“无鸡之蛋”导致了恶性循环：

- 无法治理： *因为没有内在的凝聚力，统治者往往只能通过极端的暴力, 或腐败贿赂, 来维持表面上的统一。* +
既然大家对这个“蛋”（国家）没有认同感，而刚果拥有极其丰富的矿产，这反而成了各派系和邻国疯狂争夺、互相屠杀的理由。

- 地缘碎片化： 正如文中所言，*现在的任务甚至不是“把碎掉的蛋拼回去”，而是“先让碎片各待各的”，别再互相撞击引发战火。*
