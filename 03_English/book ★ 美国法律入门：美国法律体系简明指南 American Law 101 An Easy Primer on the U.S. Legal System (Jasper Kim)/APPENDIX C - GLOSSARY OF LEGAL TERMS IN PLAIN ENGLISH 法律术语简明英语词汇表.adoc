== 释义



APPENDIX C +
GLOSSARY OF LEGAL TERMS IN PLAIN ENGLISH

=== A

Acquittal:: A jury verdict 裁决 that a criminal defendant 刑事被告 is not guilty, or the finding of a judge that the evidence 证据 is insufficient 不足的 to support a conviction 定罪.

[.my2]
无罪裁决：指陪审团作出的刑事被告无罪的裁决，或是法官作出的证据不足以支持定罪的认定。

Active judge:: A judge in the full-time service 全职任职 of the court. Compare to senior judge 资深法官.

[.my2]
在职法官：指在法院全职任职的法官。与资深法官相对。

Administrative Office of the United States Courts (AO):: The federal agency 联邦机构 responsible for collecting court statistics 法院统计数据, administering 管理 the federal courts’ budget 预算, and performing many other administrative 行政的 and programmatic 计划性的 functions, under the direction and supervision 指导与监督 of the Judicial Conference of the United States 美国司法会议.

[.my2]
美国法院行政管理局（AO）：在美国司法会议的指导与监督下，负责收集法院统计数据、管理联邦法院预算，并履行诸多其他行政及计划性职能的联邦机构。

Admissible:: A term used to describe evidence that may be considered 被采信 by a jury or judge in civil and criminal cases.

[.my2]
可采信的：用于描述民事和刑事案件中可被陪审团或法官采信的证据的术语。

Adversary proceeding:: A lawsuit 诉讼 arising in or related to a bankruptcy case 破产案件 that begins by filing a complaint 提交起诉状 with the court; that is, a “trial” that takes place within the context of a bankruptcy case.

[.my2]
对抗式程序：指因破产案件引发或与破产案件相关的诉讼，需通过向法院提交起诉状启动；也就是在破产案件框架内进行的“审判”。

Affidavit:: A written or printed statement 陈述 made under oath 经宣誓作出的.

[.my2]
宣誓书：经宣誓后作出的书面或打印陈述。

Affirmed:: In the practice of the court of appeals 上诉法院实务中, it means that the court of appeals has concluded that the lower court 下级法院 decision is correct and will stand 维持有效 as rendered by the lower court.

[.my2]
维持原判：在上诉法院实务中，该术语指上诉法院认定下级法院的判决无误，将维持下级法院作出的判决效力。

Alternate juror:: A juror 陪审员 selected in the same manner as a regular juror who hears all the evidence but does not help decide the case unless called on to replace 接替 a regular juror.

[.my2]
候补陪审员：指以与正式陪审员相同的方式选出的陪审员，候补陪审员需听取全部证据，但除非被要求接替正式陪审员，否则不参与案件裁决。

Alternative dispute resolution (ADR):: A procedure 程序 for settling a dispute 解决纠纷 outside the courtroom 法庭外. Most forms of ADR are not binding 不具有约束力的, and involve referral of the case to a neutral party 中立第三方 such as an arbitrator 仲裁员 or mediator 调解员.

[.my2]
替代性纠纷解决方式（ADR）：指在法庭外解决纠纷的程序。大多数替代性纠纷解决方式不具有约束力，通常是将案件交由仲裁员或调解员等中立第三方处理。

Amicus curiae:: Latin for “friend of the court 法庭之友.” It is advice 意见 formally offered to the court in a brief 辩护状 filed by an entity 机构 interested in, but not a party to 非案件当事方, the case.

[.my2]
法庭之友：拉丁语，意为“法庭之友”。指由与案件有利害关系但并非当事方的机构，通过提交辩护状的方式向法院正式提出的意见。

Answer:: The formal written statement 书面陈述 by a defendant in a civil case that responds to a complaint, articulating 阐明 the grounds for defense 辩护理由.

[.my2]
答辩状：指民事诉讼中被告提交的正式书面陈述，用于回应起诉状并阐明辩护理由。

Appeal:: A request 申请 made after a trial by a party that has lost on one or more issues 就一个或多个争议点败诉 that a higher court 上级法院 review 复审 the decision to determine if it was correct. To make such a request is “to appeal” or “to take an appeal.” One who appeals is called the “appellant 上诉人”; the other party is the “appellee 被上诉人.”

[.my2]
上诉：指庭审结束后，就一个或多个争议点败诉的一方请求上级法院复审判决，以判定其是否合法正确的申请。提出该申请的行为称为“上诉”。提起上诉的一方称为“上诉人”，另一方则为“被上诉人”。

Appellant:: The party who appeals a district court’s 地区法院 decision, usually seeking reversal 撤销 of that decision.

[.my2]
上诉人：指对地区法院的判决提起上诉，通常意在请求撤销该判决的一方当事人。

Appellate:: About appeals 与上诉相关的; an appellate court 上诉法院 has the power to review the judgment of a lower court (trial court 初审法院) or tribunal 裁判机构. For example, the U.S. circuit courts of appeals 美国联邦巡回上诉法院 review the decisions of the U.S. district courts 美国联邦地区法院.

[.my2]
上诉相关的：与上诉有关的；上诉法院有权复审下级法院（初审法院）或裁判机构作出的判决。例如，美国联邦巡回上诉法院负责复审美国联邦地区法院的判决。

Appellee:: The party who opposes 反对 an appellant’s appeal, and who seeks to persuade 说服 the appeals court to affirm 维持 the district court’s decision.

[.my2]
被上诉人：指反对上诉人的上诉请求，并试图说服上诉法院维持地区法院判决的一方当事人。

Arraignment:: A proceeding 诉讼程序 in which a criminal defendant is brought into court, told of the charges 指控 in an indictment 起诉书 or information 公诉书, and asked to plead guilty 认罪 or not guilty 不认罪.

[.my2]
传讯：指将刑事被告带至法庭，向其宣读起诉书或公诉书中的指控，并要求其作出认罪或不认罪答辩的诉讼程序。

Article III judge:: A federal judge 联邦法官 who is appointed for life 终身任职, during “good behavior 品行良好期间,” under Article III of the Constitution 宪法第三条. Article III judges are nominated 提名 by the President and confirmed 批准 by the Senate 参议院.

[.my2]
宪法第三条法官：指依据宪法第三条规定，在“品行良好期间”享有终身任职资格的联邦法官。此类法官由总统提名，并经参议院批准任命。

Assets:: Property 财产 of all kinds, including real 不动产 and personal 动产, tangible 有形的 and intangible 无形的.

[.my2]
资产：指各类财产，包括不动产与动产、有形财产与无形财产。

Assume:: An agreement 协议 to continue performing duties 履行义务 under a contract 合同 or lease 租约.

[.my2]
承继：指协议继续履行某份合同或租约项下义务的行为。

Automatic stay:: An injunction 禁令 that automatically stops 自动中止 lawsuits, foreclosures 止赎程序, garnishments 工资扣押, and most collection activities 催收行为 against the debtor 债务人 the moment a bankruptcy petition 破产申请 is filed.

[.my2]
自动中止：指破产申请提交的瞬间，自动中止针对债务人的诉讼、止赎程序、工资扣押以及大多数催收行为的禁令。

=== B

Bail:: The release, prior to trial 庭审前释放, of a person accused of a crime 刑事被指控人, under specified conditions 特定条件 designed to assure 确保 that person’s appearance in court when required 按要求出庭. Also can refer to the amount of bond money 保释金数额 posted as a financial condition 经济条件 of pretrial release.

[.my2]
保释：指在庭审前，将刑事被指控人在特定条件下释放，这些条件旨在确保其能按要求出庭。该术语也可指作为审前释放经济条件而缴纳的保释金数额。

Bankruptcy:: A legal procedure 法律程序 for dealing with debt problems 债务问题 of individuals and businesses; specifically, a case filed under one of the chapters of Title 11 of the United States Code 美国法典第11编 (the Bankruptcy Code 破产法典).

[.my2]
破产：指处理个人和企业债务问题的法律程序；具体而言，是指依据《美国法典》第11编（《破产法典》）某一章节提起的案件。

Bankruptcy administrator:: An officer of the Judiciary 司法机构官员 serving in the judicial districts 司法辖区 of Alabama and North Carolina who, like the United States trustee 美国联邦托管人, is responsible for supervising 监督 the administration of bankruptcy cases, estates 破产财产, and trustees 破产受托人; monitoring 监督 plans 重整计划 and disclosure statements 披露声明; monitoring creditors’ committees 债权人委员会; monitoring fee applications 费用申请; and performing other statutory duties 法定义务.

[.my2]
破产管理人：指在亚拉巴马州和北卡罗来纳州司法辖区履职的司法机构官员，其职责与美国联邦托管人类似，负责监督破产案件、破产财产及破产受托人的管理工作，监督重整计划与披露声明、债权人委员会、费用申请，并履行其他法定义务。

Bankruptcy code:: The informal name 非正式名称 for Title 11 of the United States Code (11 U.S.C. §§ 101-1330), the federal bankruptcy law 联邦破产法.

[.my2]
《破产法典》：《美国法典》第11编（《美国法典》第11篇第101至1330节）的非正式名称，即联邦破产法。

Bankruptcy court:: The bankruptcy judges in regular active service 全职在职 in each district; a unit of the district court 地区法院的一个分支.

[.my2]
破产法院：指各司法辖区内全职在职的破产法官群体；是地区法院的一个分支。

Bankruptcy estate:: All interests 权益 of the debtor in property at the time of the bankruptcy filing. The estate technically 严格来说 becomes the temporary legal owner 临时合法所有人 of all of the debtor’s property.

[.my2]
破产财产：指债务人提交破产申请时所拥有的全部财产权益。严格来说，破产财产会成为债务人全部财产的临时合法所有人。

Bankruptcy judge:: A judicial officer 司法官员 of the United States district court who is the court official with decision-making power 决策权 over federal bankruptcy cases.

[.my2]
破产法官：指美国联邦地区法院的司法官员，是拥有联邦破产案件决策权的法院公职人员。

Bankruptcy petition:: A formal request 正式申请 for the protection of the federal bankruptcy laws. (There is an official form 官方表格 for bankruptcy petitions.)

[.my2]
破产申请书：指请求获得联邦破产法保护的正式申请。（破产申请书有专门的官方表格。）

Bankruptcy trustee:: A private individual or corporation 私人或公司 appointed in all Chapter 7 and Chapter 13 cases to represent the interests of the bankruptcy estate and the debtor’s creditors 债权人.

[.my2]
破产受托人：指在所有第7章和第13章破产案件中被指定的私人或公司，代表破产财产和债务人债权人的利益。

Bench trial:: A trial without a jury 无陪审团审判, in which the judge serves as the fact-finder 事实认定者.

[.my2]
法官独任审判：指不设陪审团的审判，由法官作为事实认定者。

Brief:: A written statement 书面陈述 submitted in a trial or appellate proceeding that explains one side’s legal and factual arguments 法律与事实论据.

[.my2]
辩护状：指在庭审或上诉程序中提交的书面陈述，用于阐明一方的法律与事实论据。

Burden of proof:: The duty 责任 to prove disputed facts 争议事实. In civil cases, a plaintiff generally has the burden of proving his or her case. In criminal cases, the government has the burden of proving the defendant’s guilt. (See standard of proof 证明标准.)

[.my2]
举证责任：指证明争议事实的责任。在民事诉讼中，通常由原告承担举证责任；在刑事诉讼中，举证责任则由政府承担，需证明被告人有罪。（参见证明标准。）

Business bankruptcy:: A bankruptcy case in which the debtor is a business or an individual involved in business 从商个人 and the debts are for business purposes 因商业目的产生的.

[.my2]
商业破产：指债务人为企业或从商个人，且债务因商业目的产生的破产案件。

=== C

Capital offense:: A crime punishable by death 可判处死刑的.

[.my2]
死刑罪名：指可判处死刑的犯罪。

Case file:: A complete collection 完整卷宗 of every document filed in court in a case.

[.my2]
案件卷宗：指某一案件中向法院提交的全部文件的完整汇编。

Case law:: The law as established in previous court decisions 先前法院判决所确立的法律. A synonym for legal precedent 法律先例的同义词. Akin to 类似于 common law 普通法, which springs from 源于 tradition 传统 and judicial decisions 司法判决.

[.my2]
判例法：指由先前法院判决所确立的法律，是法律先例的同义词。类似于源于传统和司法判决的普通法。

Caseload:: The number of cases handled by a judge or a court 法官或法院处理的案件数量.

[.my2]
案件量：指法官或法院所处理的案件数量。

Cause of action:: A legal claim 法律诉求.

[.my2]
诉由：指一项法律诉求。

Chambers:: The offices of a judge and his or her staff 法官及其工作人员的办公室.

[.my2]
法官办公室：指法官及其工作人员的办公室。

Chapter 7:: The chapter of the Bankruptcy Code providing for “liquidation 清算,” that is, the sale of a debtor’s nonexempt property 非豁免财产 and the distribution of the proceeds 收益分配 to creditors. In order to be eligible for Chapter 7, the debtor must satisfy a “means test 收入测试.” The court will evaluate the debtor’s income and expenses 收入与支出 to determine if the debtor may proceed under Chapter 7.

[.my2]
第7章：《破产法典》中规定“清算”程序的章节，即变卖债务人的非豁免财产，并将变卖收益分配给债权人的程序。债务人需通过“收入测试”才有资格适用第7章，法院会评估债务人的收入与支出，以判定其是否可依据第7章启动破产程序。

Chapter 7 trustee:: A person appointed in a Chapter 7 case to represent the interests of the bankruptcy estate and the creditors. The trustee’s responsibilities 职责 include reviewing 审查 the debtor’s petition and schedules 财产清单, liquidating 清算 the property of the estate, and making distributions 分配财产 to creditors. The trustee may also bring actions 提起诉讼 against creditors or the debtor to recover property 追回财产 of the bankruptcy estate.

[.my2]
第7章受托人：指在第7章破产案件中被指定的人员，代表破产财产和债权人的利益。受托人的职责包括审查债务人的申请书与财产清单、清算破产财产、向债权人分配财产，也可对债权人或债务人提起诉讼，以追回破产财产。

Chapter 9:: The chapter of the Bankruptcy Code providing for reorganization 重整 of municipalities 市政实体 (which includes cities and towns 城镇, as well as villages 村庄, counties 县, taxing districts 征税区, municipal utilities 市政公用事业单位, and school districts 学区).

[.my2]
第9章：《破产法典》中规定市政实体重整程序的章节，市政实体包括城镇、村庄、县、征税区、市政公用事业单位及学区。

Chapter 11:: A reorganization bankruptcy 重整破产, usually involving a corporation 公司 or partnership 合伙企业. A Chapter 11 debtor usually proposes a plan of reorganization 重整计划 to keep its business alive 维持企业运营 and pay creditors over time 分期向债权人偿债. Individuals or people in business can also seek relief 寻求救济 in Chapter 11.

[.my2]
第11章：指重整破产程序，通常适用于公司或合伙企业。适用第11章的债务人通常会提出重整计划，以维持企业运营并分期向债权人偿债。个人或从商者也可依据第11章寻求救济。

Chapter 12:: The chapter of the Bankruptcy Code providing for adjustment of debts 债务调整 of a “family farmer 家庭农场主” or “family fisherman 家庭渔民,” as the terms are defined in the Bankruptcy Code.

[.my2]
第12章：《破产法典》中规定“家庭农场主”或“家庭渔民”债务调整程序的章节，上述两类主体的定义由《破产法典》明确。

Chapter 13:: The chapter of the Bankruptcy Code providing for the adjustment of debts of an individual with regular income 有固定收入的个人, often referred to as a “wage-earner” plan 工薪阶层计划. Chapter 13 allows a debtor to keep property 保留财产 and use his or her disposable income 可支配收入 to pay debts over time, usually three to five years 通常为三至五年.

[.my2]
第13章：《破产法典》中规定有固定收入的个人债务调整程序的章节，该程序常被称为“工薪阶层计划”。第13章允许债务人保留财产，并以其可支配收入分期偿债，还款期限通常为三至五年。

Chapter 13 trustee:: A person appointed to administer 管理 a Chapter 13 case. A Chapter 13 trustee’s responsibilities are similar to those of a Chapter 7 trustee; however, a Chapter 13 trustee has the additional responsibilities 额外职责 of overseeing 监督 the debtor’s plan, receiving payments from debtors 收取债务人还款, and disbursing plan payments 向债权人拨付计划还款资金 to creditors.

[.my2]
第13章受托人：指被指定管理第13章破产案件的人员。其职责与第7章受托人类似，但还负有监督债务人的重整计划、收取债务人还款以及向债权人拨付计划还款资金的额外职责。

Chapter 15:: The chapter of the Bankruptcy Code dealing with cases of cross-border insolvency 跨境破产案件.

[.my2]
第15章：《破产法典》中处理跨境破产案件的章节。

Chief judge:: The judge who has primary responsibility 主要职责 for the administration of a court 法院行政管理; chief judges are determined by seniority 按资历确定.

[.my2]
首席法官：指对法院行政管理负主要责任的法官，首席法官的人选按法官资历确定。

Claim:: A creditor’s assertion 主张 of a right to payment 付款请求权 from a debtor or the debtor’s property.

[.my2]
债权主张：指债权人提出的，要求债务人或从债务人财产中获得偿付的付款请求权主张。

Class action:: A lawsuit in which one or more members of a large group 群体, or class 集体, of individuals or other entities sue on behalf of 代表……起诉 the entire class. The district court must find that the claims of the class members contain questions of law or fact in common 存在共同的法律或事实问题 before the lawsuit can proceed as a class action.

[.my2]
集体诉讼：指由某一庞大群体（或集体）中的一名或多名个人或机构，代表整个集体提起的诉讼。地区法院必须认定集体成员的主张存在共同的法律或事实问题，该诉讼才能以集体诉讼的形式推进。

Clerk of court:: The court officer 法院官员 who oversees administrative functions 行政管理职能, especially managing the flow of cases through the court 案件审理流程管理. The clerk’s office is often called a court’s central nervous system 中枢神经系统.

[.my2]
法院书记员：指负责监督法院行政管理职能（尤其是案件审理流程管理）的法院官员。书记员办公室常被称为法院的“中枢神经系统”。

Collateral:: Property that is promised as security 担保物 for the satisfaction of a debt 债务清偿.

[.my2]
担保物：指为保障债务清偿而提供的抵押财产。

Common law:: The legal system 法律体系 that originated in England 起源于英国 and is now in use in the United States, which relies on the articulation 阐述 of legal principles 法律原则 in a historical succession 历史沿革 of judicial decisions. Common law principles can be changed by legislation 立法.

[.my2]
普通法：指起源于英国且现通行于美国的法律体系，该体系依赖于司法判决历史沿革中所阐述的法律原则。普通法原则可通过立法予以修改。

Community service:: A special condition 特殊条件 the court imposes 法院施加的 that requires an individual to work—without pay 无偿工作—for a civic 市政的 or nonprofit organization 非营利组织.

[.my2]
社区服务：指法院施加的特殊处罚条件，要求个人为市政机构或非营利组织无偿工作。

Complaint:: A written statement that begins a civil lawsuit 启动民事诉讼, in which the plaintiff details 详述 the claims against the defendant.

[.my2]
起诉状：指启动民事诉讼的书面陈述，其中原告会详述针对被告的各项主张。

Concurrent sentence:: Prison terms 刑期 for two or more offenses 多项罪名 to be served at the same time 同时执行, rather than one after the other 而非依次执行. Example: Two five-year sentences and one three-year sentence, if served concurrently, result in a maximum of five years behind bars 最高服刑五年.

[.my2]
合并执行刑期：指对两项或多项罪名判处的刑期同时执行，而非依次执行。例如：两项五年刑期和一项三年刑期若合并执行，最高服刑期限为五年。

Confirmation:: Approval of a plan of reorganization by a bankruptcy judge 破产法官批准重整计划.

[.my2]
批准：指破产法官对重整计划予以批准。

Consecutive sentence:: Prison terms for two or more offenses to be served one after the other 依次执行. Example: Two five-year sentences and one three-year sentence, if served consecutively, result in a maximum of 13 years behind bars 最高服刑十三年.

[.my2]
连续执行刑期：指对两项或多项罪名判处的刑期依次执行。例如：两项五年刑期和一项三年刑期若连续执行，最高服刑期限为十三年。

Consumer bankruptcy:: A bankruptcy case filed to reduce or eliminate debts 减免或免除债务 that are primarily consumer debts 消费性债务.

[.my2]
消费者破产：指为减免或免除以消费性债务为主的债务而提起的破产案件。

Consumer debts:: Debts incurred for personal 因个人目的产生的, as opposed to business 而非商业目的, needs.

[.my2]
消费性债务：指因个人目的而非商业目的产生的债务。

Contingent claim:: A claim that may be owed by the debtor under certain circumstances 在特定情况下 (e.g., where the debtor is a cosigner 担保人 on another person’s loan and that person fails to pay 未能还款).

[.my2]
或有债权主张：指在特定情况下才可能由债务人承担的债权主张（例如，债务人是他人贷款的担保人，且该借款人未能还款）。

Contract:: An agreement between two or more people that creates an obligation 创设义务 to do or not to do a particular thing 为或不为特定行为.

[.my2]
合同：指两人或多人之间达成的协议，该协议创设了为或不为特定行为的义务。

Conviction:: A judgment of guilt 有罪判决 against a criminal defendant.

[.my2]
定罪：指针对刑事被告作出的有罪判决。

Counsel:: Legal advice 法律建议; a term also used to refer to the lawyers in a case 也指案件中的律师.

[.my2]
律师/法律建议：指法律建议；该术语也可指代案件中的律师。

Court:: Government entity 政府机构 authorized to resolve legal disputes 解决法律纠纷. Judges sometimes use “court” to refer to themselves in the third person 以第三人称指代自己, as in “the court has read the briefs.”

[.my2]
法院：指被授权解决法律纠纷的政府机构。法官有时会以第三人称用“本庭”指代自己，例如“本庭已阅毕辩护状”。

Court reporter:: A person who makes a word-for-word record 逐字记录 of what is said in court, generally by using a stenographic machine 速记机, shorthand 速记法, or audio recording 录音, and then produces a transcript 制作文本记录 of the proceedings upon request 根据要求制作庭审记录文本.

[.my2]
法庭记录员：指负责对庭审中的发言进行逐字记录的人员，通常借助速记机、速记法或录音设备完成记录，并可根据要求制作庭审记录文本。

Count:: An allegation 指控 in an indictment or information, charging a defendant with a crime. An indictment or information may contain allegations that the defendant committed more than one crime. Each allegation is referred to as a count 一项罪名指控.

[.my2]
罪名指控项：指起诉书或公诉书中指控被告人犯罪的一项内容。一份起诉书或公诉书可包含多项针对被告人的犯罪指控，每一项指控均被称为一项罪名指控。

Creditor:: A person to whom or business to which the debtor owes money 债务人对其负有债务的个人或企业 or that claims to be owed money by the debtor 或主张债务人对其负有债务的个人或企业.

[.my2]
债权人：指债务人对其负有债务，或主张债务人对其负有债务的个人或企业。

Credit counseling:: Generally refers to two events in individual bankruptcy cases: (1) the “individual or group briefing 个人或团体咨询会” from a nonprofit budget and credit counseling agency 非营利性预算与信用咨询机构 that individual debtors must attend prior to filing 申请前必须参加 under any chapter of the Bankruptcy Code; and (2) the “instructional course in personal financial management 个人财务管理指导课程” in Chapters 7 and 13 that an individual debtor must complete before a discharge 债务免除 is entered. There are exceptions 例外情况 to both requirements for certain categories of debtors 特定类别债务人, exigent circumstances 紧急情况, or if the U.S. trustee or bankruptcy administrator have determined that there are insufficient approved credit counseling agencies 经批准的信用咨询机构不足 available to provide the necessary counseling.

[.my2]
信用咨询：通常指个人破产案件中的两项程序：（1）个人债务人依据《破产法典》任一章节申请破产前，必须参加的、由非营利性预算与信用咨询机构举办的“个人或团体咨询会”；（2）个人债务人在获得债务免除前，必须完成的、适用于第7章和第13章破产案件的“个人财务管理指导课程”。针对特定类别债务人、紧急情况，或当美国联邦托管人或破产管理人认定经批准的信用咨询机构不足以提供必要咨询服务时，上述两项要求均可豁免。


=== D

Damages:: Money that a defendant pays a plaintiff in a civil case if the plaintiff has won. Damages may be compensatory 补偿性的 (for loss or injury) or punitive 惩罚性的 (to punish and deter 威慑 future misconduct 不当行为).

[.my2]
损害赔偿金：指民事诉讼中原告胜诉后，被告需向原告支付的款项。损害赔偿金分为补偿性赔偿金（用于弥补损失或伤害）和惩罚性赔偿金（用于惩罚并威慑未来的不当行为）。

Debtor:: A person who has filed a petition 提交申请 for relief 获得救济 under the Bankruptcy Code.

[.my2]
债务人：指依据《破产法典》提交申请以获得救济的个人。

Defendant:: An individual (or business) against whom a lawsuit is filed 成为诉讼对象的.

[.my2]
被告：指被提起诉讼的个人（或企业）。

Debtor’s plan:: A debtor’s detailed description 详细说明 of how the debtor proposes to pay creditors’ claims 债权人的债权主张 over a fixed period of time 固定期限内.

[.my2]
债务人计划：指债务人对其如何在固定期限内偿付债权人债权主张作出的详细说明。

Declaratory judgment:: A judge’s statement 声明 about someone’s rights. For example, a plaintiff may seek a declaratory judgment that a particular statute 法规, as written, violates some constitutional right 违宪权利.

[.my2]
确认判决：指法官就某人权利作出的声明。例如，原告可请求法院作出确认判决，认定某一法规的条文内容侵犯了某项宪法权利。

De facto:: Latin, meaning “in fact 事实上” or “actually 实际上.” Something that exists in fact but not as a matter of law 法律层面上不成立.

[.my2]
事实上的：拉丁语，意为“事实上”或“实际上”。指某事物在现实中存在，但在法律层面上并不成立。

Default judgment:: A judgment awarding 判定 the plaintiff the relief sought in the complaint 起诉状中请求的救济 because the defendant has failed to appear in court 未出庭 or otherwise respond to the complaint 未对起诉状作出回应.

[.my2]
缺席判决：指因被告未出庭或未对起诉状作出回应，法院判定原告获得其在起诉状中请求的救济的判决。

Defendant:: In a civil case, the person or organization against whom the plaintiff brings suit 提起诉讼; in a criminal case, the person accused of the crime 被指控犯罪的人.

[.my2]
被告：民事诉讼中，指被原告提起诉讼的个人或机构；刑事诉讼中，指被指控犯罪的人。

De jure:: Latin, meaning “in law 法律上.” Something that exists by operation of law 依据法律规定而存在.

[.my2]
法律上的：拉丁语，意为“法律上”。指依据法律规定而存在的事物。

De novo:: Latin, meaning “anew 重新.” A trial de novo is a completely new trial 全新审判. Appellate review de novo 重新进行的上诉审查 implies no deference 不尊重；不依从 to the trial judge’s ruling 初审法官的裁定.

[.my2]
重新的：拉丁语，意为“重新”。重新审判指的是一场完全全新的审判。重新进行的上诉审查意味着不依从初审法官的裁定。

Deposition:: An oral statement 口头陈述 made before an officer who is authorized by law to administer oaths 经法律授权主持宣誓的官员. Such statements are often taken to examine potential witnesses 询问潜在证人, to obtain discovery 开展证据开示, or to be used later in trial 庭审中使用. See discovery 见证据开示.

[.my2]
证词录取：指在经法律授权主持宣誓的官员面前作出的口头陈述。此类陈述通常用于询问潜在证人、开展证据开示或在后续庭审中使用。见证据开示。

Discharge:: A release of a debtor from personal liability 个人责任免除 for certain dischargeable debts 可免除债务. Notable exceptions 显著例外 to dischargeability are taxes 税款 and student loans 学生贷款. A discharge releases a debtor from personal liability for certain debts known as dischargeable debts and prevents the creditors owed those debts from taking any action 采取任何行动 against the debtor or the debtor’s property to collect the debts 追讨债务. The discharge also prohibits 禁止 creditors from communicating with the debtor regarding the debt, including through telephone calls, letters, and personal contact 电话、信件及私人接触.

[.my2]
债务免除：指免除债务人对特定可免除债务的个人责任。税款和学生贷款是债务免除的显著例外情形。债务免除会解除债务人对特定可免除债务的个人责任，并禁止享有这些债权的债权人针对债务人或债务人财产采取任何追讨债务的行动。债务免除还禁止债权人就相关债务与债务人进行联络，包括通过电话、信件及私人接触等方式。

Dischargeable debt:: A debt for which the Bankruptcy Code allows the debtor’s personal liability to be eliminated 被免除的.

[.my2]
可免除债务：指《破产法典》允许免除债务人个人责任的债务。

Disclosure statement:: A written document 书面文件 prepared by the Chapter 11 debtor or other plan proponent 计划提出方 that is designed to provide “adequate information 充分信息” to creditors to enable them to evaluate 评估 the Chapter 11 plan of reorganization 重整计划.

[.my2]
披露声明：指由第11章破产案件的债务人或其他计划提出方编制的书面文件，旨在向债权人提供“充分信息”，使其能够评估第11章重整计划。

Discovery:: Procedures 程序 used to obtain disclosure of evidence 获取证据披露 before trial.

[.my2]
证据开示：指庭审前用于获取证据披露的程序。

Dismissal with prejudice:: Court action that prevents an identical lawsuit 完全相同的诉讼 from being filed later 日后被提起.

[.my2]
有既判力的驳回：指法院作出的、禁止当事人日后提起完全相同诉讼的裁定。

Dismissal without prejudice:: Court action that allows the later filing 允许日后再行起诉.

[.my2]
无既判力的驳回：指法院作出的、允许当事人日后再行起诉的裁定。

Disposable income:: Income not reasonably necessary for the maintenance or support 维持生计 or support of the debtor or dependents 受扶养人. If the debtor operates a business 经营企业, disposable income is defined as those amounts over and above what is necessary for the payment of ordinary operating expenses 日常运营开支.

[.my2]
可支配收入：指并非债务人或其受扶养人维持生计所必需的收入。若债务人经营企业，可支配收入指扣除支付日常运营开支所需费用后的剩余收入。

Docket:: A log 日志 containing the complete history 完整历程 of each case in the form of brief chronological entries 按时间顺序的简要记录 summarizing the court proceedings 庭审进程.

[.my2]
案件登记簿：指以按时间顺序的简要记录形式呈现的日志，包含每个案件的完整历程，概述庭审进程。

Due process:: In criminal law, the constitutional guarantee 宪法保障 that a defendant will receive a fair and impartial trial 公正无偏的审判. In civil law, the legal rights 法律权利 of someone who confronts an adverse action 不利行为 threatening liberty or property 威胁自由或财产的.

[.my2]
正当程序：刑法层面，指宪法保障被告人获得公正无偏的审判；民法层面，指遭遇威胁其自由或财产的不利行为的当事人所享有的法律权利。

=== E

En banc:: French, meaning “on the bench 全体法官出庭.” All judges of an appellate court sitting together 全体出庭 to hear a case, as opposed to the routine disposition 常规审理 by panels of three judges 三人合议庭. In the Ninth Circuit 第九巡回上诉法院, an en banc panel consists of 11 randomly selected judges 随机选出的法官.

[.my2]
全体法官出庭审理：法语，意为“全体法官出庭”。指上诉法院全体法官共同出庭审理案件，区别于由三人合议庭进行的常规审理。在第九巡回上诉法院，全体出庭合议庭由11名随机选出的法官组成。

Equitable:: Pertaining to 关于 civil suits in “equity 衡平法” rather than in “law 普通法.” In English legal history 英国法律史, the courts of “law” could order the payment of damages and could afford no other remedy 提供其他救济 (see damages 见损害赔偿金). A separate court of “equity” could order someone to do something or to cease to do something 责令某人作出或停止某行为 (e.g., injunction 禁制令). In American jurisprudence 美国司法体系, the federal courts have both legal and equitable power 普通法与衡平法权力, but the distinction is still an important one. For example, a trial by jury 陪审团审判 is normally available in “law” cases but not in “equity” cases.

[.my2]
衡平法的：指关于衡平法诉讼而非普通法诉讼的。在英国法律史上，普通法法院仅能判令支付损害赔偿金，无法提供其他救济（见损害赔偿金）。独立的衡平法法院则可责令某人作出或停止某行为（例如，禁制令）。在美国司法体系中，联邦法院同时拥有普通法与衡平法权力，但二者的区别仍具有重要意义。例如，陪审团审判通常适用于普通法案件，而不适用于衡平法案件。

Equity:: The value of a debtor’s interest in property 债务人在财产中的权益价值 that remains after liens 留置权 and other creditors’ interests 其他债权人权益 are considered. (Example: If a house valued at $60,000 is subject to a $30,000 mortgage 抵押贷款, there is $30,000 of equity.)

[.my2]
资产净值：指扣除留置权及其他债权人权益后，债务人在财产中剩余的权益价值。（例如：一套价值6万美元的房屋背负3万美元抵押贷款，则该房屋的资产净值为3万美元。）

Evidence:: Information presented in testimony 证言中提交的 or in documents that is used to persuade the fact finder 事实认定者 (judge or jury) to decide the case in favor of one side or the other 作出有利于一方的裁判.

[.my2]
证据：指在证言或文件中提交的信息，用于说服事实认定者（法官或陪审团）作出有利于诉讼一方的裁判。

Exclusionary rule:: Doctrine 原则 that says evidence obtained in violation of a criminal defendant’s constitutional or statutory rights 违反刑事被告宪法或成文法权利获取的 is not admissible at trial 庭审中不可采信.

[.my2]
证据排除规则：指一项法律原则，规定以违反刑事被告宪法或成文法权利的方式获取的证据，在庭审中不可采信。

Exculpatory evidence:: Evidence indicating that a defendant did not commit the crime 证明被告未犯罪的.

[.my2]
无罪证据：指能够证明被告未实施犯罪的证据。

Executory contracts:: Contracts or leases under which both parties to the agreement have duties remaining to be performed 仍有义务待履行的. If a contract or lease is executory, a debtor may assume it 承继合约 (keep the contract) or reject it 拒绝履行 (terminate the contract 终止合约).

[.my2]
待履行合同：指协议双方均仍有义务待履行的合同或租约。若某份合同或租约属于待履行合同，债务人可选择承继合约（继续履行合同）或拒绝履行（终止合约）。

Exempt assets:: Property that a debtor is allowed to retain 保留的, free from the claims of creditors who do not have liens on the property 不受无留置权债权人的债权主张影响的.

[.my2]
豁免资产：指允许债务人保留、且不受无留置权债权人的债权主张影响的财产。

Exemptions, exempt property:: Certain property owned by an individual debtor 个人债务人拥有的特定财产 that the Bankruptcy Code or applicable state law permits the debtor to keep from unsecured creditors 不受无担保债权人追讨的. For example, in some states the debtor may be able to exempt all or a portion of the equity in the debtor’s primary residence 主要居所的资产净值 (homestead exemption 宅地豁免), or some or all “tools of the trade 职业工具” used by the debtor to make a living (i.e., auto tools for an auto mechanic 汽车修理工的修车工具 or dental tools for a dentist 牙医的牙科工具). The availability and amount of property the debtor may exempt 债务人可豁免财产的范围与额度 depends on the state the debtor lives in 取决于债务人的居住州.

[.my2]
豁免财产：指《破产法典》或相关州法律允许个人债务人保留、不受无担保债权人追讨的特定财产。例如，在部分州，债务人可豁免其主要居所的全部或部分资产净值（宅地豁免），或豁免其用于谋生的全部或部分“职业工具”（如汽车修理工的修车工具、牙医的牙科工具）。债务人可豁免财产的范围与额度取决于其居住的州。

Ex parte:: A proceeding brought before a court by one party only 仅由一方当事人向法院提起的程序, without notice to or challenge by the other side 未通知对方或未经对方抗辩的.

[.my2]
单方程序：指仅由一方当事人向法院提起、未通知对方或未经对方抗辩的程序。

=== F

Face sheet filing:: A bankruptcy case filed either without schedules 无财产及债务清单的 or with incomplete schedules listing few creditors and debts 清单不完整、仅列明少量债权人和债务的. (Face sheet filings are often made to delay an eviction 拖延驱逐令 or foreclosure 止赎程序.)

[.my2]
仅提交首页的破产申请：指未附财产及债务清单，或所附清单不完整、仅列明少量债权人和债务的破产案件申请。（提交此类申请通常是为了拖延驱逐令或止赎程序。）

Family farmer:: An individual, individual and spouse 个人及配偶, corporation, or partnership engaged in a farming operation 从事农业生产经营的 that meets certain debt limits 符合特定债务限额 and other statutory criteria 其他法定条件 for filing a petition under Chapter 12.

[.my2]
家庭农场主：指从事农业生产经营，且符合依据第12章提交破产申请的特定债务限额及其他法定条件的个人、个人及配偶、公司或合伙企业。

Federal public defender:: An attorney employed by the federal courts on a full-time basis 联邦法院全职聘用的律师 to provide legal defense 提供法律辩护 to defendants who are unable to afford counsel 无力聘请律师的. The judiciary administers 管理 the federal defender program pursuant to 依据 the Criminal Justice Act 《刑事司法法》.

[.my2]
联邦公设辩护人：指联邦法院全职聘用的律师，为无力聘请律师的被告人提供法律辩护。联邦司法机构依据《刑事司法法》管理联邦公设辩护人项目。

Federal public defender organization:: As provided for in the Criminal Justice Act, an organization established within a federal judicial circuit 联邦司法巡回区内设立的机构 to represent criminal defendants who cannot afford an adequate defense 无力获得充分辩护的刑事被告. Each organization is supervised by 由……监督 a federal public defender appointed by the court of appeals for the circuit 由该巡回区上诉法院任命的.

[.my2]
联邦公设辩护人机构：指依据《刑事司法法》设立的、在联邦司法巡回区内运作的机构，旨在为无力获得充分辩护的刑事被告提供代理服务。每个此类机构均由该巡回区上诉法院任命的联邦公设辩护人负责监督。

Federal question jurisdiction:: Jurisdiction 管辖权 given to federal courts in cases involving the interpretation and application 解释与适用 of the U.S. Constitution, acts of Congress 国会法案, and treaties 条约.

[.my2]
联邦问题管辖权：指联邦法院对涉及美国宪法、国会法案及条约的解释与适用的案件所拥有的管辖权。

Felony:: A serious crime 重罪, usually punishable by at least one year in prison 可判处至少一年监禁的.

[.my2]
重罪：指性质严重的犯罪，通常可判处至少一年监禁。

File:: To place a paper in the official custody 正式存档 of the clerk of court to enter into the files or records of a case 归入案件卷宗或记录的.

[.my2]
提交备案：指将文件交由法院书记员正式存档，归入案件的卷宗或记录。

Fraudulent transfer:: A transfer of a debtor’s property 债务人财产转让 made with intent to defraud 具有欺诈意图的 or for which the debtor receives less than the transferred property’s value 债务人所得对价低于财产价值的.

[.my2]
欺诈性财产转让：指债务人具有欺诈意图的财产转让，或债务人所得对价低于所转让财产价值的财产转让。

Fresh start:: The characterization of a debtor’s status after bankruptcy 债务人破产后的身份定性 (i.e., free of most debts 免除大部分债务). Giving debtors a fresh start is one purpose of the Bankruptcy Code 《破产法典》的宗旨之一.

[.my2]
重新开始：指对债务人破产后身份的定性（即免除大部分债务）。赋予债务人重新开始的机会是《破产法典》的宗旨之一。

=== G

Grand jury:: A body 团体 of 16 to 23 citizens who listen to evidence of criminal allegations 刑事指控证据, which is presented by the prosecutors 检察官提交的, and determine whether there is probable cause 合理根据 to believe an individual committed an offense 实施了犯罪. See also indictment 见起诉书 and U.S. attorney 美国联邦检察官.

[.my2]
大陪审团：指由16至23名公民组成的团体，负责听取检察官提交的刑事指控证据，并判定是否存在合理根据相信某人实施了犯罪。另见起诉书及美国联邦检察官。

=== H

Habeas corpus:: Latin, meaning “you have the body 你须交出人身.” A writ of habeas corpus 人身保护令状 generally is a judicial order 司法命令 forcing law enforcement authorities 执法机关 to produce a prisoner they are holding 交出被羁押者, and to justify the prisoner’s continued confinement 说明继续羁押的理由. Federal judges receive petitions 收到申请 for a writ of habeas corpus from state prison inmates 州监狱囚犯 who say their state prosecutions violated federally protected rights 侵犯了联邦宪法保障权利 in some way.

[.my2]
人身保护令：拉丁语，意为“你须交出人身”。人身保护令状通常是一项司法命令，强制执法机关交出被其羁押的囚犯，并说明继续羁押该囚犯的理由。联邦法官会收到州监狱囚犯提交的人身保护令申请，这些囚犯主张其在州层面的诉讼中，其联邦宪法保障的权利在某种程度上受到了侵犯。

Hearsay:: Evidence presented by a witness who did not see or hear the incident in question 未亲身感知涉案事件的证人提交的 but heard about it from someone else 道听途说的. With some exceptions 存在例外情形, hearsay generally is not admissible as evidence at trial 庭审中通常不可采信.

[.my2]
传闻证据：指由未亲身感知涉案事件、仅道听途说的证人提交的证据。存在例外情形，但传闻证据在庭审中通常不可采信。

Home confinement:: A special condition 特殊条件 the court imposes 法院施加的 that requires an individual to remain at home 居家的 except for certain approved activities 特定经批准的活动, such as work and medical appointments 工作及就医. Home confinement may include the use of electronic monitoring equipment 电子监控设备—a transmitter attached to the wrist or the ankle 佩戴于手腕或脚踝的发射器—to help ensure that the person stays at home as required 确保其按要求居家.

[.my2]
居家监禁：指法院施加的特殊处罚条件，要求个人除从事工作、就医等特定经批准的活动外，必须留在家中。居家监禁可能包括使用电子监控设备——即佩戴于手腕或脚踝的发射器——以确保受刑人按要求居家。

=== I

Impeachment:: 1. The process of calling a witness’s testimony into doubt 质疑证人证言可信度的程序 (e.g., if the attorney can show that the witness may have fabricated portions of his testimony 编造部分证言, the witness is said to be “impeached” 证言可信度被质疑); 2. The constitutional process 宪法程序 whereby the House of Representatives may “impeach” (accuse of misconduct 指控不当行为) high officers of the federal government 联邦政府高级官员, who are then tried by the Senate 随后由参议院审理.

[.my2]
弹劾/质疑证言可信度：1. 指质疑证人证言可信度的程序（例如，若律师能够证明证人可能编造了部分证言，则该证人的证言可信度即被质疑）；2. 指宪法规定的程序，众议院可依据此程序对联邦政府高级官员提出“弹劾”（即指控其存在不当行为），随后由参议院对其进行审理。

In camera:: Latin, meaning “in a judge’s chambers 法官议事室内.” Often means outside the presence of a jury and the public 无陪审团及公众在场的; in private 私下的.

[.my2]
秘密审理：拉丁语，意为“在法官议事室内”。通常指无陪审团及公众在场的、私下进行的审理程序。

Inculpatory evidence:: Evidence indicating that a defendant did commit the crime 证明被告确实施行了犯罪的.

[.my2]
有罪证据：指能够证明被告确实施行了犯罪的证据。

Indictment:: The formal charge 正式指控 issued by a grand jury stating that there is enough evidence that the defendant committed the crime to justify having a trial 有充分证据支持对被告进行审判的; it is used primarily for felonies 主要适用于重罪案件. See also information 见公诉书.

[.my2]
起诉书：指由大陪审团签发的正式指控，载明存在充分证据证明被告实施了犯罪，因此有理由对其进行审判，该文书主要适用于重罪案件。另见公诉书。

In forma pauperis:: Latin, meaning “in the manner of a pauper 以贫民身份.” Permission given by the court to a person to file a case without payment of the required court fees 免缴法定诉讼费的许可 because the person cannot pay them 无力支付的.

[.my2]
以贫民身份免缴诉讼费：拉丁语，意为“以贫民身份”。指法院授予无力支付诉讼费者的一项许可，允许其免缴法定诉讼费而提起诉讼。

Information:: A formal accusation 正式指控 by a government attorney 政府检察官 that the defendant committed a misdemeanor 实施了轻罪的. See also indictment 见起诉书.

[.my2]
公诉书：指由政府检察官作出的正式指控，指控被告实施了轻罪。另见起诉书。

Injunction:: A court order preventing one or more named parties 禁止特定当事人 from taking some action 作出某行为的. A preliminary injunction 初步禁制令 often is issued to allow fact-finding 便于开展事实调查, so a judge can determine whether a permanent injunction 永久禁制令 is justified 具有正当性的.

[.my2]
禁制令：指禁止特定当事人作出某行为的法院命令。法院通常会签发初步禁制令以方便开展事实调查，进而判定是否有理由签发永久禁制令。

Insider (of corporate debtor):: A director 董事, officer 高管, or person in control of the debtor 实际控制人; a partnership in which the debtor is a general partner 债务人为普通合伙人的合伙企业; a general partner of the debtor 债务人的普通合伙人; or a relative of a general partner, director, officer, or person in control of the debtor 前述人员的亲属.

[.my2]
（公司债务人的）内部人：指债务人的董事、高管或实际控制人；债务人为普通合伙人的合伙企业；债务人的普通合伙人；或前述人员的亲属。

Insider (of individual debtor):: Any relative of the debtor 债务人的亲属 or of a general partner of the debtor 债务人普通合伙人的亲属; partnership in which the debtor is a general partner 债务人为普通合伙人的合伙企业; general partner of the debtor 债务人的普通合伙人; or corporation of which the debtor is a director, officer, or person in control 债务人为董事、高管或实际控制人的公司.

[.my2]
（个人债务人的）内部人：指债务人或其普通合伙人的任何亲属；债务人为普通合伙人的合伙企业；债务人的普通合伙人；或债务人为董事、高管或实际控制人的公司。

Interrogatories:: A form of discovery consisting of written questions 书面问题组成的证据开示形式 to be answered in writing and under oath 经宣誓后以书面形式作答的.

[.my2]
书面质询：指一种证据开示形式，由一系列书面问题组成，被质询方需经宣誓后以书面形式作答。

Issue:: 1. The disputed point between parties in a lawsuit 诉讼当事人之间的争议焦点; 2. To send out officially 正式签发, as in a court issuing an order 法院签发命令.

[.my2]
争议焦点/签发：1. 指诉讼当事人之间的争议焦点；2. 指正式签发，例如法院签发一项命令。




=== J

Joint administration:: A court-approved mechanism 机制 under which two or more cases can be administered together 合并审理的. (Assuming no conflicts of interest 假设无利益冲突, these separate businesses or individuals can pool their resources 集中资源, hire the same professionals 聘请同一批专业人员, etc.)

[.my2]
合并管理：指经法院批准的、可将两件或多件案件合并审理的机制。（假设无利益冲突，分属不同主体的企业或个人可通过该机制集中资源、聘请同一批专业人员等。）

Joint petition:: One bankruptcy petition filed by a husband and wife together 夫妻共同提交的.

[.my2]
联合破产申请：指由夫妻双方共同提交的一份破产申请书。

Judge:: An official of the Judicial branch 司法部门官员 with the authority to decide lawsuits brought before courts 有权裁决提交至法院的诉讼的. Used generically 作为通用称谓时, the term judge may also refer to all judicial officers 司法官员, including Supreme Court justices 最高法院大法官.

[.my2]
法官：指司法部门中有权裁决提交至法院的诉讼的官员。该术语作为通用称谓时，也可指代包括最高法院大法官在内的所有司法官员。

Judgeship:: The position of judge 法官职位. By statute 依据成文法, Congress authorizes 授权 the number of judgeships for each district and appellate court 各地区法院和上诉法院的法官编制数量.

[.my2]
法官职位：指法官的任职岗位。国会依据成文法，为各地区法院和上诉法院核定法官编制数量。

Judgment:: The official decision of a court finally resolving the dispute between the parties to the lawsuit 最终解决诉讼当事人之间争议的.

[.my2]
判决：指法院作出的、最终解决诉讼当事人之间争议的正式裁定。

Judicial Conference of the United States:: The policy-making entity 决策机构 for the federal court system 联邦法院系统的. A 27-judge body 由27名法官组成的机构 whose presiding officer 主席 is the Chief Justice of the United States 美国首席大法官.

[.my2]
美国司法会议：指联邦法院系统的决策机构，是一个由27名法官组成的机构，主席由美国首席大法官担任。

Jurisdiction:: The legal authority of a court to hear and decide a certain type of case 审理并裁决某类案件的法定权限. It also is used as a synonym for venue 审判地的同义词, meaning the geographic area over which the court has territorial jurisdiction 属地管辖权 to decide cases.

[.my2]
管辖权：指法院审理并裁决某类案件的法定权限。该术语也可作为“审判地”的同义词，指法院拥有属地管辖权、可对案件进行裁决的地理区域。

Jurisprudence:: The study of law and the structure of the legal system 法律及法律体系结构的研究.

[.my2]
法理学：指对法律及法律体系结构进行的研究。

Jury:: The group of persons selected to hear the evidence in a trial 经遴选后在庭审中听取证据的人员群体 and render a verdict on matters of fact 就事实问题作出裁决的. See also grand jury 另见大陪审团.

[.my2]
陪审团：指经遴选后，在庭审中听取证据并就事实问题作出裁决的人员群体。另见大陪审团。

Jury instructions:: A judge’s directions 指示 to the jury before it begins deliberations 开始评议前 regarding the factual questions it must answer 其必须回答的事实问题 and the legal rules that it must apply 其必须适用的法律规则.

[.my2]
陪审团指示：指法官在陪审团开始评议前，向其作出的关于必须回答的事实问题和必须适用的法律规则的指示。

=== L

Lawsuit:: A legal action 法律行动 started by a plaintiff against a defendant based on a complaint 基于起诉状提起的 that the defendant failed to perform a legal duty 未履行法定义务, which resulted in harm to the plaintiff 对原告造成损害的.

[.my2]
诉讼：指原告基于被告未履行法定义务并对其造成损害的起诉状，针对被告提起的法律行动。

Lien:: A charge on specific property 特定财产上的担保权益 that is designed to secure payment of a debt 担保债务清偿的 or performance of an obligation 担保义务履行的. A debtor may still be responsible for a lien after a discharge 债务免除后仍可能对留置权承担责任.

[.my2]
留置权：指为担保债务清偿或义务履行而在特定财产上设立的担保权益。债务人在债务免除后，仍可能需对留置权承担责任。

Litigation:: A case, controversy 争议, or lawsuit. Participants (plaintiffs and defendants) in lawsuits are called litigants 诉讼当事人.

[.my2]
诉讼：指一起案件、争议或诉讼程序。参与诉讼的各方（原告和被告）被称为诉讼当事人。

Liquidation:: The sale of a debtor’s property 债务人财产的变卖 with the proceeds to be used for the benefit of creditors 收益用于向债权人偿债的.

[.my2]
清算：指变卖债务人的财产，并将变卖收益用于向债权人偿债的程序。

Liquidated claim:: A creditor’s claim for a fixed amount of money 金额固定的债权主张.

[.my2]
确定金额债权：指债权人提出的、金额固定的债权主张。

=== M

Magistrate judge:: A judicial officer of a district court 地区法院司法官员 who conducts initial proceedings in criminal cases 主持刑事案件初步程序, decides criminal misdemeanor cases 裁决轻罪刑事案件, conducts many pretrial civil and criminal matters on behalf of district judges 代表地区法官处理大量民事和刑事预审事务, and decides civil cases with the consent of the parties 经当事人同意后裁决民事案件的.

[.my2]
治安法官：指地区法院的司法官员，负责主持刑事案件初步程序、裁决轻罪刑事案件、代表地区法官处理大量民事和刑事预审事务，以及经当事人同意后裁决民事案件。

Means test:: Section 707(b)(2) of the Bankruptcy Code applies a “means test” 收入测试 to determine whether an individual debtor’s Chapter 7 filing is presumed to be an abuse of the Bankruptcy Code 推定存在滥用《破产法典》情形 requiring dismissal or conversion of the case 需驳回案件或转换破产章节的 (generally to Chapter 13 通常转为第13章). Abuse is presumed if the debtor’s aggregate current monthly income 月度总收入 (see definition above 见前述定义) over 5 years, net of certain statutorily allowed expenses 扣除法定许可开支后的净额 is more than (i) $10,000, or (ii) 25 percent of the debtor’s nonpriority unsecured debt 非优先无担保债务的25%, as long as that amount is at least $6,000 且该金额至少为6000美元. The debtor may rebut a presumption of abuse 推翻滥用推定 only by a showing of special circumstances 证明存在特殊情形 that justify additional expenses 有理由产生额外开支 or adjustments of current monthly income 或调整月度总收入的.

[.my2]
收入测试：《破产法典》第707(b)(2)条规定了“收入测试”，用于判定个人债务人依据第7章提交的破产申请是否推定存在滥用《破产法典》的情形，进而需驳回案件或转换破产章节（通常转为第13章）。如果债务人五年内的月度总收入扣除法定许可开支后的净额，超过（1）10000美元，或（2）其非优先无担保债务的25%且该金额至少为6000美元，则推定存在滥用情形。债务人仅可通过证明存在特殊情形、有理由产生额外开支或调整月度总收入，来推翻该滥用推定。

Mental health treatment:: Special condition the court imposes 法院施加的特殊条件 to require an individual to undergo evaluation and treatment for a mental disorder 接受精神障碍评估和治疗的. Treatment may include psychiatric 精神病学的, psychological 心理学的, and sex offense–specific evaluations 针对性犯罪的评估, inpatient or outpatient counseling 住院或门诊咨询, and medication 药物治疗.

[.my2]
心理健康治疗：指法院施加的特殊处罚条件，要求相关个人接受精神障碍的评估和治疗。治疗内容可能包括精神病学评估、心理学评估、针对性犯罪的评估、住院或门诊咨询以及药物治疗。

Misdemeanor:: An offense punishable by one year of imprisonment or less 可判处一年以下监禁的违法行为. See also felony 另见重罪.

[.my2]
轻罪：指可判处一年以下监禁的违法行为。另见重罪。

Mistrial:: An invalid trial 无效审判, caused by fundamental error 因重大错误导致的. When a mistrial is declared 宣布审判无效后, the trial must start again with the selection of a new jury 需遴选新的陪审团重新开庭.

[.my2]
无效审判：指因重大错误导致的无效审判程序。法院宣布审判无效后，需遴选新的陪审团重新开庭审理。

Moot:: Not subject to a court ruling 不受法院裁决约束的 because the controversy has not actually arisen 争议尚未实际发生 or has ended 或争议已终结的.

[.my2]
无实际争议的：指因争议尚未实际发生或已终结，而不受法院裁决约束的状态。

Motion:: A request by a litigant to a judge for a decision on an issue relating to the case 就案件相关争议事项作出裁定的申请.

[.my2]
动议：指诉讼当事人向法官提出的、请求就案件相关争议事项作出裁定的申请。

Motion to lift the automatic stay:: A request by a creditor to allow the creditor to take action against the debtor or the debtor’s property 针对债务人或其财产采取行动的申请 that would otherwise be prohibited by the automatic stay 若不申请则会被自动中止程序禁止的.

[.my2]
解除自动中止动议：指债权人提出的申请，请求法院允许其针对债务人或其财产采取行动——该行动若不申请则会被自动中止程序所禁止。

Motion in limine:: A pretrial motion 审前动议 requesting the court to prohibit the other side from presenting 禁止对方提交证据的, or even referring to 甚至禁止提及相关内容的, evidence on matters said to be so highly prejudicial 具有高度偏见性的 that no steps taken by the judge can prevent the jury from being unduly influenced 法官无法采取措施阻止陪审团受到不当影响的.

[.my2]
审前证据排除动议：指一种审前动议，请求法院禁止对方提交或甚至提及某些事项的证据——这些事项被认为具有高度偏见性，法官无法通过任何措施阻止陪审团受到其不当影响。

=== N

No-asset case:: A Chapter 7 case in which there are no assets available to satisfy any portion of the creditors’ unsecured claims 无可用资产偿付债权人任何无担保债权的.

[.my2]
无资产案件：指第7章破产案件中，债务人无可用资产偿付债权人任何无担保债权的情形。

Nolo contendere:: Latin, meaning “no contest 不抗辩.” A plea of nolo contendere has the same effect as a plea of guilty 与认罪答辩具有同等效力 as far as the criminal sentence is concerned 就刑事量刑而言, but may not be considered as an admission of guilt for any other purpose 但在其他任何情形下不得被视为认罪的.

[.my2]
不抗辩答辩：拉丁语，意为“不抗辩”。就刑事量刑而言，不抗辩答辩与认罪答辩具有同等效力，但在其他任何情形下，该答辩不得被视为被告人认罪。

Nondischargeable debt:: A debt that cannot be eliminated in bankruptcy 破产程序中不可免除的债务. Examples include a home mortgage 住房抵押贷款, debts for alimony or child support 赡养费或子女抚养费债务, certain taxes 特定税款, debts for most government-funded or guaranteed educational loans 大部分政府资助或担保的教育贷款债务 or benefit overpayments 福利多付款项债务, debts arising from death or personal injury caused by driving while intoxicated 醉酒驾驶导致他人死亡或人身伤害产生的债务 or under the influence of drugs 吸毒后驾驶导致的, and debts for restitution 赔偿金债务 or a criminal fine 刑事罚金债务 included in a sentence on the debtor’s conviction of a crime 属于债务人定罪判决中载明的内容的. Some debts 部分债务, such as debts for money or property obtained by false pretenses 以欺诈手段获取金钱或财产产生的债务 and debts for fraud or defalcation 欺诈或挪用产生的债务 while acting in a fiduciary capacity 以受托人身份履职期间的, may be declared nondischargeable 可被宣告为不可免除债务 only if a creditor timely files 债权人及时提起诉讼 and prevails in a nondischargeability action 并在不可免除债务诉讼中胜诉的.

[.my2]
不可免除债务：指在破产程序中不可被免除的债务。例如住房抵押贷款、赡养费或子女抚养费债务、特定税款、大部分政府资助或担保的教育贷款债务、福利多付款项债务、醉酒或吸毒后驾驶导致他人死亡或人身伤害产生的债务，以及属于债务人定罪判决中载明的赔偿金债务或刑事罚金债务。部分债务（如以欺诈手段获取金钱或财产产生的债务、以受托人身份履职期间因欺诈或挪用产生的债务），仅在债权人及时提起诉讼并在不可免除债务诉讼中胜诉的情况下，才可被宣告为不可免除债务。

Nonexempt assets:: Property of a debtor that can be liquidated to satisfy claims of creditors 可被清算以偿付债权人债权的债务人财产.

[.my2]
非豁免资产：指可被清算以偿付债权人债权的债务人财产。

=== O

Objection to dischargeability:: A trustee’s or creditor’s objection to the debtor being released from personal liability for certain dischargeable debts 反对债务人免除特定可免除债务的个人责任的异议. Common reasons include allegations that the debt to be discharged was incurred by false pretenses 以欺诈手段产生的 or that debt arose because of the debtor’s fraud while acting as a fiduciary 债务人以受托人身份履职期间因欺诈产生的.

[.my2]
债务免除异议：指破产受托人或债权人提出的异议，反对债务人免除特定可免除债务的个人责任。常见理由包括主张相关债务是债务人以欺诈手段产生的，或是债务人以受托人身份履职期间因欺诈产生的。

Objection to exemptions:: A trustee’s or creditor’s objection to the debtor’s attempt to claim certain property as exempt from liquidation by the trustee to creditors 反对债务人主张特定财产为豁免资产、免受受托人清算以偿付债权人的异议.

[.my2]
豁免财产异议：指破产受托人或债权人提出的异议，反对债务人主张特定财产为豁免资产、免受受托人清算以偿付债权人。

Opinion:: A judge’s written explanation of the decision of the court 对法院裁定作出的书面解释. Because a case may be heard by three or more judges in the court of appeals 上诉法院可能由三名或更多法官审理案件, the opinion in appellate decisions can take several forms 上诉裁定的意见书分为多种形式. If all the judges completely agree on the result 若全体法官对裁定结果完全一致, one judge will write the opinion for all 由一名法官代表全体撰写意见书. If all the judges do not agree 若法官意见存在分歧, the formal decision will be based upon the view of the majority 正式裁定以多数法官的意见为准, and one member of the majority will write the opinion 由多数方中的一名法官撰写意见书. The judges who did not agree with the majority 持不同意见的法官 may write separately in dissenting 异议意见书 or concurring opinions 协同意见书 to present their views 单独撰写文书阐述自身观点的. A dissenting opinion disagrees with the majority opinion 与多数意见相悖的 because of the reasoning 基于推理过程 and/or the principles of law 及/或法律原则 the majority used to decide the case 多数方作出裁定时所依据的. A concurring opinion agrees with the decision of the majority opinion 认同多数意见的裁定结果的, but offers further comment or clarification 但会提供进一步的评述或说明 or even an entirely different reason for reaching the same result 甚至会提出得出同一结果的完全不同的理由的. Only the majority opinion can serve as binding precedent in future cases 只有多数意见书可作为未来案件的有约束力先例. See also precedent 另见先例.

[.my2]
意见书：指法官对法院裁定作出的书面解释。上诉法院审理案件时可能由三名或更多法官组成合议庭，因此上诉裁定的意见书分为多种形式。若全体法官对裁定结果完全一致，则由一名法官代表全体撰写意见书；若法官意见存在分歧，则正式裁定以多数法官的意见为准，由多数方中的一名法官撰写意见书。持不同意见的法官可单独撰写异议意见书或协同意见书，阐述自身观点。异议意见书因多数方作出裁定时所依据的推理过程及/或法律原则，而与多数意见相悖；协同意见书认同多数意见的裁定结果，但会提供进一步的评述或说明，甚至提出得出同一结果的完全不同的理由。只有多数意见书可作为未来案件的有约束力先例。另见先例。

Oral argument:: An opportunity for lawyers to summarize their position before the court 向法院概述己方立场的机会 and also to answer the judges’ questions 并回答法官提问的.

[.my2]
口头辩论：指律师向法院概述己方立场，并回答法官提问的机会。

=== P

Panel:: 1. In appellate cases 上诉案件中, a group of judges (usually three) assigned to decide the case 被指派审理案件的法官小组（通常为三人）; 2. In the jury selection process 陪审团遴选程序中, the group of potential jurors 潜在陪审员群体; 3. The list of attorneys who are both available and qualified to serve as court-appointed counsel 有资格担任法院指定辩护律师的 for criminal defendants who cannot afford their own counsel 无力聘请私人律师的刑事被告的律师名单.

[.my2]
合议庭/候选陪审员名单/指定律师名单：1. 上诉案件中，指被指派审理案件的法官小组（通常为三人）；2. 陪审团遴选程序中，指潜在陪审员群体；3. 指有时间且有资格，为无力聘请私人律师的刑事被告担任法院指定辩护律师的律师名单。

Parole:: The release of a prison inmate 囚犯的释放—granted by the U.S. Parole Commission 由美国假释委员会批准的—after the inmate has completed part of his or her sentence in a federal prison 囚犯在联邦监狱服满部分刑期后. When the parolee is released to the community 假释人员获释重返社区后, he or she is placed under the supervision of a U.S. probation officer 需接受美国缓刑官的监督. The Sentencing Reform Act of 1984 abolished parole 废除了假释制度 in favor of a determinate sentencing system 代之以确定刑制度 in which the sentence is set by sentencing guidelines 量刑由量刑指南确定的. Now 如今, without the option of parole 由于假释制度已被取消, the term of imprisonment the court imposes 法院判处的监禁期限 is the actual time the person spends in prison 即为囚犯实际服刑的时间.

[.my2]
假释：指囚犯在联邦监狱服满部分刑期后，由美国假释委员会批准予以释放的制度。假释人员获释重返社区后，需接受美国缓刑官的监督。1984年《量刑改革法》废除了假释制度，代之以确定刑制度，量刑由量刑指南确定。如今，由于假释制度已被取消，法院判处的监禁期限即为囚犯实际服刑的时间。

Party in interest:: A party who has standing to be heard by the court 有资格向法院陈述意见的当事人 in a matter to be decided in the bankruptcy case 就破产案件中待裁决事项的. The debtor 债务人, U.S. trustee or bankruptcy administrator 美国联邦托管人或破产管理人, case trustee 案件受托人, and creditors 债权人 are parties in interest for most matters 就大部分事项而言均为利害关系方.

[.my2]
利害关系方：指就破产案件中待裁决事项，有资格向法院陈述意见的当事人。就大部分事项而言，债务人、美国联邦托管人或破产管理人、案件受托人以及债权人均为利害关系方。

Petition preparer:: A business not authorized to practice law 未经授权执业的机构 that prepares bankruptcy petitions 代拟破产申请书的.

[.my2]
申请书代拟机构：指未经授权执业、但为他人代拟破产申请书的机构。

Per curiam:: Latin, meaning “for the court 代表法院.” In appellate courts 上诉法院中, often refers to an unsigned opinion 通常指无署名的意见书.

[.my2]
代表法院的：拉丁语，意为“代表法院”。在上诉法院中，该术语通常指无署名的意见书。

Peremptory challenge:: A district court may grant each side in a civil or criminal trial the right to exclude a certain number of prospective jurors 排除一定数量潜在陪审员的权利 without cause or giving a reason 无需说明理由的.

[.my2]
无因回避：指地区法院可赋予民事或刑事诉讼的双方当事人，无需说明理由即排除一定数量潜在陪审员的权利。

Petit jury (or trial jury):: A group of citizens who hear the evidence presented by both sides at trial 庭审中听取双方提交证据的公民群体 and determine the facts in dispute 并就争议事实作出认定的. Federal criminal juries consist of 12 persons 联邦刑事陪审团由12人组成. Federal civil juries consist of at least six persons 联邦民事陪审团至少由6人组成.

[.my2]
小陪审团（或审判陪审团）：指庭审中听取双方提交证据并就争议事实作出认定的公民群体。联邦刑事陪审团由12人组成，联邦民事陪审团至少由6人组成。

Petition:: The document that initiates the filing of a bankruptcy proceeding 启动破产程序的文件, setting forth basic information regarding the debtor 载明债务人基本信息的, including name 姓名, address 住址, chapter under which the case is filed 案件所依据的破产章节, and estimated amount of assets and liabilities 资产和负债的预估金额.

[.my2]
破产申请书：指启动破产程序的文件，其中载明债务人的基本信息，包括姓名、住址、案件所依据的破产章节，以及资产和负债的预估金额。

Petty offense:: A federal misdemeanor punishable by six months or less in prison 可判处六个月以下监禁的联邦轻罪.

[.my2]
轻微违法行为：指可判处六个月以下监禁的联邦轻罪。

Plaintiff:: A person or business that files a formal complaint with the court 向法院提交正式起诉状的个人或企业.

[.my2]
原告：指向法院提交正式起诉状的个人或企业。

Plan:: A debtor’s detailed description of how the debtor proposes to pay creditors’ claims over a fixed period of time 债务人就如何在固定期限内偿付债权人债权主张作出的详细说明.

[.my2]
重整计划：指债务人就如何在固定期限内偿付债权人债权主张作出的详细说明。

Plea:: In a criminal case 刑事案件中, the defendant’s statement pleading “guilty” or “not guilty” 作出认罪或不认罪答辩的陈述 in answer to the charges 针对指控作出的. See also nolo contendere 另见不抗辩答辩.

[.my2]
答辩：指刑事案件中，被告人针对指控作出的认罪或不认罪答辩的陈述。另见不抗辩答辩。

Pleadings:: Written statements filed with the court 向法院提交的书面陈述 that describe a party’s legal or factual assertions about the case 阐述当事人对案件的法律或事实主张的.

[.my2]
诉答文书：指向法院提交的书面陈述，用于阐述当事人对案件的法律或事实主张。

Postpetition transfer:: A transfer of the debtor’s property made after the commencement of the case 案件启动后进行的债务人财产转让.

[.my2]
破产申请后财产转让：指破产案件启动后进行的债务人财产转让行为。

Prebankruptcy planning:: The arrangement (or rearrangement) of a debtor’s property 债务人对其财产进行的安排（或重新安排） to allow the debtor to take maximum advantage of exemptions 以最大限度利用豁免制度的. (Prebankruptcy planning typically includes converting nonexempt assets into exempt assets 通常包括将非豁免资产转换为豁免资产).

[.my2]
破产前置规划：指债务人为最大限度利用豁免制度，对其财产进行的安排（或重新安排）。（破产前置规划通常包括将非豁免资产转换为豁免资产）

Precedent:: A court decision in an earlier case with facts and legal issues similar to a dispute currently before a court 与当前法院审理的争议具有相似事实和法律问题的先前案件判决. Judges will generally “follow precedent” 遵循先例, meaning that they use the principles established in earlier cases 适用先前案件确立的原则 to decide new cases that have similar facts and raise similar legal issues 裁决具有相似事实和法律问题的新案件的. A judge will disregard precedent 法官会无视先例 if a party can show that the earlier case was wrongly decided 若当事人能够证明先前案件的裁决存在错误的 or that it differed in some significant way from the current case 或先前案件与当前案件存在重大差异的.

[.my2]
先例：指与当前法院审理的争议具有相似事实和法律问题的先前案件判决。法官通常会“遵循先例”，即适用先前案件确立的原则，裁决具有相似事实和法律问题的新案件。若当事人能够证明先前案件的裁决存在错误，或先前案件与当前案件存在重大差异，法官则可无视该先例。

Preferential debt payment:: A debt payment made to a creditor in the 90-day period before a debtor files bankruptcy 债务人提交破产申请前90天内向债权人支付的款项 (or within one year if the creditor was an insider 若债权人为内部人则为提交申请前一年内) that gives the creditor more than the creditor would receive in the debtor’s Chapter 7 case 使该债权人获得的偿付高于其在债务人第7章破产案件中可获得的金额的.

[.my2]
优先偿付债务：指债务人提交破产申请前90天内向债权人支付的款项（若债权人为内部人，则为提交申请前一年内），且该笔付款使该债权人获得的偿付高于其在债务人第7章破产案件中可获得的金额。

Presentence report:: A report prepared by a court’s probation officer 法院缓刑官编制的报告, after a person has been convicted of an offense 某人被定罪后, summarizing for the court the background information needed to determine the appropriate sentence 为法院汇总确定适当刑罚所需的背景信息的.

[.my2]
量刑前调查报告：指某人被定罪后，由法院缓刑官编制的报告，用于为法院汇总确定适当刑罚所需的背景信息。

Pretrial conference:: A meeting of the judge and lawyers 法官与律师召开的会议 to plan the trial 规划庭审流程的, to discuss which matters should be presented to the jury 讨论哪些事项应提交陪审团审议的, to review proposed evidence and witnesses 审核拟提交的证据和证人的, and to set a trial schedule 确定庭审时间表的. Typically 通常情况下, the judge and the parties also discuss the possibility of settlement of the case 法官与各方当事人还会讨论案件的和解可能性.

[.my2]
审前会议：指法官与律师召开的会议，用于规划庭审流程、讨论哪些事项应提交陪审团审议、审核拟提交的证据和证人，以及确定庭审时间表。通常情况下，法官与各方当事人还会在会议中讨论案件的和解可能性。

Pretrial services:: A function of the federal courts 联邦法院的一项职能 that takes place at the very start of the criminal justice process 于刑事司法程序启动之初开展的—after a person has been arrested and charged with a federal crime 某人被逮捕并被指控犯有联邦罪行后 and before he or she goes to trial 且在其受审前. Pretrial services officers focus on investigating the backgrounds of these persons 审前服务官负责调查这些人的背景 to help the court determine whether to release or detain them while they await trial 以协助法院判定其在候审期间应被释放还是羁押的. The decision is based on whether these individuals are likely to flee 判定依据是这些人是否存在潜逃风险 or pose a threat to the community 或对社区构成威胁. If the court orders release 若法院裁定释放, a pretrial services officer supervises the person in the community until he or she returns to court 审前服务官需在社区中对其进行监督,直至其按时出庭.

[.my2]
审前服务：指联邦法院的一项职能，于刑事司法程序启动之初开展——即某人被逮捕并被指控犯有联邦罪行后、受审前。审前服务官负责调查这些人的背景，协助法院判定其在候审期间应被释放还是羁押，判定依据是这些人是否存在潜逃风险或对社区构成威胁。若法院裁定释放，审前服务官需在社区中对其进行监督，直至其按时出庭。

Priority:: The Bankruptcy Code’s statutory ranking of unsecured claims 《破产法典》对无担保债权的法定顺位排序 that determines the order in which unsecured claims will be paid 该排序决定了无担保债权的偿付顺序 if there is not enough money to pay all unsecured claims in full 当资金不足以全额偿付所有无担保债权时.

[.my2]
优先顺位：指《破产法典》对无担保债权的法定顺位排序，该排序会在资金不足以全额偿付所有无担保债权时，决定无担保债权的偿付顺序。

Priority claim:: An unsecured claim that is entitled to be paid ahead of other unsecured claims 有权优先于其他无担保债权获得偿付的无担保债权 that are not entitled to priority status 不享有优先顺位的. Priority refers to the order in which these unsecured claims are to be paid 优先顺位指的是此类无担保债权的偿付顺序.

[.my2]
优先债权：指有权优先于其他不享有优先顺位的无担保债权获得偿付的无担保债权。优先顺位指的是此类无担保债权的偿付顺序。

Probation:: Sentencing option in the federal courts. With probation, instead of sending an individual to prison, the court releases the person to the community and orders him or her to complete a period of supervision monitored by a U.S. probation officer and to abide by 遵守；遵循 certain conditions.

[.my2]
缓刑 联邦法院的一种量刑选项。适用缓刑时，法院不会将个人送入监狱，而是将其释放回社区，并责令其在一段监督期内接受美国缓刑官的监管，同时遵守特定条件。

Probation officer:: Officers of the probation office of a court. Probation officer duties include conducting presentence investigations 调查, preparing presentence reports on convicted 被定罪的 defendants 被告, and supervising released defendants.

[.my2]
缓刑官 法院缓刑办公室的工作人员。缓刑官的职责包括开展判刑前调查、为已定罪的被告撰写判刑前报告，以及对获释的被告进行监管。

Procedure:: The rules for conducting a lawsuit 诉讼；官司; there are rules of civil procedure 民事诉讼程序, criminal procedure 刑事诉讼程序, evidence 证据, bankruptcy 破产, and appellate procedure 上诉程序.

[.my2]
诉讼程序 进行诉讼所依据的规则；其中包含民事诉讼程序规则、刑事诉讼程序规则、证据规则、破产程序规则以及上诉程序规则。

Proof of claim:: A written statement describing the reason a debtor 债务人 owes a creditor 债权人 money, which typically sets forth 阐明；陈述 the amount of money owed. (There is an official form for this purpose.)

[.my2]
债权证明 一份书面声明，用于说明债务人拖欠债权人款项的缘由，通常会列明所欠款项的金额。（此事有专门的官方表格可供填写。）

Pro per:: A slang 俚语的 expression sometimes used to refer to a pro se litigant 诉讼当事人. It is a corruption 讹用；误用 of the Latin phrase “in propria persona.”

[.my2]
自行诉讼（非正式说法） 一个俚语表达，有时用于指代自行诉讼的当事人。该说法是拉丁语短语“in propria persona（亲自、本人）”的讹用形式。

Property of the estate:: All legal or equitable 衡平法的 interests 权益 of the debtor in property as of the commencement 开始；启动 of the case.

[.my2]
破产财产 自案件启动之日起，债务人在各类财产中所享有的全部法定权益或衡平法权益。

Pro se:: Representing oneself. Serving as one’s own lawyer.

[.my2]
自行诉讼 自我代理，担任自己的律师。

Prosecute:: To charge 控告；指控 someone with a crime. A prosecutor 检察官 tries a criminal case on behalf of 代表 the government.

[.my2]
提起公诉；起诉: 控告某人犯罪。检察官代表政府对刑事案件提起诉讼。

Pro tem:: Temporary 暂时的；临时的.

[.my2]
临时的: 暂时的；临时的。

=== R

Reaffirmation agreement:: An agreement by a debtor to continue paying a dischargeable 可免除的 debt after the bankruptcy, usually to keep collateral 抵押品 or mortgaged 抵押的 property that would otherwise be subject to repossession 收回；取回.

[.my2]
债务重新确认协议: 债务人与债权人达成的协议，约定在破产程序结束后继续偿还原本可免除的债务，通常是为了保留抵押品或已抵押的财产，避免这些财产被收回。

Record:: A written account 记录；记述 of the proceedings 诉讼过程；程序 in a case, including all pleadings 诉状, evidence, and exhibits 证据物；物证 submitted in the course of the case.

[.my2]
诉讼记录: 对案件诉讼过程的书面记录，包含案件审理期间提交的所有诉状、证据以及物证。

Redemption:: A procedure in a link:americanlaw101_ch07.html[Chapter 7] case whereby 凭此；借以 a debtor removes a secured creditor’s 有担保债权人 lien 留置权；抵押权 on collateral by paying the creditor the value of the property. The debtor may then retain 保留；保有 the property.

[.my2]
赎回权: 在《美国破产法》第七章规定的案件中适用的一项程序，债务人通过向有担保债权人支付财产相应价值，解除债权人对抵押品享有的留置权或抵押权，进而保留该财产的所有权。

Remand:: Send back 送回；发回.

[.my2]
发回重审: 送回；发回（下级法院）。

Reverse:: The act of a court setting aside 撤销；驳回 the decision of a lower court 下级法院. A reversal 撤销原判 is often accompanied by 伴随；随同 a remand to the lower court for further proceedings 进一步诉讼程序.

[.my2]
撤销原判: 法院撤销下级法院判决的行为。撤销原判的裁定作出后，往往会伴随将案件发回下级法院进行进一步审理的决定。

=== S

Sanction:: A penalty 处罚；惩罚 or other type of enforcement 强制措施 used to bring about compliance with 遵守 the law or with rules and regulations 规章制度.

[.my2]
制裁；处罚: 一种处罚手段或其他类型的强制措施，用于督促相关主体遵守法律或规章制度。

Schedules:: Lists submitted by the debtor along with the petition 请愿书；破产申请书 (or shortly thereafter), showing the debtor’s assets 资产, liabilities 负债, and other financial information. (There are official forms a debtor must use.)

[.my2]
财产及债务明细表: 债务人在提交破产申请书时（或提交后不久）一并提交的清单，用于列明自身的资产、负债以及其他财务信息。（债务人必须使用专门的官方表格填写。）

Secured creditor:: A secured creditor is an individual or business that holds a claim against the debtor that is secured by a lien on property of the estate. The property subject to 受……约束的 the lien is the secured creditor’s collateral.

[.my2]
有担保债权人: 指对债务人享有债权，且该债权以破产财产上的留置权或抵押权作为担保的个人或企业。被设定担保权益的财产即为有担保债权人的抵押品。

Secured debt:: Debt backed by 以……为担保 a mortgage 抵押, pledge of collateral 抵押品质押, or other lien; debt for which the creditor has the right to pursue 追索；主张 specific pledged property upon default 违约；不履行义务. Examples include home mortgages, auto loans 汽车贷款 and tax liens 税务留置权.

[.my2]
有担保债务: 以抵押、抵押品质押或其他担保权益作为担保的债务；债权人有权在债务人违约时，对特定的质押财产行使追索权。此类债务包括住房抵押贷款、汽车贷款以及税务留置权债务等。

Senior judge:: A federal judge who, after attaining 达到；获得 the requisite 必要的；必需的 age and length of judicial experience 司法经验, takes senior status 资深法官身份, thus creating a vacancy 空缺 among a court’s active judges 在职法官. A senior judge retains the judicial office and may cut back 削减；减少 his or her workload 工作量 by as much as 75 percent, but many opt to 选择 keep a larger caseload 案件数量.

[.my2]
资深法官: 达到法定年龄且具备规定司法工作年限的联邦法官，可申请转为资深法官身份，此举会使法院的在职法官职位出现空缺。资深法官保留法官职务，可将工作量削减高达75%，但许多人仍选择承担较多的案件量。

Sentence:: The punishment ordered by a court for a defendant convicted of a crime.

[.my2]
判决；量刑: 法院对被定罪的被告判处的刑罚。

Sentencing guidelines:: A set of rules and principles established by the United States Sentencing Commission 美国量刑委员会 that trial judges 初审法官 use to determine the sentence for a convicted defendant.

[.my2]
量刑指南: 由美国量刑委员会制定的一套规则与准则，供初审法官在确定已定罪被告的刑罚时参考适用。

Service of process:: The delivery 送达 of writs 令状 or summonses 传票 to the appropriate party 相关方；当事人.

[.my2]
诉讼文书送达: 将令状或传票送达给相关当事人的行为。

Settlement:: Parties to a lawsuit resolve 解决 their dispute 争议 without having a trial. Settlements often involve the payment of compensation 赔偿 by one party in at least partial satisfaction of 满足 the other party’s claims 诉求, but usually do not include the admission of fault 过错承认.

[.my2]
和解: 诉讼各方无需经过庭审即可解决争议的方式。和解通常涉及一方支付赔偿金，至少部分满足另一方的诉求，但一般不包含过错承认的内容。

Sequester:: To separate 使隔离；使分开. Sometimes juries 陪审团 are sequestered from outside influences 外界影响 during their deliberations 审议；评议.

[.my2]
隔离；隔离候审: 使隔离；使分开。在陪审团进行评议期间，有时会将其与外界隔离开来，避免受到外界干扰。

Small business case:: A special type of Chapter 11 case in which there is no creditors’ committee 债权人委员会 (or the creditors’ committee is deemed 被视为；被认为 inactive 不活跃的；不起作用的 by the court) and in which the debtor is subject to more oversight 监督；监管 by the U.S. trustee 美国破产管理人 than other Chapter 11 debtors. The Bankruptcy Code 破产法典 contains certain provisions 条款；规定 designed to reduce the time a small business debtor is in bankruptcy.

[.my2]
小型企业破产案件: 一种特殊类型的《美国破产法》第十一章破产案件，此类案件中不存在债权人委员会（或经法院认定债权人委员会处于不活跃状态），且相较于其他适用第十一章的债务人，小型企业债务人需接受美国破产管理人更严格的监管。《破产法典》中设有特定条款，旨在缩短小型企业债务人处于破产程序中的时间。

Statement of financial affairs:: A series of questions the debtor must answer in writing concerning 关于 sources of income 收入来源, transfers of property 财产转让, lawsuits by creditors, etc. (There is an official form a debtor must use.)

[.my2]
财务状况声明: 包含一系列问题的文件，债务人必须以书面形式作答，内容涉及收入来源、财产转让情况、债权人提起的诉讼等事项。（债务人必须使用专门的官方表格填写。）

Statement of intention:: A declaration 声明 made by a link:americanlaw101_ch07.html[Chapter 7] debtor concerning plans for dealing with consumer debts 消费债务 that are secured by property of the estate.

[.my2]
意向声明: 适用《美国破产法》第七章的债务人作出的声明，内容为其计划如何处理以破产财产作为担保的消费债务。

Standard of proof:: Degree of proof required. In criminal cases, prosecutors must prove a defendant’s guilt “beyond a reasonable doubt 排除合理怀疑.” The majority of civil lawsuits require proof “by a preponderance of the evidence 优势证据” (50 percent plus), but in some cases the standard is higher and requires “clear and convincing 清晰且有说服力的” proof.

[.my2]
证明标准: 案件审理所需达到的证明程度。在刑事案件中，检察官必须“排除合理怀疑”地证明被告有罪。大多数民事诉讼要求以“优势证据”（即证据的可信度超过50%）作为证明标准，但在部分案件中，证明标准要求更高，需达到“清晰且有说服力”的程度。

Statute:: A law passed by a legislature 立法机关.

[.my2]
成文法；法规: 由立法机关通过的法律条文。

Statute of limitations:: The time within which a lawsuit must be filed 提起诉讼 or a criminal prosecution begun. The deadline 截止期限 can vary, depending on 取决于 the type of civil case or the crime charged.

[.my2]
诉讼时效: 提起民事诉讼或启动刑事诉讼的法定期限。该截止期限会根据民事案件的类型或所指控罪名的不同而有所差异。

Sua sponte:: Latin, meaning “of its own will 出于自身意愿.” Often refers to a court taking an action in a case without being asked to do so by either side.

[.my2]
依职权（拉丁语）: 拉丁语词汇，意为“出于自身意愿”。通常指法院未经案件任何一方申请，主动对案件采取相关措施的行为。

Subordination:: The act or process by which a person’s rights or claims are ranked below 位列低于 those of others.

[.my2]
权利顺位让渡: 指个人所享有的权利或债权的顺位被排列在他人权利或债权之后的行为或过程。

Subpoena:: A command, issued under a court’s authority 授权；权威, to a witness 证人 to appear and give testimony 作证.

[.my2]
传票: 由法院依职权发出的命令，要求证人出庭作证。

Subpoena duces tecum:: A command to a witness to appear and produce 提交；出示 documents.

[.my2]
书证传票: 要求证人出庭并提交相关文件的命令。

Substance abuse treatment:: A special condition the court imposes 施加；规定 that requires an individual to undergo 接受 testing and treatment for abuse of illegal drugs 非法药物, prescription drugs 处方药, or alcohol. Treatment may include inpatient 住院的 or outpatient 门诊的 counseling 咨询 and detoxification 脱瘾治疗；戒毒治疗.

[.my2]
药物滥用治疗: 法院施加的一项特殊条件，要求相关个人接受针对非法药物、处方药或酒精滥用的检测与治疗。治疗内容可包括住院咨询、门诊咨询以及脱瘾治疗。

Substantial abuse:: The characterization 定性 of a bankruptcy case filed by an individual whose debts are primarily 主要地 consumer debts, where the court finds that the granting of relief 救济；救助 would be an abuse of link:americanlaw101_ch07.html[Chapter 7] because, for example, the debtor can pay its debts.

[.my2]
严重滥用破产程序: 对个人提起的破产案件所做的定性，此类案件中债务人的债务主要为消费债务，且法院经审理认定，批准破产救济属于对《美国破产法》第七章的滥用，例如债务人实际上有能力偿还债务却申请破产的情况。

Substantive consolidation:: Putting the assets and liabilities of two or more related debtors into a single pool 集合；总和 to pay creditors. (Courts are reluctant to 不情愿；勉强 allow substantive consolidation because the action must justify 证明……合理 not only the benefit that one set of creditors receives but also the harm that other creditors suffer as a result.)

[.my2]
实体合并: 将两个或多个关联债务人的资产与负债合并为一个整体，用以向债权人清偿债务。（法院通常不愿批准实体合并程序，因为该行为不仅要证明部分债权人会从中获益，还需证明此操作不会对其他债权人造成损害。）

Summary judgment:: A decision made on the basis of statements and evidence presented for the record without a trial. It is used when it is not necessary to resolve 解决 any factual disputes 事实争议 in the case. Summary judgment is granted when—on the undisputed facts 无争议事实 in the record—one party is entitled to 有权获得 judgment as a matter of law 依法.

[.my2]
简易判决: 无需经过庭审，法院根据已记录在案的陈述与证据直接作出的判决。该判决适用于案件不存在需要解决的事实争议的情形。当案件记录中的事实均无争议，且从法律层面而言一方当事人有权获得判决时，法院将作出简易判决。

Supervised release:: Term of supervision served after a person is released from prison. The court imposes supervised release during sentencing in addition to 除……之外 the sentence of imprisonment 监禁刑. Unlike parole 假释, supervised release does not replace a portion of the sentence of imprisonment but is in addition to the time spent in prison. U.S. probation officers supervise people on supervised release.

[.my2]
监督释放: 个人出狱后需接受的一段监管期。法院在量刑时，会在判处监禁刑之外附加监督释放的要求。与假释不同，监督释放并非替代部分监禁刑期，而是在监禁时间之外额外增加的监管措施。处于监督释放状态的人员由美国缓刑官负责监管。

=== T

Temporary restraining order:: Akin to 类似于；近似于 a preliminary injunction 初步禁令, it is a judge’s short-term 短期的 order forbidding 禁止；不准 certain actions until a full hearing 正式听证会 can be conducted. Often referred to as 被称为；简称为 a TRO.

[.my2]
临时限制令: 类似于初步禁令，是法官发布的短期命令，禁止特定行为直至正式听证会召开。通常简称为TRO。

Testimony:: Evidence presented orally 口头地 by witnesses during trials or before grand juries 大陪审团.

[.my2]
证词: 证人在庭审期间或大陪审团面前口头提供的证据。

Toll:: See statute of limitations 诉讼时效.

[.my2]
诉讼时效中止/中断: 参见“诉讼时效”。

Tort:: A civil, not criminal, wrong 不法行为. A negligent 过失的 or intentional 故意的 injury 损害；伤害 against a person or property, with the exception of 除……之外 breach of contract 违约.

[.my2]
侵权行为: 一种民事不法行为（而非刑事犯罪），指因过失或故意对他人人身或财产造成损害的行为，但不包括违约行为。

Transfer:: Any mode 方式；方法 or means by which a debtor disposes of 处置；处理 or parts with 放弃；出让 his or her property.

[.my2]
财产转让: 债务人处置或出让其财产的任何方式或手段。

Transcript:: A written, word-for-word 逐字逐句的 record of what was said, either in a proceeding such as a trial or during some other formal conversation, such as a hearing 听证会 or oral deposition 口头证词；宣誓证言.

[.my2]
庭审记录；书面记录: 对所说话语的逐字书面记录，既可以是庭审等法律程序中的记录，也可以是听证会、口头证词等其他正式谈话中的记录。

Trustee:: The representative 代表 of the bankruptcy estate who exercises 行使 statutory powers 法定权力, principally 主要地 for the benefit of 为……的利益 the unsecured creditors 无担保债权人, under the general supervision 总体监督 of the court and the direct supervision 直接监督 of the U.S. trustee or bankruptcy administrator 破产管理人. The trustee is a private individual 个人 or corporation 公司 appointed 任命；指定 in all link:americanlaw101_ch07.html[Chapter 7], Chapter 12, and Chapter 13 cases and some Chapter 11 cases. The trustee’s responsibilities include reviewing 审查；审核 the debtor’s petition and schedules and bringing actions against 对……提起诉讼 creditors or the debtor to recover 追回；收回 property of the bankruptcy estate. In link:americanlaw101_ch07.html[Chapter 7], the trustee liquidates 清算 property of the estate and makes distributions 分配 to creditors. Trustees in Chapter 12 and 13 have similar duties to a Chapter 7 trustee and the additional responsibilities of overseeing 监督；监管 the debtor’s plan, receiving payments from debtors, and disbursing 支付；发放 plan payments to creditors.

[.my2]
破产受托人: 破产财产的代表人，在法院的总体监督和美国破产管理人的直接监督下行使法定权力，主要为无担保债权人的利益服务。受托人可以是个人或公司，在所有《美国破产法》第七章、第十二章、第十三章案件以及部分第十一章案件中由法院指定。受托人的职责包括审查债务人的破产申请书和财产债务明细表、对债权人或债务人提起诉讼以追回破产财产。在第七章案件中，受托人负责清算破产财产并向债权人分配清偿款。第十二章和第十三章的受托人与第七章受托人的职责类似，同时还需承担监督债务人的偿债计划、接收债务人的付款以及向债权人发放计划约定款项的额外职责。

Typing service:: A business not authorized to 无权；未被授权 practice law 从事法律业务 that prepares bankruptcy petitions.

[.my2]
文书录入服务机构: 无权从事法律业务的机构，其业务范围为撰写破产申请书。

=== U

U.S. attorney:: A lawyer appointed by the President 总统 in each judicial district 司法管辖区 to prosecute 提起公诉 and defend 辩护 cases for the federal government. The U.S. Attorney employs 雇佣；聘用 a staff of Assistant U.S. Attorneys 联邦助理检察官 who appear as 作为……出庭 the government’s attorneys in individual cases.

[.my2]
联邦检察官: 由总统在每个司法管辖区任命的律师，负责代表联邦政府提起公诉和参与辩护。联邦检察官手下有一批联邦助理检察官，这些助理检察官会作为政府律师在具体案件中出庭。

U.S. trustee:: An officer 官员 of the U.S. Department of Justice 美国司法部 responsible for supervising 监督 the administration 管理；执行 of bankruptcy cases, estates, and trustees; monitoring 监控；监督 plans and disclosure statements 披露声明; monitoring creditors’ committees; monitoring fee applications 费用申请; and performing 履行 other statutory duties 法定义务.

[.my2]
美国破产管理人: 美国司法部的官员，负责监督破产案件、破产财产及受托人的管理工作，监控偿债计划和披露声明的执行情况，监督债权人委员会的运作，审核费用申请，并履行其他法定义务。

Undersecured claim:: A debt secured by property that is worth less than the amount of the debt.

[.my2]
担保不足债权: 以财产作为担保，但该财产价值低于所欠债务金额的债权。

Undue hardship:: The most widely used test 标准；检验方法 for evaluating 评估；判定 undue hardship in the dischargeability 可免除性 of a student loan 学生贷款 includes three conditions: (1) the debtor cannot maintain—based on current income and expenses—a minimal standard of living 最低生活标准 if forced to repay the loans; (2) there are indications 迹象；表明 that the state of affairs 状况；情形 is likely to persist 持续 for a significant portion of 很大一部分 the repayment period 还款期; and (3) the debtor made good-faith 善意的 efforts to repay the loans.

[.my2]
过度困难: 评估学生贷款是否因“过度困难”而可免除的最常用标准包含三个条件：（1）基于当前的收入和支出情况，若债务人被迫偿还贷款，则无法维持最低生活标准；（2）有迹象表明，该经济状况可能会在还款期的很大一部分时间内持续存在；（3）债务人已尽善意努力尝试偿还贷款。

Unlawful detainer action:: A lawsuit brought by a landlord 房东 against a tenant 租户 to evict 驱逐；赶出 the tenant from rental property—usually for nonpayment of rent 拖欠租金.

[.my2]
非法滞留诉讼: 房东对租户提起的诉讼，目的是将租户从租赁财产中驱逐，通常是因为租户拖欠租金。

Unliquidated claim:: A claim for which a specific value 具体价值 has not been determined 确定；判定.

[.my2]
未确定金额债权: 尚未确定具体金额的债权。

Unscheduled debt:: A debt that should have been listed 列明；列入 by the debtor in the schedules filed with the court but was not. (Depending on the circumstances 视情况而定, an unscheduled debt may or may not be discharged 免除.)

[.my2]
未列入明细表债务: 债务人本应在提交给法院的财产债务明细表中列明，但未予列明的债务。（视具体情况而定，此类未列入的债务可能会被免除，也可能无法被免除。）

Unsecured claim:: A claim or debt for which a creditor holds no special assurance of payment 付款保证, such as a mortgage or lien; a debt for which credit was extended 提供信贷 based solely upon 仅基于 the creditor’s assessment 评估 of the debtor’s future ability to pay.

[.my2]
无担保债权: 债权人未持有抵押、留置权等特殊付款保证的债权或债务；此类债务的信贷发放仅基于债权人对债务人未来还款能力的评估。

Uphold:: The appellate court 上诉法院 agrees with 同意；认可 the lower court decision and allows it to stand 维持原判. See affirmed 维持原判.

[.my2]
维持原判: 上诉法院认可下级法院的判决，并维持该判决的效力。参见“维持原判（affirmed）”。

=== V

Venue:: The geographic area 地理区域 in which a court has jurisdiction 管辖权. A change of venue 变更审判地 is a change or transfer of a case from one judicial district to another.

[.my2]
审判地: 法院享有管辖权的地理区域。变更审判地指将案件从一个司法管辖区转移至另一个司法管辖区。

Verdict:: The decision of a trial jury 陪审团 or a judge that determines the guilt 有罪 or innocence 无罪 of a criminal defendant, or that determines the final outcome 最终结果 of a civil case.

[.my2]
裁决； verdict: 初审陪审团或法官作出的决定，用于判定刑事被告是否有罪，或确定民事诉讼的最终结果。

Voir dire:: French, meaning “say the truth 说实话.” Jury selection process 陪审团遴选程序 of questioning 询问；盘问 prospective jurors 潜在陪审员, to ascertain 确定；查明 their qualifications 资格 and determine any basis for challenge 回避理由.

[.my2]
陪审团遴选询问（法语）: 法语词汇，意为“说实话”。指陪审团遴选过程中，通过询问潜在陪审员以确定其是否具备任职资格，并查明是否存在回避理由的程序。

Voluntary transfer:: A transfer of a debtor’s property with the debtor’s consent 同意；许可.

[.my2]
自愿转让: 经债务人同意后进行的财产转让。

=== W

Wage garnishment:: A nonbankruptcy legal proceeding 非破产法律程序 whereby a plaintiff 原告 or creditor seeks to subject to 使……服从于 his or her claim the future wages 未来工资 of a debtor. In other words, the creditor seeks to have part of the debtor’s future wages paid to the creditor for a debt owed to the creditor.

[.my2]
工资扣押: 一种非破产法律程序，原告或债权人通过该程序要求将债务人的未来工资用于清偿其债权。换句话说，债权人试图让债务人将未来工资的一部分支付给自己，以偿还所欠债务。

Warrant:: Court authorization 授权；许可, most often for law enforcement officers 执法人员, to conduct a search 搜查 or make an arrest 逮捕.

[.my2]
搜查令；逮捕令: 法院颁发的授权文件，最常授予执法人员，允许其执行搜查或逮捕行为。

Witness:: A person called upon 传唤；要求 by either side in a lawsuit to give testimony 作证 before the court or jury.

[.my2]
证人: 被诉讼任何一方传唤，需在法院或陪审团面前作证的人。

Writ:: A written court order directing 指令；命令 a person to take, or refrain from taking 避免采取, a certain act 特定行为.

[.my2]
令状: 法院签发的书面命令，指令某人采取某一行为或避免采取某一行为。

Writ of certiorari:: An order issued by the U.S. Supreme Court 美国最高法院 directing the lower court to transmit 传送；提交 records 案卷 for a case which it will hear on appeal 上诉审理.

[.my2]
调卷令: 美国最高法院签发的命令，指令下级法院将其即将进行上诉审理的案件案卷提交最高法院。

Source: _Glossary_, UNITED STATES COURTS, link:http://www.uscourts.gov/Common/Glossary.aspx[http://www.uscourts.gov/Common/Glossary.aspx] (last visited September 12, 2014).

[.my2]

APPENDIX C +
GLOSSARY OF LEGAL TERMS IN PLAIN ENGLISH

=== A

Acquittal:: A jury verdict that a criminal defendant is not guilty, or the finding of a judge that the evidence is insufficient to support a conviction.
Active judge:: A judge in the full-time service of the court. Compare to senior judge.
Administrative Office of the United States Courts (AO):: The federal agency responsible for collecting court statistics, administering the federal courts’ budget, and performing many other administrative and programmatic functions, under the direction and supervision of the Judicial Conference of the United States.
Admissible:: A term used to describe evidence that may be considered by a jury or judge in civil and criminal cases.
Adversary proceeding:: A lawsuit arising in or related to a bankruptcy case that begins by filing a complaint with the court; that is, a “trial” that takes place within the context of a bankruptcy case.
Affidavit:: A written or printed statement made under oath.
Affirmed:: In the practice of the court of appeals, it means that the court of appeals has concluded that the lower court decision is correct and will stand as rendered by the lower court.
Alternate juror:: A juror selected in the same manner as a regular juror who hears all the evidence but does not help decide the case unless called on to replace a regular juror.
Alternative dispute resolution (ADR):: A procedure for settling a dispute outside the courtroom. Most forms of ADR are not binding, and involve referral of the case to a neutral party such as an arbitrator or mediator.
Amicus curiae:: Latin for “friend of the court.” It is advice formally offered to the court in a brief filed by an entity interested in, but not a party to, the case.
Answer:: The formal written statement by a defendant in a civil case that responds to a complaint, articulating the grounds for defense.
Appeal:: A request made after a trial by a party that has lost on one or more issues that a higher court review the decision to determine if it was correct. To make such a request is “to appeal” or “to take an appeal.” One who appeals is called the “appellant”; the other party is the “appellee.”
Appellant:: The party who appeals a district court’s decision, usually seeking reversal of that decision.
Appellate:: About appeals; an appellate court has the power to review the judgment of a lower court (trial court) or tribunal. For example, the U.S. circuit courts of appeals review the decisions of the U.S. district courts.
Appellee:: The party who opposes an appellant’s appeal, and who seeks to persuade the appeals court to affirm the district court’s decision.
Arraignment:: A proceeding in which a criminal defendant is brought into court, told of the charges in an indictment or information, and asked to plead guilty or not guilty.
Article III judge:: A federal judge who is appointed for life, during “good behavior,” under Article III of the Constitution. Article III judges are nominated by the President and confirmed by the Senate.
Assets:: Property of all kinds, including real and personal, tangible and intangible.
Assume:: An agreement to continue performing duties under a contract or lease.
Automatic stay:: An injunction that automatically stops lawsuits, foreclosures, garnishments, and most collection activities against the debtor the moment a bankruptcy petition is filed.

=== B

Bail:: The release, prior to trial, of a person accused of a crime, under specified conditions designed to assure that person’s appearance in court when required. Also can refer to the amount of bond money posted as a financial condition of pretrial release.
Bankruptcy:: A legal procedure for dealing with debt problems of individuals and businesses; specifically, a case filed under one of the chapters of Title 11 of the United States Code (the Bankruptcy Code).
Bankruptcy administrator:: An officer of the Judiciary serving in the judicial districts of Alabama and North Carolina who, like the United States trustee, is responsible for supervising the administration of bankruptcy cases, estates, and trustees; monitoring plans and disclosure statements; monitoring creditors’ committees; monitoring fee applications; and performing other statutory duties.
Bankruptcy code:: The informal name for Title 11 of the United States Code (11 U.S.C. §§ 101-1330), the federal bankruptcy law.
Bankruptcy court:: The bankruptcy judges in regular active service in each district; a unit of the district court.
Bankruptcy estate:: All interests of the debtor in property at the time of the bankruptcy filing. The estate technically becomes the temporary legal owner of all of the debtor’s property.
Bankruptcy judge:: A judicial officer of the United States district court who is the court official with decision-making power over federal bankruptcy cases.
Bankruptcy petition:: A formal request for the protection of the federal bankruptcy laws. (There is an official form for bankruptcy petitions.)
Bankruptcy trustee:: A private individual or corporation appointed in all link:americanlaw101_ch07.html[Chapter 7] and Chapter 13 cases to represent the interests of the bankruptcy estate and the debtor’s creditors.
Bench trial:: A trial without a jury, in which the judge serves as the fact-finder.
Brief:: A written statement submitted in a trial or appellate proceeding that explains one side’s legal and factual arguments.
Burden of proof:: The duty to prove disputed facts. In civil cases, a plaintiff generally has the burden of proving his or her case. In criminal cases, the government has the burden of proving the defendant’s guilt. (See standard of proof.)
Business bankruptcy:: A bankruptcy case in which the debtor is a business or an individual involved in business and the debts are for business purposes.

=== C

Capital offense:: A crime punishable by death.
Case file:: A complete collection of every document filed in court in a case.
Case law:: The law as established in previous court decisions. A synonym for legal precedent. Akin to common law, which springs from tradition and judicial decisions.
Caseload:: The number of cases handled by a judge or a court.
Cause of action:: A legal claim.
Chambers:: The offices of a judge and his or her staff.
Chapter 7:: The chapter of the Bankruptcy Code providing for “liquidation,” that is, the sale of a debtor’s nonexempt property and the distribution of the proceeds to creditors. In order to be eligible for link:americanlaw101_ch07.html[Chapter 7], the debtor must satisfy a “means test.” The court will evaluate the debtor’s income and expenses to determine if the debtor may proceed under link:americanlaw101_ch07.html[Chapter 7].
Chapter 7 trustee:: A person appointed in a link:americanlaw101_ch07.html[Chapter 7] case to represent the interests of the bankruptcy estate and the creditors. The trustee’s responsibilities include reviewing the debtor’s petition and schedules, liquidating the property of the estate, and making distributions to creditors. The trustee may also bring actions against creditors or the debtor to recover property of the bankruptcy estate.
Chapter 9:: The chapter of the Bankruptcy Code providing for reorganization of municipalities (which includes cities and towns, as well as villages, counties, taxing districts, municipal utilities, and school districts).
Chapter 11:: A reorganization bankruptcy, usually involving a corporation or partnership. A Chapter 11 debtor usually proposes a plan of reorganization to keep its business alive and pay creditors over time. Individuals or people in business can also seek relief in Chapter 11.
Chapter 12:: The chapter of the Bankruptcy Code providing for adjustment of debts of a “family farmer” or “family fisherman,” as the terms are defined in the Bankruptcy Code.
Chapter 13:: The chapter of the Bankruptcy Code providing for the adjustment of debts of an individual with regular income, often referred to as a “wage-earner” plan. Chapter 13 allows a debtor to keep property and use his or her disposable income to pay debts over time, usually three to five years.
Chapter 13 trustee:: A person appointed to administer a Chapter 13 case. A Chapter 13 trustee’s responsibilities are similar to those of a link:americanlaw101_ch07.html[Chapter 7] trustee; however, a Chapter 13 trustee has the additional responsibilities of overseeing the debtor’s plan, receiving payments from debtors, and disbursing plan payments to creditors.
Chapter 15:: The chapter of the Bankruptcy Code dealing with cases of cross-border insolvency.
Chief judge:: The judge who has primary responsibility for the administration of a court; chief judges are determined by seniority.
Claim:: A creditor’s assertion of a right to payment from a debtor or the debtor’s property.
Class action:: A lawsuit in which one or more members of a large group, or class, of individuals or other entities sue on behalf of the entire class. The district court must find that the claims of the class members contain questions of law or fact in common before the lawsuit can proceed as a class action.
Clerk of court:: The court officer who oversees administrative functions, especially managing the flow of cases through the court. The clerk’s office is often called a court’s central nervous system.
Collateral:: Property that is promised as security for the satisfaction of a debt.
Common law:: The legal system that originated in England and is now in use in the United States, which relies on the articulation of legal principles in a historical succession of judicial decisions. Common law principles can be changed by legislation.
Community service:: A special condition the court imposes that requires an individual to work—without pay—for a civic or nonprofit organization.
Complaint:: A written statement that begins a civil lawsuit, in which the plaintiff details the claims against the defendant.
Concurrent sentence:: Prison terms for two or more offenses to be served at the same time, rather than one after the other. Example: Two five-year sentences and one three-year sentence, if served concurrently, result in a maximum of five years behind bars.
Confirmation:: Approval of a plan of reorganization by a bankruptcy judge.
Consecutive sentence:: Prison terms for two or more offenses to be served one after the other. Example: Two five-year sentences and one three-year sentence, if served consecutively, result in a maximum of 13 years behind bars.
Consumer bankruptcy:: A bankruptcy case filed to reduce or eliminate debts that are primarily consumer debts.
Consumer debts:: Debts incurred for personal, as opposed to business, needs.
Contingent claim:: A claim that may be owed by the debtor under certain circumstances (e.g., where the debtor is a cosigner on another person’s loan and that person fails to pay).
Contract:: An agreement between two or more people that creates an obligation to do or not to do a particular thing.
Conviction:: A judgment of guilt against a criminal defendant.
Counsel:: Legal advice; a term also used to refer to the lawyers in a case.
Court:: Government entity authorized to resolve legal disputes. Judges sometimes use “court” to refer to themselves in the third person, as in “the court has read the briefs.”
Court reporter:: A person who makes a word-for-word record of what is said in court, generally by using a stenographic machine, shorthand, or audio recording, and then produces a transcript of the proceedings upon request.
Count:: An allegation in an indictment or information, charging a defendant with a crime. An indictment or information may contain allegations that the defendant committed more than one crime. Each allegation is referred to as a count.
Creditor:: A person to whom or business to which the debtor owes money or that claims to be owed money by the debtor.
Credit counseling:: Generally refers to two events in individual bankruptcy cases: (1) the “individual or group briefing” from a nonprofit budget and credit counseling agency that individual debtors must attend prior to filing under any chapter of the Bankruptcy Code; and (2) the “instructional course in personal financial management” in link:americanlaw101_ch07.html[Chapters 7] and 13 that an individual debtor must complete before a discharge is entered. There are exceptions to both requirements for certain categories of debtors, exigent circumstances, or if the U.S. trustee or bankruptcy administrator have determined that there are insufficient approved credit counseling agencies available to provide the necessary counseling.

=== D

Damages:: Money that a defendant pays a plaintiff in a civil case if the plaintiff has won. Damages may be compensatory (for loss or injury) or punitive (to punish and deter future misconduct).
Debtor:: A person who has filed a petition for relief under the Bankruptcy Code.
Defendant:: An individual (or business) against whom a lawsuit is filed.
Debtor’s plan:: A debtor’s detailed description of how the debtor proposes to pay creditors’ claims over a fixed period of time.
Declaratory judgment:: A judge’s statement about someone’s rights. For example, a plaintiff may seek a declaratory judgment that a particular statute, as written, violates some constitutional right.
De facto:: Latin, meaning “in fact” or “actually.” Something that exists in fact but not as a matter of law.
Default judgment:: A judgment awarding a plaintiff the relief sought in the complaint because the defendant has failed to appear in court or otherwise respond to the complaint.
Defendant:: In a civil case, the person or organization against whom the plaintiff brings suit; in a criminal case, the person accused of the crime.
De jure:: Latin, meaning “in law.” Something that exists by operation of law.
De novo:: Latin, meaning “anew.” A trial de novo is a completely new trial. Appellate review de novo implies no deference to the trial judge’s ruling.
Deposition:: An oral statement made before an officer who is authorized by law to administer oaths. Such statements are often taken to examine potential witnesses, to obtain discovery, or to be used later in trial. See discovery.
Discharge:: A release of a debtor from personal liability for certain dischargeable debts. Notable exceptions to dischargeability are taxes and student loans. A discharge releases a debtor from personal liability for certain debts known as dischargeable debts and prevents the creditors owed those debts from taking any action against the debtor or the debtor’s property to collect the debts. The discharge also prohibits creditors from communicating with the debtor regarding the debt, including through telephone calls, letters, and personal contact.
Dischargeable debt:: A debt for which the Bankruptcy Code allows the debtor’s personal liability to be eliminated.
Disclosure statement:: A written document prepared by the Chapter 11 debtor or other plan proponent that is designed to provide “adequate information” to creditors to enable them to evaluate the Chapter 11 plan of reorganization.
Discovery:: Procedures used to obtain disclosure of evidence before trial.
Dismissal with prejudice:: Court action that prevents an identical lawsuit from being filed later.
Dismissal without prejudice:: Court action that allows the later filing.
Disposable income:: Income not reasonably necessary for the maintenance or support of the debtor or dependents. If the debtor operates a business, disposable income is defined as those amounts over and above what is necessary for the payment of ordinary operating expenses.
Docket:: A log containing the complete history of each case in the form of brief chronological entries summarizing the court proceedings.
Due process:: In criminal law, the constitutional guarantee that a defendant will receive a fair and impartial trial. In civil law, the legal rights of someone who confronts an adverse action threatening liberty or property.

=== E

En banc:: French, meaning “on the bench.” All judges of an appellate court sitting together to hear a case, as opposed to the routine disposition by panels of three judges. In the Ninth Circuit, an en banc panel consists of 11 randomly selected judges.
Equitable:: Pertaining to civil suits in “equity” rather than in “law.” In English legal history, the courts of “law” could order the payment of damages and could afford no other remedy (see damages). A separate court of “equity” could order someone to do something or to cease to do something (e.g., injunction). In American jurisprudence, the federal courts have both legal and equitable power, but the distinction is still an important one. For example, a trial by jury is normally available in “law” cases but not in “equity” cases.
Equity:: The value of a debtor’s interest in property that remains after liens and other creditors’ interests are considered. (Example: If a house valued at $60,000 is subject to a $30,000 mortgage, there is $30,000 of equity.)
Evidence:: Information presented in testimony or in documents that is used to persuade the fact finder (judge or jury) to decide the case in favor of one side or the other.
Exclusionary rule:: Doctrine that says evidence obtained in violation of a criminal defendant’s constitutional or statutory rights is not admissible at trial.
Exculpatory evidence:: Evidence indicating that a defendant did not commit the crime.
Executory contracts:: Contracts or leases under which both parties to the agreement have duties remaining to be performed. If a contract or lease is executory, a debtor may assume it (keep the contract) or reject it (terminate the contract).
Exempt assets:: Property that a debtor is allowed to retain, free from the claims of creditors who do not have liens on the property.
Exemptions, exempt property:: Certain property owned by an individual debtor that the Bankruptcy Code or applicable state law permits the debtor to keep from unsecured creditors. For example, in some states the debtor may be able to exempt all or a portion of the equity in the debtor’s primary residence (homestead exemption), or some or all “tools of the trade” used by the debtor to make a living (i.e., auto tools for an auto mechanic or dental tools for a dentist). The availability and amount of property the debtor may exempt depends on the state the debtor lives in.
Ex parte:: A proceeding brought before a court by one party only, without notice to or challenge by the other side.

=== F

Face sheet filing:: A bankruptcy case filed either without schedules or with incomplete schedules listing few creditors and debts. (Face sheet filings are often made to delay an eviction or foreclosure.)
Family farmer:: An individual, individual and spouse, corporation, or partnership engaged in a farming operation that meets certain debt limits and other statutory criteria for filing a petition under Chapter 12.
Federal public defender:: An attorney employed by the federal courts on a full-time basis to provide legal defense to defendants who are unable to afford counsel. The judiciary administers the federal defender program pursuant to the Criminal Justice Act.
Federal public defender organization:: As provided for in the Criminal Justice Act, an organization established within a federal judicial circuit to represent criminal defendants who cannot afford an adequate defense. Each organization is supervised by a federal public defender appointed by the court of appeals for the circuit.
Federal question jurisdiction:: Jurisdiction given to federal courts in cases involving the interpretation and application of the U.S. Constitution, acts of Congress, and treaties.
Felony:: A serious crime, usually punishable by at least one year in prison.
File:: To place a paper in the official custody of the clerk of court to enter into the files or records of a case.
Fraudulent transfer:: A transfer of a debtor’s property made with intent to defraud or for which the debtor receives less than the transferred property’s value.
Fresh start:: The characterization of a debtor’s status after bankruptcy (i.e., free of most debts). Giving debtors a fresh start is one purpose of the Bankruptcy Code.

=== G

Grand jury:: A body of 16 to 23 citizens who listen to evidence of criminal allegations, which is presented by the prosecutors, and determine whether there is probable cause to believe an individual committed an offense. See also indictment and U.S. attorney.

=== H

Habeas corpus:: Latin, meaning “you have the body.” A writ of habeas corpus generally is a judicial order forcing law enforcement authorities to produce a prisoner they are holding, and to justify the prisoner’s continued confinement. Federal judges receive petitions for a writ of habeas corpus from state prison inmates who say their state prosecutions violated federally protected rights in some way.
Hearsay:: Evidence presented by a witness who did not see or hear the incident in question but heard about it from someone else. With some exceptions, hearsay generally is not admissible as evidence at trial.
Home confinement:: A special condition the court imposes that requires an individual to remain at home except for certain approved activities, such as work and medical appointments. Home confinement may include the use of electronic monitoring equipment—a transmitter attached to the wrist or the ankle—to help ensure that the person stays at home as required.

=== I

Impeachment:: 1. The process of calling a witness’s testimony into doubt (e.g., if the attorney can show that the witness may have fabricated portions of his testimony, the witness is said to be “impeached”); 2. The constitutional process whereby the House of Representatives may “impeach” (accuse of misconduct) high officers of the federal government, who are then tried by the Senate.
In camera:: Latin, meaning “in a judge’s chambers.” Often means outside the presence of a jury and the public; in private.
Inculpatory evidence:: Evidence indicating that a defendant did commit the crime.
Indictment:: The formal charge issued by a grand jury stating that there is enough evidence that the defendant committed the crime to justify having a trial; it is used primarily for felonies. See also information.
In forma pauperis:: Latin, meaning “in the manner of a pauper.” Permission given by the court to a person to file a case without payment of the required court fees because the person cannot pay them.
Information:: A formal accusation by a government attorney that the defendant committed a misdemeanor. See also indictment.
Injunction:: A court order preventing one or more named parties from taking some action. A preliminary injunction often is issued to allow fact-finding, so a judge can determine whether a permanent injunction is justified.
Insider (of corporate debtor):: A director, officer, or person in control of the debtor; a partnership in which the debtor is a general partner; a general partner of the debtor; or a relative of a general partner, director, officer, or person in control of the debtor.
Insider (of individual debtor):: Any relative of the debtor or of a general partner of the debtor; partnership in which the debtor is a general partner; general partner of the debtor; or corporation of which the debtor is a director, officer, or person in control.
Interrogatories:: A form of discovery consisting of written questions to be answered in writing and under oath.
Issue:: 1. The disputed point between parties in a lawsuit; 2. To send out officially, as in a court issuing an order.

=== J

Joint administration:: A court-approved mechanism under which two or more cases can be administered together. (Assuming no conflicts of interest, these separate businesses or individuals can pool their resources, hire the same professionals, etc.)
Joint petition:: One bankruptcy petition filed by a husband and wife together.
Judge:: An official of the Judicial branch with the authority to decide lawsuits brought before courts. Used generically, the term judge may also refer to all judicial officers, including Supreme Court justices.
Judgeship:: The position of judge. By statute, Congress authorizes the number of judgeships for each district and appellate court.
Judgment:: The official decision of a court finally resolving the dispute between the parties to the lawsuit.
Judicial Conference of the United States:: The policy-making entity for the federal court system. A 27-judge body whose presiding officer is the Chief Justice of the United States.
Jurisdiction:: The legal authority of a court to hear and decide a certain type of case. It also is used as a synonym for venue, meaning the geographic area over which the court has territorial jurisdiction to decide cases.
Jurisprudence:: The study of law and the structure of the legal system.
Jury:: The group of persons selected to hear the evidence in a trial and render a verdict on matters of fact. See also grand jury.
Jury instructions:: A judge’s directions to the jury before it begins deliberations regarding the factual questions it must answer and the legal rules that it must apply.

=== L

Lawsuit:: A legal action started by a plaintiff against a defendant based on a complaint that the defendant failed to perform a legal duty, which resulted in harm to the plaintiff.
Lien:: A charge on specific property that is designed to secure payment of a debt or performance of an obligation. A debtor may still be responsible for a lien after a discharge.
Litigation:: A case, controversy, or lawsuit. Participants (plaintiffs and defendants) in lawsuits are called litigants.
Liquidation:: The sale of a debtor’s property with the proceeds to be used for the benefit of creditors.
Liquidated claim:: A creditor’s claim for a fixed amount of money.

=== M

Magistrate judge:: A judicial officer of a district court who conducts initial proceedings in criminal cases, decides criminal misdemeanor cases, conducts many pretrial civil and criminal matters on behalf of district judges, and decides civil cases with the consent of the parties.
Means test:: Section 707(b)(2) of the Bankruptcy Code applies a “means test” to determine whether an individual debtor’s link:americanlaw101_ch07.html[Chapter 7] filing is presumed to be an abuse of the Bankruptcy Code requiring dismissal or conversion of the case (generally to Chapter 13). Abuse is presumed if the debtor’s aggregate current monthly income (see definition above) over 5 years, net of certain statutorily allowed expenses is more than (i) $10,000, or (ii) 25 percent of the debtor’s nonpriority unsecured debt, as long as that amount is at least $6,000. The debtor may rebut a presumption of abuse only by a showing of special circumstances that justify additional expenses or adjustments of current monthly income.
Mental health treatment:: Special condition the court imposes to require an individual to undergo evaluation and treatment for a mental disorder. Treatment may include psychiatric, psychological, and sex offense–specific evaluations, inpatient or outpatient counseling, and medication.
Misdemeanor:: An offense punishable by one year of imprisonment or less. See also felony.
Mistrial:: An invalid trial, caused by fundamental error. When a mistrial is declared, the trial must start again with the selection of a new jury.
Moot:: Not subject to a court ruling because the controversy has not actually arisen, or has ended.
Motion:: A request by a litigant to a judge for a decision on an issue relating to the case.
Motion to lift the automatic stay:: A request by a creditor to allow the creditor to take action against the debtor or the debtor’s property that would otherwise be prohibited by the automatic stay.
Motion in limine:: A pretrial motion requesting the court to prohibit the other side from presenting, or even referring to, evidence on matters said to be so highly prejudicial that no steps taken by the judge can prevent the jury from being unduly influenced.

=== N

No-asset case:: A link:americanlaw101_ch07.html[Chapter 7] case in which there are no assets available to satisfy any portion of the creditors’ unsecured claims.
Nolo contendere:: Latin, meaning “no contest.” A plea of nolo contendere has the same effect as a plea of guilty, as far as the criminal sentence is concerned, but may not be considered as an admission of guilt for any other purpose.
Nondischargeable debt:: A debt that cannot be eliminated in bankruptcy. Examples include a home mortgage, debts for alimony or child support, certain taxes, debts for most government-funded or guaranteed educational loans or benefit overpayments, debts arising from death or personal injury caused by driving while intoxicated or under the influence of drugs, and debts for restitution or a criminal fine included in a sentence on the debtor’s conviction of a crime. Some debts, such as debts for money or property obtained by false pretenses and debts for fraud or defalcation while acting in a fiduciary capacity, may be declared nondischargeable only if a creditor timely files and prevails in a nondischargeability action.
Nonexempt assets:: Property of a debtor that can be liquidated to satisfy claims of creditors.

=== O

Objection to dischargeability:: A trustee’s or creditor’s objection to the debtor being released from personal liability for certain dischargeable debts. Common reasons include allegations that the debt to be discharged was incurred by false pretenses or that debt arose because of the debtor’s fraud while acting as a fiduciary.
Objection to exemptions:: A trustee’s or creditor’s objection to the debtor’s attempt to claim certain property as exempt from liquidation by the trustee to creditors.
Opinion:: A judge’s written explanation of the decision of the court. Because a case may be heard by three or more judges in the court of appeals, the opinion in appellate decisions can take several forms. If all the judges completely agree on the result, one judge will write the opinion for all. If all the judges do not agree, the formal decision will be based upon the view of the majority, and one member of the majority will write the opinion. The judges who did not agree with the majority may write separately in dissenting or concurring opinions to present their views. A dissenting opinion disagrees with the majority opinion because of the reasoning and/or the principles of law the majority used to decide the case. A concurring opinion agrees with the decision of the majority opinion, but offers further comment or clarification or even an entirely different reason for reaching the same result. Only the majority opinion can serve as binding precedent in future cases. See also precedent.
Oral argument:: An opportunity for lawyers to summarize their position before the court and also to answer the judges’ questions.

=== P

Panel:: 1. In appellate cases, a group of judges (usually three) assigned to decide the case; 2. In the jury selection process, the group of potential jurors; 3. The list of attorneys who are both available and qualified to serve as court-appointed counsel for criminal defendants who cannot afford their own counsel.
Parole:: The release of a prison inmate—granted by the U.S. Parole Commission—after the inmate has completed part of his or her sentence in a federal prison. When the parolee is released to the community, he or she is placed under the supervision of a U.S. probation officer. +
The Sentencing Reform Act of 1984 abolished parole in favor of a determinate sentencing system in which the sentence is set by sentencing guidelines. Now, without the option of parole, the term of imprisonment the court imposes is the actual time the person spends in prison.
Party in interest:: A party who has standing to be heard by the court in a matter to be decided in the bankruptcy case. The debtor, U.S. trustee or bankruptcy administrator, case trustee, and creditors are parties in interest for most matters.
Petition preparer:: A business not authorized to practice law that prepares bankruptcy petitions.
Per curiam:: Latin, meaning “for the court.” In appellate courts, often refers to an unsigned opinion.
Peremptory challenge:: A district court may grant each side in a civil or criminal trial the right to exclude a certain number of prospective jurors without cause or giving a reason.
Petit jury (or trial jury):: A group of citizens who hear the evidence presented by both sides at trial and determine the facts in dispute. Federal criminal juries consist of 12 persons. Federal civil juries consist of at least six persons.
Petition:: The document that initiates the filing of a bankruptcy proceeding, setting forth basic information regarding the debtor, including name, address, chapter under which the case is filed, and estimated amount of assets and liabilities.
Petty offense:: A federal misdemeanor punishable by six months or less in prison.
Plaintiff:: A person or business that files a formal complaint with the court.
Plan:: A debtor’s detailed description of how the debtor proposes to pay creditors’ claims over a fixed period of time.
Plea:: In a criminal case, the defendant’s statement pleading “guilty” or “not guilty” in answer to the charges. See also nolo contendere.
Pleadings:: Written statements filed with the court that describe a party’s legal or factual assertions about the case.
Postpetition transfer:: A transfer of the debtor’s property made after the commencement of the case.
Prebankruptcy planning:: The arrangement (or rearrangement) of a debtor’s property to allow the debtor to take maximum advantage of exemptions. (Prebankruptcy planning typically includes converting nonexempt assets into exempt assets.)
Precedent:: A court decision in an earlier case with facts and legal issues similar to a dispute currently before a court. Judges will generally “follow precedent,” meaning that they use the principles established in earlier cases to decide new cases that have similar facts and raise similar legal issues. A judge will disregard precedent if a party can show that the earlier case was wrongly decided, or that it differed in some significant way from the current case.
Preferential debt payment:: A debt payment made to a creditor in the 90-day period before a debtor files bankruptcy (or within one year if the creditor was an insider) that gives the creditor more than the creditor would receive in the debtor’s link:americanlaw101_ch07.html[Chapter 7] case.
Presentence report:: A report prepared by a court’s probation officer, after a person has been convicted of an offense, summarizing for the court the background information needed to determine the appropriate sentence.
Pretrial conference:: A meeting of the judge and lawyers to plan the trial, to discuss which matters should be presented to the jury, to review proposed evidence and witnesses, and to set a trial schedule. Typically, the judge and the parties also discuss the possibility of settlement of the case.
Pretrial services:: A function of the federal courts that takes place at the very start of the criminal justice process—after a person has been arrested and charged with a federal crime and before he or she goes to trial. Pretrial services officers focus on investigating the backgrounds of these persons to help the court determine whether to release or detain them while they await trial. The decision is based on whether these individuals are likely to flee or pose a threat to the community. If the court orders release, a pretrial services officer supervises the person in the community until he or she returns to court.
Priority:: The Bankruptcy Code’s statutory ranking of unsecured claims that determines the order in which unsecured claims will be paid if there is not enough money to pay all unsecured claims in full.
Priority claim:: An unsecured claim that is entitled to be paid ahead of other unsecured claims that are not entitled to priority status. Priority refers to the order in which these unsecured claims are to be paid.
Probation:: Sentencing option in the federal courts. With probation, instead of sending an individual to prison, the court releases the person to the community and orders him or her to complete a period of supervision monitored by a U.S. probation officer and to abide by certain conditions.
Probation officer:: Officers of the probation office of a court. Probation officer duties include conducting presentence investigations, preparing presentence reports on convicted defendants, and supervising released defendants.
Procedure:: The rules for conducting a lawsuit; there are rules of civil procedure, criminal procedure, evidence, bankruptcy, and appellate procedure.
Proof of claim:: A written statement describing the reason a debtor owes a creditor money, which typically sets forth the amount of money owed. (There is an official form for this purpose.)
Pro per:: A slang expression sometimes used to refer to a pro se litigant. It is a corruption of the Latin phrase “in propria persona.”
Property of the estate:: All legal or equitable interests of the debtor in property as of the commencement of the case.
Pro se:: Representing oneself. Serving as one’s own lawyer.
Prosecute:: To charge someone with a crime. A prosecutor tries a criminal case on behalf of the government.
Pro tem:: Temporary.

=== R

Reaffirmation agreement:: An agreement by a debtor to continue paying a dischargeable debt after the bankruptcy, usually to keep collateral or mortgaged property that would otherwise be subject to repossession.
Record:: A written account of the proceedings in a case, including all pleadings, evidence, and exhibits submitted in the course of the case.
Redemption:: A procedure in a link:americanlaw101_ch07.html[Chapter 7] case whereby a debtor removes a secured creditor’s lien on collateral by paying the creditor the value of the property. The debtor may then retain the property.
Remand:: Send back.
Reverse:: The act of a court setting aside the decision of a lower court. A reversal is often accompanied by a remand to the lower court for further proceedings.

=== S

Sanction:: A penalty or other type of enforcement used to bring about compliance with the law or with rules and regulations.
Schedules:: Lists submitted by the debtor along with the petition (or shortly thereafter), showing the debtor’s assets, liabilities, and other financial information. (There are official forms a debtor must use.)
Secured creditor:: A secured creditor is an individual or business that holds a claim against the debtor that is secured by a lien on property of the estate. The property subject to the lien is the secured creditor’s collateral.
Secured debt:: Debt backed by a mortgage, pledge of collateral, or other lien; debt for which the creditor has the right to pursue specific pledged property upon default. Examples include home mortgages, auto loans and tax liens.
Senior judge:: A federal judge who, after attaining the requisite age and length of judicial experience, takes senior status, thus creating a vacancy among a court’s active judges. A senior judge retains the judicial office and may cut back his or her workload by as much as 75 percent, but many opt to keep a larger caseload.
Sentence:: The punishment ordered by a court for a defendant convicted of a crime.
Sentencing guidelines:: A set of rules and principles established by the United States Sentencing Commission that trial judges use to determine the sentence for a convicted defendant.
Service of process:: The delivery of writs or summonses to the appropriate party.
Settlement:: Parties to a lawsuit resolve their dispute without having a trial. Settlements often involve the payment of compensation by one party in at least partial satisfaction of the other party’s claims, but usually do not include the admission of fault.
Sequester:: To separate. Sometimes juries are sequestered from outside influences during their deliberations.
Small business case:: A special type of Chapter 11 case in which there is no creditors’ committee (or the creditors’ committee is deemed inactive by the court) and in which the debtor is subject to more oversight by the U.S. trustee than other Chapter 11 debtors. The Bankruptcy Code contains certain provisions designed to reduce the time a small business debtor is in bankruptcy.
Statement of financial affairs:: A series of questions the debtor must answer in writing concerning sources of income, transfers of property, lawsuits by creditors, etc. (There is an official form a debtor must use.)
Statement of intention:: A declaration made by a link:americanlaw101_ch07.html[Chapter 7] debtor concerning plans for dealing with consumer debts that are secured by property of the estate.
Standard of proof:: Degree of proof required. In criminal cases, prosecutors must prove a defendant’s guilt “beyond a reasonable doubt.” The majority of civil lawsuits require proof “by a preponderance of the evidence” (50 percent plus), but in some cases the standard is higher and requires “clear and convincing” proof.
Statute:: A law passed by a legislature.
Statute of limitations:: The time within which a lawsuit must be filed or a criminal prosecution begun. The deadline can vary, depending on the type of civil case or the crime charged.
Sua sponte:: Latin, meaning “of its own will.” Often refers to a court taking an action in a case without being asked to do so by either side.
Subordination:: The act or process by which a person’s rights or claims are ranked below those of others.
Subpoena:: A command, issued under a court’s authority, to a witness to appear and give testimony.
Subpoena duces tecum:: A command to a witness to appear and produce documents.
Substance abuse treatment:: A special condition the court imposes that requires an individual to undergo testing and treatment for abuse of illegal drugs, prescription drugs, or alcohol. Treatment may include inpatient or outpatient counseling and detoxification.
Substantial abuse:: The characterization of a bankruptcy case filed by an individual whose debts are primarily consumer debts, where the court finds that the granting of relief would be an abuse of link:americanlaw101_ch07.html[Chapter 7] because, for example, the debtor can pay its debts.
Substantive consolidation:: Putting the assets and liabilities of two or more related debtors into a single pool to pay creditors. (Courts are reluctant to allow substantive consolidation because the action must justify not only the benefit that one set of creditors receives but also the harm that other creditors suffer as a result.)
Summary judgment:: A decision made on the basis of statements and evidence presented for the record without a trial. It is used when it is not necessary to resolve any factual disputes in the case. Summary judgment is granted when—on the undisputed facts in the record—one party is entitled to judgment as a matter of law.
Supervised release:: Term of supervision served after a person is released from prison. The court imposes supervised release during sentencing in addition to the sentence of imprisonment. Unlike parole, supervised release does not replace a portion of the sentence of imprisonment but is in addition to the time spent in prison. U.S. probation officers supervise people on supervised release.

=== T

Temporary restraining order:: Akin to a preliminary injunction, it is a judge’s short-term order forbidding certain actions until a full hearing can be conducted. Often referred to as a TRO.
Testimony:: Evidence presented orally by witnesses during trials or before grand juries.
Toll:: See statute of limitations.
Tort:: A civil, not criminal, wrong. A negligent or intentional injury against a person or property, with the exception of breach of contract.
Transfer:: Any mode or means by which a debtor disposes of or parts with his or her property.
Transcript:: A written, word-for-word record of what was said, either in a proceeding such as a trial or during some other formal conversation, such as a hearing or oral deposition.
Trustee:: The representative of the bankruptcy estate who exercises statutory powers, principally for the benefit of the unsecured creditors, under the general supervision of the court and the direct supervision of the U.S. trustee or bankruptcy administrator. The trustee is a private individual or corporation appointed in all link:americanlaw101_ch07.html[Chapter 7], Chapter 12, and Chapter 13 cases and some Chapter 11 cases. The trustee’s responsibilities include reviewing the debtor’s petition and schedules and bringing actions against creditors or the debtor to recover property of the bankruptcy estate. In link:americanlaw101_ch07.html[Chapter 7], the trustee liquidates property of the estate and makes distributions to creditors. Trustees in Chapter 12 and 13 have similar duties to a Chapter 7 trustee and the additional responsibilities of overseeing the debtor’s plan, receiving payments from debtors, and disbursing plan payments to creditors.
Typing service:: A business not authorized to practice law that prepares bankruptcy petitions.

=== U

U.S. attorney:: A lawyer appointed by the President in each judicial district to prosecute and defend cases for the federal government. The U.S. Attorney employs a staff of Assistant U.S. Attorneys who appear as the government’s attorneys in individual cases.
U.S. trustee:: An officer of the U.S. Department of Justice responsible for supervising the administration of bankruptcy cases, estates, and trustees; monitoring plans and disclosure statements; monitoring creditors’ committees; monitoring fee applications; and performing other statutory duties.
Undersecured claim:: A debt secured by property that is worth less than the amount of the debt.
Undue hardship:: The most widely used test for evaluating undue hardship in the dischargeability of a student loan includes three conditions: (1) the debtor cannot maintain—based on current income and expenses—a minimal standard of living if forced to repay the loans; (2) there are indications that the state of affairs is likely to persist for a significant portion of the repayment period; and (3) the debtor made good-faith efforts to repay the loans.
Unlawful detainer action:: A lawsuit brought by a landlord against a tenant to evict the tenant from rental property—usually for nonpayment of rent.
Unliquidated claim:: A claim for which a specific value has not been determined.
Unscheduled debt:: A debt that should have been listed by the debtor in the schedules filed with the court but was not. (Depending on the circumstances, an unscheduled debt may or may not be discharged.)
Unsecured claim:: A claim or debt for which a creditor holds no special assurance of payment, such as a mortgage or lien; a debt for which credit was extended based solely upon the creditor’s assessment of the debtor’s future ability to pay.
Uphold:: The appellate court agrees with the lower court decision and allows it to stand. See affirmed.

=== V

Venue:: The geographic area in which a court has jurisdiction. A change of venue is a change or transfer of a case from one judicial district to another.
Verdict:: The decision of a trial jury or a judge that determines the guilt or innocence of a criminal defendant, or that determines the final outcome of a civil case.
Voir dire:: French, meaning “say the truth.” Jury selection process of questioning prospective jurors, to ascertain their qualifications and determine any basis for challenge.
Voluntary transfer:: A transfer of a debtor’s property with the debtor’s consent.

=== W

Wage garnishment:: A nonbankruptcy legal proceeding whereby a plaintiff or creditor seeks to subject to his or her claim the future wages of a debtor. In other words, the creditor seeks to have part of the debtor’s future wages paid to the creditor for a debt owed to the creditor.
Warrant:: Court authorization, most often for law enforcement officers, to conduct a search or make an arrest.
Witness:: A person called upon by either side in a lawsuit to give testimony before the court or jury.
Writ:: A written court order directing a person to take, or refrain from taking, a certain act.
Writ of certiorari:: An order issued by the U.S. Supreme Court directing the lower court to transmit records for a case which it will hear on appeal.
