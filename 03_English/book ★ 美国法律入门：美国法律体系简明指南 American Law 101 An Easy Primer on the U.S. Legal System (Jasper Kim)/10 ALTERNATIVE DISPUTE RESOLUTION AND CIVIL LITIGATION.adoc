== 释义

10.ALTERNATIVE DISPUTE RESOLUTION AND CIVIL LITIGATION

Most cases in the American legal system 法律体系 are resolved 解决 out of the courtroom 法庭外 through alternative dispute resolution 替代性纠纷解决方式 (ADR) before the dispute is brought into 被提交至 the courtroom through the civil litigation 民事诉讼 process 程序. This chapter discusses various aspects 方面 of ADR and then focuses on 聚焦于 the civil litigation process. In this process, ADR options 选项 (namely 即, mediation 调解) can be set aside 搁置, temporarily 暂时地 or entirely 完全地, for a court-based 法院主导的 civil litigation process and ruling 裁决.

[.my2]
在美国法律体系中，大多数案件会在通过民事诉讼程序提交至法庭之前，通过替代性纠纷解决方式（ADR）在法庭外得到解决。本章将探讨替代性纠纷解决方式的多个方面，随后聚焦于民事诉讼程序。在这一流程中，替代性纠纷解决方式的相关选项（即调解）可被暂时或完全搁置，转而采用法院主导的民事诉讼程序并由法院作出裁决。

=== Alternative Dispute Resolution

Alternative dispute resolution is a broad term 宽泛的术语 attached to 适用于 various methods 方法 of resolving disputes within the American legal system, including, but not limited to 包括但不限于, arbitration 仲裁, mediation 调解, and negotiation 协商. Each of these ADR approaches 方式 is covered in this section 本节将对上述每种方式进行阐述.

[.my2]
替代性纠纷解决方式是一个适用于美国法律体系内多种纠纷解决方法的宽泛术语，其范畴包括但不限于仲裁、调解与协商。本节将对上述每种替代性纠纷解决方式进行阐述。

---

==== Arbitration

Arbitration represents 代表；是 one type of dispute resolution. It is often used to resolve disputes to get to a yes or no final determination 最终裁定 on a particular 特定的 issue 事项 or issues. Typically 通常, arbitration is used when mediation has not been chosen or has failed 失败. Although states can be involved in 参与 arbitration proceedings 仲裁程序, private parties 私人当事方 also are free to 有权 enter into 启动 arbitration proceedings so long as 只要 all relevant 相关的 parties consent to 同意 arbitration proceedings.

[.my2]
仲裁是纠纷解决方式的一种。它通常用于对某一或某些特定事项作出非此即彼的最终裁定。一般情况下，当未选择调解或调解失败时，会采用仲裁的方式。尽管国家可参与仲裁程序，但只要所有相关当事方均同意，私人当事方也有权启动仲裁程序。

Arbitration involves 涉及 an impartial 公正的；中立的 third party 第三方—referred to as 被称为 the arbitrator 仲裁员—who renders 作出 a decision relating to a specific 具体的 issue or issues in a dispute between two or more parties. The number of arbitrators varies 各不相同, where having one, two, three, or even more arbitrators would be the norm 常态, although they are usually in odd numbers 奇数 to avoid 避免 an evenly split 平分的 judgment 判决. Each arbitrator is generally chosen by one or more of the relevant parties to the dispute, often selected based on 基于 the arbitrator’s knowledge 知识 and expertise 专业技能 in a field 领域 related to the dispute, which can include current 现任的 or former 前任的 attorneys 律师, judges 法官, academics 学者, and/or other relevant experts 专家.

[.my2]
仲裁程序会由一名中立的第三方（即仲裁员）参与，仲裁员需针对两名或多名当事方之间纠纷的某一或某些具体事项作出裁决。仲裁员的人数没有定数，一名、两名、三名甚至更多都属常态，但通常会设定为奇数，以避免出现判决结果平分的情况。每位仲裁员一般由纠纷的一方或多方相关当事方选定，遴选的依据往往是仲裁员在与纠纷相关领域具备的知识与专业技能，仲裁员的人选可以是现任或前任律师、法官、学者以及其他相关领域的专家。

An arbitration proceeding can be distinguished from 区别于 a legal action 诉讼行为 heard by judges in a court in that 因为 an arbitration proceeding is typically private 私下进行的, whereas 而 litigation in a court is typically public in nature 本质上是公开的. Thus 因此, one benefit 优势 of a private proceeding, as in the case of arbitration, is that outside parties 外部主体 not directly related to the dispute are normally not allowed to listen in on 旁听 the proceedings. This generally includes members of the press 新闻媒体从业者, and possibly other industry members 行业从业者, in which public disclosure 公开披露 (and even the mere public appearance 曝光 in a lawsuit dispute) can result in 导致 less than positive consequences 负面后果. For example, an article could be printed in a financial news publication 财经新闻出版物 that a particular lawsuit proceeding has commenced 启动 between two large firms 大型企业, which could clearly have potentially negative effects 潜在负面影响 on stock prices 股价.

[.my2]
仲裁程序与法院法官审理的诉讼行为存在区别，原因在于仲裁程序通常私下进行，而法庭诉讼本质上是公开的。因此，像仲裁这样的私下程序，其优势之一是与纠纷无直接关联的外部主体通常不得旁听程序过程。这类外部主体一般包括新闻媒体从业者，也可能涵盖其他行业从业者——对这些主体而言，相关事项的公开披露（甚至仅仅是涉诉纠纷的曝光）都可能引发负面后果。例如，某份财经新闻出版物若刊登了两家大型企业之间启动诉讼程序的报道，显然可能会对两家企业的股价产生潜在负面影响。

The parties to an arbitration proceeding also generally can decide 决定 whether the arbitration decision is binding on 对……具有约束力 the parties as well as whether the opinion 裁决意见 is appealable 可上诉的 or nonappealable 不可上诉的. The parties often opt for 选择 a nonappealable arbitration decision, because this can decrease 减少 the amount of time involved in the dispute before a final outcome 最终结果 is rendered. In contrast 相比之下, in a public court system, depending on 取决于 which court is the court of first instance 初审法院 to hear the dispute, one or more appeals 上诉 can be allowed for 被允许, which can elongate 延长 the time it takes to get to a final dispute decision for months 数月, if not years 甚至数年. Thus, in comparison to 与……相比 a public court system, arbitration can be faster and more cost-effective 性价比更高的 because of less time and resources 资源 required over a shorter period 更短的周期.

[.my2]
仲裁程序的当事方通常也有权决定仲裁裁决是否对各方具有约束力，以及该裁决意见是否可以上诉。当事方往往会选择不可上诉的仲裁裁决，因为这能缩短纠纷达成最终结果所需的时间。相比之下，在公开的法院体系中，根据审理纠纷的初审法院层级不同，当事方可能获得一次或多次上诉的机会，这会将纠纷作出最终裁决的时间延长数月甚至数年。因此，相较于公开法院体系，仲裁耗时更短，所需投入的资源更少，整体性价比更高。

As a result of 由于 these cost and time advantages 成本与时间优势, within the American legal system, arbitration proceedings have become an increasingly preferred 愈发受青睐的 option 选择 to resolve disputes regarding 关于 domestic 国内的 and cross-border 跨境的 dealings 交易. Various organizations 组织, such as the International Bar Association 国际律师协会, have set 制定 broad-based 全面的 arbitration rules 仲裁规则, but many arbitration panels 仲裁庭 have the option to 有权 flexibly modify 灵活修改 procedures 流程 and rules as deemed necessary 必要的 and appropriate 恰当的 for the dispute in question 相关纠纷. Arbitration is also available for 适用于 any party, including states, corporations 企业, and individuals 个人, which makes it a viable 切实可行的 alternative 选择 for actors 主体 in the international economic system 国际经济体系.

[.my2]
正是由于这些成本与时间方面的优势，在美国法律体系内，仲裁程序已愈发成为解决国内及跨境交易纠纷的优选方式。包括国际律师协会在内的多个组织都制定了全面的仲裁规则，但许多仲裁庭有权根据相关纠纷的实际情况，灵活修改其认为必要且恰当的流程与规则。仲裁同样适用于任何当事方，包括国家、企业与个人，这使其成为国际经济体系内各类主体切实可行的纠纷解决选择。

==== Mediation

Much like 很像 arbitration, mediation also involves an impartial third party—referred to as the mediator 调解员—to resolve a particular dispute between two or more parties. But unlike 与……不同 an arbitration proceeding, a mediator’s decision is often not binding 不具有约束力的. As a result 因此, a primary objective 主要目标 of a mediation session 调解会议 is often to have a trusted 受信任的 impartial third party to raise the communication lines 搭建沟通渠道 between the parties to help find common ground 共识 to resolve the dispute. Mediation is often used at the early stages 初期阶段 in a dispute—even, at times 有时, before arbitration and litigation 诉讼.

[.my2]
与仲裁类似，调解也会引入一名中立的第三方（即调解员），协助两名或多名当事方解决特定纠纷。但与仲裁程序不同的是，调解员作出的决定通常不具有约束力。因此，调解会议的主要目标往往是借助一名受信任的中立第三方，为当事方搭建沟通渠道，帮助双方找到解决纠纷的共识。调解通常用于纠纷的初期阶段——有时甚至会早于仲裁和诉讼启动。

As in arbitration, the relevant parties must also consent to 同意 the mediation process 调解流程. At least one or more of the parties also typically must consent to the mediator(s) chosen to resolve the dispute. Mediators, who are often but not always 通常但并非总是 licensed attorneys 持证律师 and/or judges, are also generally chosen for their expertise 专业能力 and knowledge in a particular field deemed relevant to 与……相关的 resolving the dispute. In principle 原则上, the mediation process is also open to 适用于 most actors in the international economic system, including, but not limited to 包括但不限于, states, private-sector entities 私营实体, and individuals.

[.my2]
与仲裁程序相同，相关当事方必须同意启动调解流程。通常，至少有一方或多方当事方还需对选定的调解员表示认可。调解员通常为持证律师或法官，但也并非绝对如此，其人选一般也是基于在与解决纠纷相关的特定领域具备的专业能力与知识来选定。原则上，调解流程也适用于国际经济体系内的大多数主体，包括但不限于国家、私营实体与个人。

==== Negotiation

Another form 形式 of alternative dispute resolution is negotiation 协商. Negotiation is the process by which disputes can be resolved and value 价值 can be created for the parties involved 所涉当事方. As you may imagine 正如你所想的, the degrees of formality 正式程度 of negotiations can vary greatly 差异很大, from a formal negotiation setting 场合 in a conference room 会议室 meeting to a simple e-mail exchange 邮件往来 regarding terms and conditions 条款 to a particular agreement 协议. At a more conceptual level 从更概念化的层面来讲, two main schools of thought 流派 exist relating to negotiations: positional bargaining 立场式谈判 and principled bargaining 原则式谈判.

[.my2]
协商是替代性纠纷解决方式的另一种形式。协商是一种既能解决纠纷，又能为所涉当事方创造价值的流程。正如你所想的，协商的正式程度差异很大，既可以是在会议室进行的正式协商会议，也可以是围绕某一特定协议条款展开的简单邮件往来。从更概念化的层面来讲，协商主要存在两种流派：立场式谈判与原则式谈判。

Positional bargaining occurs when 发生于……的情况 parties’ negotiation focuses primarily on 主要聚焦于 each of their respective positions 各自的立场 (i.e., set terms and conditions 既定条款, such as price 价格, quantity 数量, land area 土地面积, and time 时间), with little or no exploration 探究 of the other side’s interests 利益诉求 (i.e., the rationale 根本原因 underlying 支撑 one’s position). Positional bargaining is often equated to 等同于 competitive 竞争性的 and hard negotiations 强硬谈判 in which a clear winner or loser 赢家或输家 is sought 追求.

[.my2]
立场式谈判是指谈判各方的协商主要聚焦于各自的立场（即既定条款，例如价格、数量、土地面积与时间），而很少甚至完全不探究对方的利益诉求（即支撑其立场的根本原因）。立场式谈判通常等同于竞争性的强硬谈判，这类谈判追求的是决出明确的赢家与输家。

Positional bargaining often involves 涉及 some form of haggling 讨价还价 or compromise 妥协, in which, for example, the price of a particular good 商品 is somewhere near 接近 the middle point 中间值 of two extreme price points 极端价格. For example, you may see positional bargaining at work 实际应用 at a weekend flea market 周末跳蚤市场 for a used coffee table 二手茶几. The exchange 交流 may begin by the buyer 买家 offering 出价 the seller 卖家 \$10, with a very quick counteroffer 还价 of \$100. Then comes the negotiation dance 谈判拉锯战 in which both sides, if interested 若有交易意愿, typically begin making concessions 作出让步 toward a price point somewhere near the middle point between \$10 and \$100.

[.my2]
立场式谈判通常会涉及某种形式的讨价还价或妥协，例如某件商品的最终定价会接近双方给出的两个极端价格的中间值。举例来说，你能在周末跳蚤市场里看到立场式谈判的实际应用——比如买卖一张二手茶几。交易的开局可能是买家向卖家出价10美元，而卖家立刻还价100美元。随后便进入了谈判拉锯战，若双方都有交易意愿，通常会开始逐步作出让步，朝着10美元至100美元区间的中间价格靠拢。

Here, the negotiator(s) assume 假定 a fixed pie 固定蛋糕理论 (or zero sum game 零和博弈). In this working assumption 基于这一假设, the negotiators are in effect 实际上 engaged in 参与 a land grab 争夺利益的行为, whereby 在此情况下 one party’s gain 收益 is directly correlated to 与……直接相关 the counterparty’s 交易对方的 loss 损失, similar to 类似于 a sports competition 体育竞赛. Thus, the more points 利益点 (in the form of 以……形式 price, quantity, land area, time, etc.) gained from the other side, the better it is from a positional negotiator’s perspective 从立场式谈判者的角度来看, because the goal 目标 of positional bargaining is to gain as much as possible 尽可能多地获取利益.

[.my2]
在立场式谈判中，谈判者会假定适用“固定蛋糕理论”（或零和博弈）。基于这一假设，谈判者实际上是在参与一场利益争夺战，一方的收益与交易对方的损失直接相关，这与体育竞赛的赛制颇为相似。因此，从立场式谈判者的角度来看，从对方那里获取的利益点（以价格、数量、土地面积、时间等形式体现）越多越好，因为立场式谈判的目标就是尽可能多地获取利益。

In contrast 相比之下, principled (or integrative) bargaining 原则式（或整合式）谈判 involves a different approach 方式. The approach is to not negotiate against 对抗 the counterparty, but instead, to negotiate with 与……协作 the counterparty (in a form of 以……形式 us instead of 而非 you vs. me 你我对立). In other words 换句话说, by linking or integrating 联结或整合 both sides, opportunities could be sought 寻找机会 in a value-added way 以增值的方式 through various means 多种途径.

[.my2]
相比之下，原则式（或整合式）谈判则采用了截然不同的思路。这种谈判方式不是与交易对方进行对抗式协商，而是与对方协作谈判（呈现出“我们联手”而非“你我对立”的态势）。换句话说，通过将双方的诉求联结或整合起来，能够以多种途径寻找增值的机会。


The main school of thought 流派 on integrative bargaining 整合式谈判 is derived from 源自于 arguably 可以说 the most popular book related to negotiations, Getting to Yes, by Roger Fisher and William Ury of Harvard University’s Program on Negotiation 哈佛大学谈判项目组. The authors suggest 建议 conducting integrative bargaining through the following structured framework 结构化框架 of seven elements 要素:

1. Communication (building effective 有效的 and respectful 尊重的 communication)
2. Relationship (to understand and analyze 分析 the type of relationship wanted)
3. Interests (exploring 探究 common 共同的 and shared 共享的 interests)
4. Options (creating options, or brainstorming 头脑风暴, in a form of 以……形式 two heads are better than one 两人智慧胜一人)
5. Legitimacy 合理性 (deciding which options are preferable 更可取的 based on agreed-upon 商定的 objective criteria 客观标准)
6. Alternatives 备选方案 (choosing no to an offer 提议 based on the calculation 考量 that the offer by the counterparty 交易对方 is not better than one’s BATNA, which stands for 代表 the Best Alternative To a Negotiated Agreement, in essence 本质上, one’s opportunity cost 机会成本 or best walk-away alternative 最佳退出方案)
7. Commitment 承诺 (choosing yes to an offer because it is better than one’s BATNA)

[.my2]
关于整合式谈判的主流理论，源自于哈佛大学谈判项目组的罗杰·费希尔与威廉·尤里所著的堪称最受欢迎的谈判类书籍——《谈判力》。两位作者建议，可通过以下包含七个要素的结构化框架开展整合式谈判：
1.  沟通（建立有效且互相尊重的沟通机制）
2.  关系（明确并分析期望达成的关系类型）
3.  利益诉求（探究双方的共同利益与共享利益）
4.  方案（提出备选方案，或通过头脑风暴集思广益，秉持“两人智慧胜一人”的原则）
5.  合理性（依据商定的客观标准，判定哪些方案更可取）
6.  备选方案（若经考量发现交易对方的提议不如自己的最佳替代方案，便拒绝该提议；最佳替代方案的英文缩写为BATNA，全称是“谈判协议的最佳替代方案”，本质上就是谈判者的机会成本或最佳退出方案）
7.  承诺（若交易对方的提议优于自己的最佳替代方案，便接受该提议并作出承诺）

In principled negotiations 原则式谈判, the spirit 核心要义 is to dig deeper into 深入挖掘 each party’s interests that exist underneath 潜藏于 a party’s position 立场之下. This is often done by simply asking “Why?” regarding a particular issue 特定事项. For example, with the previous coffee table example 前文提到的茶几案例, the buyer may want to ask why the seller wants \$100 for the table. It could be that an underlying reason 深层原因 exists to rationalize 合理解释 the price (e.g., a high quality of wood 优质木材, historical significance 历史意义) and vice versa 反之亦然 (e.g., the buyer is a student with little cash 现金拮据 and just wants basic cheap furniture 简易廉价家具 for his dorm room 宿舍房间). The thinking is that exploring each party’s underlying interests can sometimes, but not always, help to bridge the gap 弥合分歧 between differences in a negotiation setting 谈判场景.

[.my2]
原则式谈判的核心要义，是深入挖掘潜藏在各方立场之下的利益诉求。通常的做法是，针对特定事项直接提出“为什么”的疑问。例如在前面提到的茶几案例中，买家可以询问卖家为何要将茶几定价为100美元。卖家的定价可能存在合理解释的深层原因（比如采用了优质木材、具有历史意义），反之买家的出价也可能有其缘由（比如买家是一名现金拮据的学生，只是想为宿舍购置一件简易廉价的家具）。这种谈判思路认为，在谈判场景中，探究各方的深层利益诉求有时（但并非总能）有助于弥合分歧。

Some may ask, “Which negotiation style 谈判风格 is better?” Both have their advantages 优势 and disadvantages 劣势. So rather than 与其 giving a broad one-size-fits-all 一刀切的 answer, it may be better to analyze the benefits 益处 and risks 风险 associated with 与……相关的 each negotiation style. One advantage of positional bargaining 立场式谈判 is that it is relatively fast 相对快捷, because only the parties’ positions need to be focused on 聚焦于, and furthermore 此外, identifying 明确 the positions (e.g., price, quantity, time) of the related parties is fairly easy 相当容易. One risk is that a relationship is never forged 建立, at least not a sustainable 可持续的 one, because the only point of focus is the parties’ positions, not on building a relationship.

[.my2]
有人可能会问：“哪种谈判风格更好？”其实两种风格各有利弊。因此，与其给出一个宽泛的一刀切答案，不如去分析每种谈判风格所对应的益处与风险。立场式谈判的优势之一是相对快捷，因为谈判只需聚焦于各方的立场，此外，明确相关当事方的立场（如价格、数量、时间）也相当容易。而这种谈判风格的风险在于，双方无法建立起关系，至少是无法建立可持续的关系，因为谈判的唯一关注点是各方立场，而非关系的构建。

Thus 因此, the negotiation can lead to 导致 negotiator’s remorse 谈判者悔意, in which either or even both sides are left with a bad taste in their mouth 心存不满 after the negotiation (i.e., the parties do not feel satisfied 满意 even after agreement 达成协议 and/or would not be happy having to negotiate with each other again due to a lack of trust 信任缺失 or goodwill 善意匮乏). Especially in an interconnected 相互关联的 and globalized 全球化的 world, in which professionals 从业者 have only a few degrees of separation 隔行如隔山, not forging strong relationships can lead to a non-optimal 非最优的 negotiated outcome 谈判结果 (i.e., getting to a yes in a deal 促成交易 merely for the sake of 为了 reaching an agreement, although the agreement may not substantively 实质上 be best for the parties). This is often the case 情况往往如此 because the negotiated outcome is merely a halfway point 中间值 between two extreme initial positions 初始极端立场, whereby 在此情况下 both parties fail to fully get what they want.

[.my2]
因此，这类谈判可能会引发谈判者悔意，即谈判结束后，一方甚至双方都会心存不满（也就是说，即便达成了协议，各方也并未感到满意；而且由于信任缺失或善意匮乏，双方都不乐意再次展开谈判）。尤其是在这个相互关联的全球化世界里，不同领域的从业者之间可谓隔行如隔山，未能建立稳固的合作关系，往往会导致非最优的谈判结果（比如，谈判只是为了促成交易而草草达成协议，但这份协议实质上可能并非对各方都最有利）。出现这种情况的原因通常是，谈判结果仅仅是双方初始极端立场的中间值，双方都未能完全获得自己想要的东西。

The advantage of principled (integrative) bargaining 原则式（整合式）谈判 is that it focuses more on forging 构建 good communication methods 沟通方式 to forge a strong relationship whereby 从而 creative solutions 创新性解决方案 can result. This approach may lead to a higher chance of 更有可能 reaching a more optimal negotiated outcome. The risk of the principled bargaining strategy 策略 is that, because it involves more variables 变量—exploring common interests, creating options together, and seeking legitimate standards 合理标准—principled negotiations can often take longer 耗时更久 to reach a negotiated outcome relative to 相较于 the more straightforward 直接的 and simplistic 简单的 positional bargaining method.

[.my2]
原则式（整合式）谈判的优势在于，它更注重构建良好的沟通方式来建立稳固的合作关系，进而催生创新性解决方案。这种谈判方式更有可能达成更优的谈判结果。而原则式谈判策略的风险在于，由于涉及更多变量——包括探究共同利益、共同制定方案、寻求合理标准等——相较于更直接简单的立场式谈判，原则式谈判往往需要耗费更长时间才能达成结果。

In a more complex 复杂的 negotiation setting, taking more time and effort 耗时费力, the sessions 谈判过程 often oscillate between 摇摆于……之间 principled and positional bargaining styles, but one takeaway lesson 经验教训 for practitioners 从业者 and students of negotiation strategy is that more than one method of negotiation strategy can be used to reach your objective 目标.

[.my2]
在更复杂的谈判场景中，谈判过程往往耗时费力，且会在原则式谈判与立场式谈判两种风格之间不断摇摆。但对于谈判策略的从业者和学习者而言，有一条重要的经验教训：达成目标的谈判策略并非只有一种，而是可以灵活采用多种方式。

This section discussed the main alternative dispute resolution 替代性纠纷解决方式 strategies and frameworks that exist. The next section focuses on 聚焦于 conceptual approaches 理念与方法 used by practitioners and others in terms of 关于 civil litigation 民事诉讼, which often intersects with 与……相互交织 ADR.

[.my2]
本节阐述了现有的主流替代性纠纷解决策略与框架。下一节将聚焦于从业者及相关人士在民事诉讼中所采用的理念与方法，而民事诉讼往往与替代性纠纷解决方式相互交织。

=== Civil Litigation

What happens when a party 当事方 is involved in 卷入 a civil dispute 民事纠纷 and enters into civil litigation? And what is the litigation process 诉讼流程 in the event of 倘若发生 a civil action lawsuit 民事诉讼案件? The topics of civil action and civil procedure 民事诉讼程序 hold some key answers to these and other related questions. Litigation is the legal system’s mechanism 机制 for resolving disputes between parties. Civil procedure is the body of law 法律体系 that provides the structure 框架 and process related to such litigation. Litigation can be initiated by 由……提起 two or more individuals 个人, companies, or other legal persons 法人.

[.my2]
民事诉讼 +
如果某一当事方卷入民事纠纷并进入民事诉讼程序，会发生什么？倘若启动民事诉讼案件，具体的诉讼流程是怎样的？民事诉讼与民事诉讼程序这两个议题，能够为这些问题及其他相关问题提供部分关键答案。诉讼是法律体系中用于解决当事方之间纠纷的机制。民事诉讼程序则是为这类诉讼提供框架与流程的法律体系。诉讼可由两个或两个以上的个人、企业或其他法人提起。

Civil action and procedure’s objectives 目标 include the following:

- Implementing 执行 substantive rules of law 实体法规则 and related policy considerations 政策考量
- Ensuring that the litigation process is fair 公正的 and transparent 透明的
- Seeking an efficient 高效的 process and outcome

[.my2]
民事诉讼及民事诉讼程序的目标如下：
- 执行实体法规则及相关政策考量
- 确保诉讼过程公正透明
- 追求高效的诉讼流程与结果

When a lawsuit begins in the litigation process, two main considerations 考量 arise 浮现: (1) which court, or courts, could have jurisdiction 管辖权 to hear the case 审理案件; and (2) if more than one court could conceivably 可能地 have jurisdiction, which specific court would be most beneficial 最有利的 from a legal strategy standpoint 从法律策略角度 for the lawyer’s client 当事人?

[.my2]
当诉讼程序启动后，会浮现出两个核心考量：（1）哪些法院拥有审理该案件的管辖权；（2）若存在多个可能拥有管辖权的法院，从法律策略角度来看，哪个具体法院对律师的当事人最为有利？

To establish jurisdiction 确立管辖权, a court must assert 主张: (1) subject matter jurisdiction 标的物管辖权 over the substantive legal issue 实体法律争议; and (2) personal jurisdiction 对人管辖权 over the relevant parties 相关当事方. Regarding personal jurisdiction, the courts have ruled 裁定 that the U.S. Constitution 美国宪法 requires that, in state courts 州法院, the defendant 被告 must have “minimum contacts 最低限度联系” with the forum state 法院地所在州 (the state where the lawsuit was filed 案件提起地) in the spirit of fair play 公平公正 and justice. At the federal level 联邦层面, courts often have limited 有限的, rather than general 一般的, jurisdiction. Thus, in contrast to 与……相比 state courts, federal courts 联邦法院 typically have jurisdiction over cases in specific categories 特定类别.

[.my2]
法院要确立管辖权，必须主张两点：（1）对该实体法律争议拥有标的物管辖权；（2）对相关当事方拥有对人管辖权。关于对人管辖权，法院裁定，美国宪法要求，在州法院体系中，本着公平公正的原则，被告必须与法院地所在州（即案件提起地所在州）存在“最低限度联系”。在联邦层面，法院通常拥有的是有限管辖权，而非一般管辖权。因此，与州法院不同，联邦法院的管辖权通常仅限于特定类别的案件。

When can a lawsuit be heard by federal courts? Federal courts generally have jurisdiction over two types of issues 案件类型:

- Federal questions 联邦问题案件
- Diversity jurisdiction 异籍管辖权案件

[.my2]
联邦法院在哪些情况下可以审理案件？联邦法院的管辖权通常涵盖两类案件：
- 联邦问题案件
- 异籍管辖权案件

Generally, federal questions involve issues such as the Constitution 宪法, federal statutes 联邦成文法, and federal administrative rules 联邦行政法规. Diversity jurisdiction typically involves a dispute between parties from two different states 不同州的当事方. Through diversity jurisdiction, prejudice 偏见 against out-of-state parties 外州当事方, if heard by a state court instead of a federal court, is prevented 得以避免. In litigation, the plaintiff 原告 can normally choose the court in which to sue 提起诉讼. However, the defendant can then ask to remove the case to 移送案件至 another court (i.e., from a state to a federal court). When legal issues of two competing jurisdictions 相冲突的管辖权 exist, an area of law known as conflict of laws 法律冲突 arises 随之产生.

[.my2]
一般来说，联邦问题案件涉及宪法、联邦成文法以及联邦行政法规等相关争议。异籍管辖权案件通常是指纠纷双方为不同州的当事方。通过异籍管辖权制度，可以避免外州当事方在州法院审理案件时受到偏见对待。在诉讼中，原告通常可以选择提起诉讼的法院。但被告随后可以申请将案件移送至其他法院（例如从州法院移送至联邦法院）。当案件涉及两个相冲突的管辖权法律问题时，一个被称为“法律冲突”的法律领域问题便随之产生。


==== A Brief Overview of a Civil Action Lawsuit: Complaint and Summons

A lawsuit begins with the plaintiff’s 原告的 lawyer drafting 起草 and submitting 提交 a document known as a complaint 起诉状. The document “complains” to the court that the defendant 被告 violated 侵犯 the plaintiff’s legal rights 合法权利, which links to 关联到 the facts 事实 relevant to 与……相关的 the complaint. The complaint also asks the court for a specific remedy 救济措施 for the violation of such legal rights. A summons 传票, which requests the defendant to legally respond to 回应 the complaint, is attached to 附于 the complaint.

[.my2]
民事诉讼案件始于原告律师起草并提交一份名为起诉状的文件。这份文件向法院“控诉”被告侵犯了原告的合法权利，并附上与起诉状相关的事实依据。起诉状同时还会请求法院针对该侵权行为作出特定的救济裁决。起诉状会附带一份传票，要求被告依法对起诉状作出回应。

The complaint is also part of the civil action litigation process by:

- Setting forth 阐明 the basis for 依据 jurisdiction 管辖权
- Putting the defendants on notice 通知 that the defendant is being sued 被起诉, and stating the basis of the lawsuit
- Stipulating 列明 the issues of law 法律争议点 and fact 事实争议点 relevant to the case

[.my2]
起诉状也是民事诉讼程序的组成部分，其作用在于：
- 阐明法院管辖权的依据
- 通知被告已被起诉，并说明诉讼的案由
- 列明与案件相关的法律争议点和事实争议点

Service of process 送达诉讼文书 is the next step in the litigation process. Service of process requires that the defendant be “served” 送达 a copy of the plaintiff’s complaint and summons, delivered by 由……送达 a process server 送达员, registered mail 挂号信, police authority 警方, or other person as required by the court that has jurisdiction in the case 对案件拥有管辖权的法院.

[.my2]
送达诉讼文书是诉讼程序的下一个环节。该环节要求将原告起诉状和传票的副本送达被告，送达方式可由送达员、挂号信、警方或对案件拥有管辖权的法院所指定的其他人员完成。

What legal options 法律选择 exist for the defendant once the plaintiff’s complaint has been served to the defendant?

[.my2]
当原告的起诉状送达被告后，被告有哪些法律选择？

1. Ignore the plaintiff’s complaint. In a criminal law case 刑事案件, this could lead to 导致 the possible arrest 逮捕 of the defendant for failure to appear in court 拒不出庭. In a civil law case 民事案件, the court could rule for 作出……的裁决 a default judgment 缺席判决 against the defendant. A default judgment prevents 使无法 the defendant from then entering any legal defenses 提出法律抗辩 in the case.
2. Raise a legal objection 提出法律异议 in the form of 以……形式 a motion to dismiss 驳回起诉动议. In a motion to dismiss, the defendant would argue 主张 that, among other things 除此之外, no remedy exists for the plaintiff’s legal claim 法律诉求 against the defendant. The motion to dismiss is a formal request 正式请求 to the court by the defendant to dismiss the case 驳回案件 without the need to consider the substantive merits 实体案情 of the case.
3. Dispute 质疑 the legal sufficiency 法律充分性 of the plaintiff’s complaint through a demurrer 抗辩书. Another term for demurrer is a motion to dismiss for failure to state a claim 因主张缺乏法律依据而提出的驳回动议, or failure to state a cause of action 因缺乏诉因而提出的驳回动议.
4. File 提交 an answer 答辩状 to the plaintiff’s complaint. The defendant’s answer can include denying 否认, disclaiming 拒绝承认, or admitting 承认 the plaintiff’s allegations 指控, but stating the basis for the defendant’s defense 抗辩依据 in the lawsuit, referred to as 被称为 an affirmative defense 积极抗辩. In an affirmative defense, the defendant argues that even assuming 即便假设 the plaintiff’s assertions 主张 are true, the defendant’s assertions of facts and arguments will prevail over 优先于 the plaintiff’s legal claims.

[.my2]
1.  无视原告的起诉状。在刑事案件中，此举可能会导致被告因拒不出庭而被逮捕。在民事案件中，法院可能会对被告作出缺席判决。缺席判决会使被告无法再就本案提出任何法律抗辩。
2.  以驳回起诉动议的形式提出法律异议。在驳回起诉动议中，被告会主张，除其他理由外，原告针对被告提出的法律诉求并无相应的救济途径。驳回起诉动议是被告向法院提交的正式请求，要求法院无需审查案件的实体案情即可驳回案件。
3.  通过提交抗辩书，质疑原告起诉状的法律充分性。抗辩书也可被称为“因主张缺乏法律依据而提出的驳回动议”或“因缺乏诉因而提出的驳回动议”。
4.  针对原告的起诉状提交答辩状。被告的答辩状可包含否认、拒绝承认或承认原告指控的内容，同时需列明被告在诉讼中的抗辩依据，该抗辩依据被称为积极抗辩。在积极抗辩中，被告主张即便原告的主张属实，被告所陈述的事实与理由也优先于原告的法律诉求。

==== Discovery Process

How do parties discover the facts in a case? Parties discover the facts in a case through a process called discovery 证据开示程序. Discovery is done at the pretrial stage 审前阶段 of the lawsuit process.

[.my2]
当事方如何查明案件事实？当事方可通过一项名为证据开示程序的流程来查明案件事实。证据开示程序在诉讼流程的审前阶段进行。

Discovery methods include the following:

- Depositions 证词录取: Interviews 询问 with other parties under oath 经宣誓后
- Interrogatories 书面质询: Written questions given to the counterparty 交易对方
- Demand for evidence (motion for production of documents 提交书证动议): Related to documents or other physical evidence 实物证据
- Physical examination 体格检查: Requires the other party to submit to 接受 a physical examination when relevant 相关时
- Requests for admissions 承认请求: Asks the other party to admit the truth of the facts relevant to the litigation; once the relevant parties believe that certain facts are undisputed 无争议的, then a request for admission can be issued 发出, narrowing 缩小 and defining 明确 the issues to be tried 待审争议点 in the case.

[.my2]
证据开示的方式包括以下几种：
- 证词录取：经宣誓后对其他当事方进行询问
- 书面质询：向交易对方发出书面问题
- 证据调取（提交书证动议）：调取相关文件或其他实物证据
- 体格检查：当相关时，要求另一方接受体格检查
- 承认请求：要求另一方承认与诉讼相关事实的真实性；一旦相关当事方认定某些事实为无争议事实，即可发出承认请求，从而缩小并明确案件的待审争议点。

The discovery process helps bring out 找出 all of the relevant evidence 相关证据 in a trial to achieve an efficient 高效的 and transparent 透明的 process to reach a fair outcome 公正结果. It also helps the relevant attorneys 律师 to better and more clearly define the issues of fact and law in the case. This process enables 使能够 both sides to bring forth 提出 the action 诉讼请求 and defenses 抗辩理由 to the case in a more optimal way 更优的方式.

[.my2]
证据开示程序有助于找出庭审中的所有相关证据，从而通过高效透明的流程实现公正的判决结果。该程序还能帮助相关律师更好、更清晰地界定案件的事实争议点与法律争议点。这一流程可使双方以更优的方式提出本案的诉讼请求与抗辩理由。

The pretrial phase 阶段 of the litigation is also an important time to reach a potential 潜在的 out-of-court settlement 庭外和解 between the parties. In most U.S. jurisdictions 司法管辖区, the majority of cases settle out-of-court before going to trial 开庭审理. Many jurisdictions also have mandatory 强制性的 programs of ADR 替代性纠纷解决方式 in the form of arbitration 仲裁, mediation 调解, or mini-trial 小型庭审. If a pretrial out-of-court settlement cannot be reached between the parties, then the lawsuit moves to 进入 the trial phase 庭审阶段.

[.my2]
诉讼的审前阶段也是当事方达成潜在庭外和解的重要时期。在美国的大多数司法管辖区，大部分案件都会在开庭审理前以庭外和解的方式结案。许多司法管辖区还设有强制性的替代性纠纷解决机制，形式包括仲裁、调解或小型庭审。如果当事方未能在审前阶段达成庭外和解，那么案件将进入庭审阶段。

==== Litigation and Civil Procedure at Trial and Beyond

The trial typically begins with opening statements 开场陈述 by both the plaintiff and defendant. The first opening statement is by the plaintiff’s legal counsel 法律顾问, because the plaintiff has the burden of proof 举证责任 (under the working assumption 基本推定 of “innocent until proven guilty 无罪推定”). The defendant’s opening statement then follows.

[.my2]
庭审通常始于原告与被告双方的开场陈述。首先由原告的法律顾问进行开场陈述，因为原告负有举证责任（基于“无罪推定”的基本推定原则）。随后由被告进行开场陈述。

The opening statements are followed by production of evidence 举证 and arguments 辩论 pursuant to 依据 the rules of evidence 证据规则 of the court’s jurisdiction. This can include the calling 传唤 and examination 询问 of witnesses 证人 in support of 支持 one side of the case. The production of evidentiary proof 证据提交 at trial is governed by 受……管辖 the law of civil procedure 民事诉讼法 as well as the law of evidence 证据法 specific to 特定于 the court’s jurisdiction. Civil procedure controls the order 顺序 and method 方式 of the production of such evidentiary proof. The rules of evidence within civil procedure determine 判定 what types of evidence are permissible 可采纳的 or not. For example, generally, evidence must be relevant 具有相关性 to be admissible 可采纳的 in a trial, but exceptions exist 存在例外, such as when the evidence is harmful character evidence 不良品格证据, and if it conflicts with 与……相冲突 legal policy 法律政策.

[.my2]
开场陈述结束后，双方依据法院管辖权范围内的证据规则进行举证和辩论。这一环节可能包括传唤并询问证人，以支持己方的诉讼主张。庭审中的证据提交受民事诉讼法以及该法院管辖权范围内特定证据法的管辖。民事诉讼法规范证据提交的顺序与方式。民事诉讼法中的证据规则判定哪些类型的证据可被采纳、哪些不可。例如，一般来说，证据必须具有相关性才能在庭审中被采纳，但也存在例外情况，比如当证据属于不良品格证据且与法律政策相冲突时，该证据不可采纳。

Another example of a rule of evidence is the inadmissibility 不可采纳性 of hearsay evidence 传闻证据 (secondhand testimony 二手证言 or out-of-court statement 庭外陈述 offered to prove the truth of a statement 用以证明所述内容的真实性). For example, a statement such as “I heard John say that Joe (defendant) always drives while he’s drunk (intoxicated 醉酒的)” would normally be considered as hearsay evidence, because the person making the statement 陈述者 did not hear the fact directly from Joe, but instead heard it from John, a secondhand testimonial source 二手证言来源.

[.my2]
证据规则的另一个例子是传闻证据的不可采纳性。传闻证据指的是二手证言或用以证明所述内容真实性的庭外陈述。例如，“我听约翰说乔（被告）经常酒后驾车”这类陈述通常会被认定为传闻证据，因为作出该陈述的人并非直接从乔口中得知此事，而是从约翰这一二手证言来源处听闻。

The end of the trial involves closing arguments 结案陈词, which are a summary 总结 of each party’s case. If the trial is a jury trial 陪审团审判, the judge 法官 would then instruct 指导 the jury 陪审团 on the relevant law 相关法律. The jury’s main task is to make determinations 作出认定 based on issues of fact. Thus, a division of labor 分工 exists between the judge (rule of law 适用法律) and jury (issues of fact 认定事实) in a jury trial. In a bench trial 法官独任审判 (a trial without a jury), the judge would consider both issues of law and fact before then rendering a ruling 作出裁决 on the case.

[.my2]
庭审的最后环节是结案陈词，即双方对各自案件主张的总结。如果案件采用陪审团审判，法官随后会向陪审团阐释相关法律规定。陪审团的主要职责是基于事实争议点作出认定。因此，在陪审团审判中，法官（适用法律）与陪审团（认定事实）之间存在明确分工。在法官独任审判（无陪审团参与的审判）中，法官需同时考量法律争议点与事实争议点，然后对案件作出裁决。

The Seventh Amendment 第七修正案 to the U.S. Constitution 美国宪法 and most state constitutions 州宪法 proclaim 赋予 the right to a jury trial in civil action cases. Generally, if either party requests a jury trial, then a jury trial will be granted 获准. Similarly, if both parties request a bench trial, then a bench trial is generally granted. In a jury trial, jury members should be representative of 代表 the general population 普通民众 and impartial 公正的. Thus, if legal counsel for a party can assert 主张 that a potential jury 潜在陪审员 or actual jury member is biased 存在偏见的, then this issue could be cause for removal 剔除理由 of a particular juror 陪审员.

[.my2]
美国宪法第七修正案以及大多数州的宪法都赋予了民事诉讼案件当事人陪审团审判的权利。一般而言，只要有一方当事人申请陪审团审判，该申请通常会获准。同样，如果双方当事人均申请法官独任审判，该申请通常也会被批准。在陪审团审判中，陪审员应当代表普通民众且保持公正立场。因此，若一方当事人的法律顾问能够主张某一潜在陪审员或现任陪审员存在偏见，那么这一点即可成为剔除该特定陪审员的理由。

After the judge and/or jury reaches a verdict 作出裁决, is this the end of the litigation cycle 诉讼周期? It generally depends on 取决于 what the parties are seeking. Among other things 除此之外, the parties can opt to 选择 appeal 上诉 the case to a higher court 上级法院. The losing party 败诉方 can appeal (becoming the appellant party 上诉方), but this is an option 选择权, not an obligatory duty 法定义务. The winning party 胜诉方 can also opt to appeal if the party received a partial remedy amount 部分救济金额 compared to what it expected.

[.my2]
当法官及/或陪审团作出裁决后，诉讼周期是否就此结束？这通常取决于当事方的诉求。除此之外，当事方可选择将案件上诉至上级法院。败诉方可提起上诉（成为上诉方），但上诉是一项权利而非法定义务。如果胜诉方获得的救济金额低于其预期，该方也可选择提起上诉。

The vast majority of 绝大多数的 disputes in the American legal system are settled out-of-court 庭外解决 through alternative dispute resolution (ADR) 替代性纠纷解决方式 methods before the actual litigation process begins in the courtroom 法庭.  10.ALTERNATIVE DISPUTE RESOLUTION AND CIVIL LITIGATION

Most cases in the American legal system are resolved out of the courtroom through alternative dispute resolution (ADR) before the dispute is brought into the courtroom through the civil litigation process. This chapter discusses various aspects of ADR and then focuses on the civil litigation process. In this process, ADR options (namely, mediation) can be set aside, temporarily or entirely, for a court-based civil litigation process and ruling.


---

=== Alternative Dispute Resolution

Alternative dispute resolution is a broad term attached to various methods of resolving disputes within the American legal system, including, but not limited to, arbitration, mediation, and negotiation. Each of these ADR approaches is covered in this section.

---

==== Arbitration

Arbitration represents one type of dispute resolution. It is often used to resolve disputes to get to a yes or no final determination on a particular issue or issues. Typically, arbitration is used when mediation has not been chosen or has failed. Although states can be involved in arbitration proceedings, private parties also are free to enter into arbitration proceedings so long as all relevant parties consent to arbitration proceedings.

Arbitration involves an impartial third party—referred to as the arbitrator—who renders a decision relating to a specific issue or issues in a dispute between two or more parties. The number of arbitrators varies, where having one, two, three, or even more arbitrators would be the norm, although they are usually in odd numbers to avoid an evenly split judgment. Each arbitrator is generally chosen by one or more of the relevant parties to the dispute, often selected based on the arbitrator’s knowledge and expertise in a field related to the dispute, which can include current or former attorneys, judges, academics, and/or other relevant experts.

An arbitration proceeding can be distinguished from a legal action heard by judges in a court in that an arbitration proceeding is typically private, whereas litigation in a court is typically public in nature. Thus, one benefit of a private proceeding, as in the case of arbitration, is that outside parties not directly related to the dispute are normally not allowed to listen in on the proceedings. This generally includes members of the press, and possibly other industry members, in which public disclosure (and even the mere public appearance in a lawsuit dispute) can result in less than positive consequences. For example, an article could be printed in a financial news publication that a particular lawsuit proceeding has commenced between two large firms, which could clearly have potentially negative effects on stock prices.

The parties to an arbitration proceeding also generally can decide whether the arbitration decision is binding on the parties as well as whether the opinion is appealable or nonappealable. The parties often opt for a nonappealable arbitration decision, because this can decrease the amount of time involved in the dispute before a final outcome is rendered. In contrast, in a public court system, depending on which court is the court of first instance to hear the dispute, one or more appeals can be allowed for, which can elongate the time it takes to get to a final dispute decision for months, if not years. Thus, in comparison to a public court system, arbitration can be faster and more cost-effective because of less time and resources required over a shorter period.

As a result of these cost and time advantages, within the American legal system, arbitration proceedings have become an increasingly preferred option to resolve disputes regarding domestic and cross-border dealings. Various organizations, such as the International Bar Association, have set broad-based arbitration rules, but many arbitration panels have the option to flexibly modify procedures and rules as deemed necessary and appropriate for the dispute in question. Arbitration is also available for any party, including states, corporations, and individuals, which makes it a viable alternative for actors in the international economic system.

---

==== Mediation

Much like arbitration, mediation also involves an impartial third party—referred to as the mediator—to resolve a particular dispute between two or more parties. But unlike an arbitration proceeding, a mediator’s decision is often not binding. As a result, a primary objective of a mediation session is often to have a trusted impartial third party to raise the communication lines between the parties to help find common ground to resolve the dispute. Mediation is often used at the early stages in a dispute—even, at times, before arbitration and litigation.

As in arbitration, the relevant parties must also consent to the mediation process. At least one or more of the parties also typically must consent to the mediator(s) chosen to resolve the dispute. Mediators, who are often but not always licensed attorneys and/or judges, are also generally chosen for their expertise and knowledge in a particular field deemed relevant to resolving the dispute. In principle, the mediation process is also open to most actors in the international economic system, including, but not limited to, states, private-sector entities, and individuals.

---

==== Negotiation

Another form of alternative dispute resolution is negotiation. Negotiation is the process by which disputes can be resolved and value can be created for the parties involved. As you may imagine, the degrees of formality of negotiations can vary greatly, from a formal negotiation setting in a conference room meeting to a simple e-mail exchange regarding terms and conditions to a particular agreement. At a more conceptual level, two main schools of thought exist relating to negotiations: positional bargaining and principled bargaining.

Positional bargaining occurs when parties’ negotiation focuses primarily on each of their respective positions (i.e., set terms and conditions, such as price, quantity, land area, and time), with little or no exploration of the other side’s interests (i.e., the rationale underlying one’s position). Positional bargaining is often equated to competitive and hard negotiations in which a clear winner or loser is sought.

Positional bargaining often involves some form of haggling or compromise, in which, for example, the price of a particular good is somewhere near the middle point of two extreme price points. For example, you may see positional bargaining at work at a weekend flea market for a used coffee table. The exchange may begin by the buyer offering the seller \$10, with a very quick counteroffer of \$100. Then comes the negotiation dance in which both sides, if interested, typically begin making concessions toward a price point somewhere near the middle point between \$10 and \$100.

Here, the negotiator(s) assume a fixed pie (or zero sum game). In this working assumption, the negotiators are in effect engaged in a land grab, whereby one party’s gain is directly correlated to the counterparty’s loss, similar to a sports competition. Thus, the more points (in the form of price, quantity, land area, time, etc.) gained from the other side, the better it is from a positional negotiator’s perspective, because the goal of positional bargaining is to gain as much as possible.

In contrast, principled (or integrative) bargaining involves a different approach. The approach is to not negotiate against the counterparty, but instead, to negotiate with the counterparty (in a form of us instead of you vs. me). In other words, by linking or integrating both sides, opportunities could be sought in a value-added way through various means.

The main school of thought on integrative bargaining is derived from arguably the most popular book related to negotiations, Getting to Yes, by Roger Fisher and William Ury of Harvard University’s Program on Negotiation. The authors suggest conducting integrative bargaining through the following structured framework of seven elements:

1. Communication (building effective and respectful communication)
2. Relationship (to understand and analyze the type of relationship wanted)
3. Interests (exploring common and shared interests)
4. Options (creating options, or brainstorming, in a form of two heads are better than one)
5. Legitimacy (deciding which options are preferable based on agreed-upon objective criteria)
6. Alternatives (choosing no to an offer based on the calculation that the offer by the counterparty is not better than one’s BATNA, which stands for the Best Alternative To a Negotiated Agreement, in essence, one’s opportunity cost or best walk-away alternative)
7. Commitment (choosing yes to an offer because it is better than one’s BATNA)


In principled negotiations, the spirit is to dig deeper into each party’s interests that exist underneath a party’s position. This is often done by simply asking “Why?” regarding a particular issue. For example, with the previous coffee table example, the buyer may want to ask why the seller wants \$100 for the table. It could be that an underlying reason exists to rationalize the price (e.g., a high quality of wood, historical significance) and vice versa (e.g., the buyer is a student with little cash and just wants basic cheap furniture for his dorm room). The thinking is that exploring each party’s underlying interests can sometimes, but not always, help to bridge the gap between differences in a negotiation setting.

Some may ask, “Which negotiation style is better?” Both have their advantages and disadvantages. So rather than giving a broad one-size-fits-all answer, it may be better to analyze the benefits and risks associated with each negotiation style. One advantage of positional bargaining is that it is relatively fast, because only the parties’ positions need to be focused on, and furthermore, identifying the positions (e.g., price, quantity, time) of the related parties is fairly easy. One risk is that a relationship is never forged, at least not a sustainable one, because the only point of focus is the parties’ positions, not on building a relationship.

Thus, the negotiation can lead to negotiator’s remorse, in which either or even both sides are left with a bad taste in their mouth after the negotiation (i.e., the parties do not feel satisfied even after agreement and/or would not be happy having to negotiate with each other again due to a lack of trust or goodwill). Especially in an interconnected and globalized world, in which professionals have only a few degrees of separation, not forging strong relationships can lead to a non-optimal negotiated outcome (i.e., getting to a yes in a deal for the mere sake of reaching an agreement, although the agreement may not substantively be best for the parties). This is often the case because the negotiated outcome is merely a halfway point between two extreme initial positions, whereby both parties fail to fully get what they want.

The advantage of principled (integrative) bargaining is that it focuses more on forging good communication methods to forge a strong relationship whereby creative solutions can result. This approach may lead to a higher chance of reaching a more optimal negotiated outcome. The risk of the principled bargaining strategy is that, because it involves more variables—exploring common interests, creating options together, and seeking legitimate standards—principled negotiations can often take longer to reach a negotiated outcome relative to the more straightforward and simplistic positional bargaining method.

In a more complex negotiation setting, taking more time and effort, the sessions often oscillate between principled and positional bargaining styles, but one takeaway lesson for practitioners and students of negotiation strategy is that more than one method of negotiation strategy can be used to reach your objective.

This section discussed the main alternative dispute resolution strategies and frameworks that exist. The next section focuses on conceptual approaches used by practitioners and others in terms of civil litigation, which often intersects with ADR.

=== Civil Litigation

What happens when a party is involved in a civil dispute and enters into civil litigation? And what is the litigation process in the event of a civil action lawsuit? The topics of civil action and civil procedure hold some key answers to these and other related questions. Litigation is the legal system’s mechanism for resolving disputes between parties. Civil procedure is the body of law that provides the structure and process related to such litigation. Litigation can be initiated by two or more individuals, companies, or other legal persons.

Civil action and procedure’s objectives include the following:

- Implementing substantive rules of law and related policy considerations
- Ensuring that the litigation process is fair and transparent
- Seeking an efficient process and outcome

When a lawsuit begins in the litigation process, two main considerations arise: (1) which court, or courts, could have jurisdiction to hear the case; and (2) if more than one court could conceivably have jurisdiction, which specific court would be most beneficial from a legal strategy standpoint for the lawyer’s client?

To establish jurisdiction, a court must assert: (1) subject matter jurisdiction over the substantive legal issue; and (2) personal jurisdiction over the relevant parties. Regarding personal jurisdiction, the courts have ruled that the U.S. Constitution requires that, in state courts, the defendant must have “minimum contacts” with the forum state (the state where the lawsuit was filed) in the spirit of fair play and justice. At the federal level, courts often have limited, rather than general, jurisdiction. Thus, in contrast to state courts, federal courts typically have jurisdiction over cases in specific categories.

When can a lawsuit be heard by federal courts? Federal courts generally have jurisdiction over two types of issues:

- Federal questions
- Diversity jurisdiction

Generally, federal questions involve issues such as the Constitution, federal statutes, and federal administrative rules. Diversity jurisdiction typically involves a dispute between parties from two different states. Through diversity jurisdiction, prejudice against out-of-state parties, if heard by a state court instead of a federal court, is prevented. In litigation, the plaintiff can normally choose the court in which to sue. However, the defendant can then ask to remove the case to another court (i.e., from a state to a federal court). When legal issues of two competing jurisdictions exist, an area of law known as conflict of laws arises.

---

==== A Brief Overview of a Civil Action Lawsuit: Complaint and Summons

A lawsuit begins with the plaintiff’s lawyer drafting and submitting a document known as a complaint. The document “complains” to the court that the defendant violated the plaintiff’s legal rights, which links to the facts relevant to the complaint. The complaint also asks the court for a specific remedy for the violation of such legal rights. A summons, which requests the defendant to legally respond to the complaint, is attached to the complaint.

The complaint is also part of the civil action litigation process by:

- Setting forth the basis for jurisdiction
- Putting the defendants on notice that the defendant is being sued, and stating the basis of the lawsuit
- Stipulating the issues of law and fact relevant to the case

Service of process is the next step in the litigation process. Service of process requires that the defendant be “served” a copy of the plaintiff’s complaint and summons, delivered by a process server, registered mail, police authority, or other person as required by the court that has jurisdiction in the case.

What legal options exist for the defendant once the plaintiff’s complaint has been served to the defendant?

1. Ignore the plaintiff’s complaint. In a criminal law case, this could lead to the possible arrest of the defendant for failure to appear in court. In a civil law case, the court could rule for a default judgment against the defendant. A default judgment prevents the defendant from then entering any legal defenses in the case.
2. Raise a legal objection in the form of a motion to dismiss. In a motion to dismiss, the defendant would argue that, among other things, no remedy exists for the plaintiff’s legal claim against the defendant. The motion to dismiss is a formal request to the court by the defendant to dismiss the case without the need to consider the substantive merits of the case.
3. Dispute the legal sufficiency of the plaintiff’s complaint through a demurrer. Another term for demurrer is a motion to dismiss for failure to state a claim, or failure to state a cause of action.
4. File an answer to the plaintiff’s complaint. The defendant’s answer can include denying, disclaiming, or admitting the plaintiff’s allegations, but stating the basis for the defendant’s defense in the lawsuit, referred to as an affirmative defense. In an affirmative defense, the defendant argues that even assuming the plaintiff’s assertions are true, the defendant’s assertions of facts and arguments will prevail over the plaintiff’s legal claims.

---

==== Discovery Process

How do parties discover the facts in a case? Parties discover the facts in a case through a process called discovery. Discovery is done at the pretrial stage of the lawsuit process.

Discovery methods include the following:

- Depositions: Interviews with other parties under oath
- Interrogatories: Written questions given to the counterparty
- Demand for evidence (motion for production of documents): Related to documents or other physical evidence
- Physical examination: Requires the other party to submit to a physical examination when relevant
- Requests for admissions: Asks the other party to admit the truth of the facts relevant to the litigation; once the relevant parties believe that certain facts are undisputed, then a request for admission can be issued, narrowing and defining the issues to be tried in the case.


The discovery process helps bring out all of the relevant evidence in a trial to achieve an efficient and transparent process to reach a fair outcome. It also helps the relevant attorneys to better and more clearly define the issues of fact and law in the case. This process enables both sides to bring forth the action and defenses to the case in a more optimal way.

The pretrial phase of the litigation is also an important time to reach a potential out-of-court settlement between the parties. In most U.S. jurisdictions, the majority of cases settle out-of-court before going to trial. Many jurisdictions also have mandatory programs of ADR in the form of arbitration, mediation, or mini-trial. If a pretrial out-of-court settlement cannot be reached between the parties, then the lawsuit moves to the trial phase.

---

==== Litigation and Civil Procedure at Trial and Beyond

The trial typically begins with opening statements by both the plaintiff and defendant. The first opening statement is by the plaintiff’s legal counsel, because the plaintiff has the burden of proof (under the working assumption of “innocent until proven guilty”). The defendant’s opening statement then follows.

The opening statements are followed by production of evidence and arguments pursuant to the rules of evidence of the court’s jurisdiction. This can include the calling and examination of witnesses in support of one side of the case. The production of evidentiary proof at trial is governed by the law of civil procedure as well as the law of evidence specific to the court’s jurisdiction. Civil procedure controls the order and method of the production of such evidentiary proof. The rules of evidence within civil procedure determine what types of evidence are permissible or not. For example, generally, evidence must be relevant to be admissible in a trial, but exceptions exist, such as when the evidence is harmful character evidence, and if it conflicts with legal policy.

Another example of a rule of evidence is the inadmissibility of hearsay evidence (secondhand testimony or out-of-court statement offered to prove the truth of a statement). For example, a statement such as “I heard John say that Joe (defendant) always drives while he’s drunk (intoxicated)” would normally be considered as hearsay evidence, because the person making the statement did not hear the fact directly from Joe, but instead heard it from John, a secondhand testimonial source.

The end of the trial involves closing arguments, which are a summary of each party’s case. If the trial is a jury trial, the judge would then instruct the jury on the relevant law. The jury’s main task is to make determinations based on issues of fact. Thus, a division of labor exists between the judge (rule of law) and jury (issues of fact) in a jury trial. In a bench trial (a trial without a jury), the judge would consider both issues of law and fact before then rendering a ruling on the case.

The Seventh Amendment to the U.S. Constitution and most state constitutions proclaim the right to a jury trial in civil action cases. Generally, if either party requests a jury trial, then a jury trial will be granted. Similarly, if both parties request a bench trial, then a bench trial is generally granted. In a jury trial, jury members should be representative of the general population and impartial. Thus, if legal counsel for a party can assert that a potential jury or actual jury member is biased, then this issue could be cause for removal of a particular juror.

After the judge and/or jury reaches a verdict, is this the end of the litigation cycle? It generally depends on what the parties are seeking. Among other things, the parties can opt to appeal the case to a higher court. The losing party can appeal (becoming the appellant party), but this is an option, not an obligatory duty. The winning party can also opt to appeal if the party received a partial remedy amount compared to what it expected.

The vast majority of disputes in the American legal system are settled out-of-court through alternative dispute resolution (ADR) methods before the actual litigation process begins in the courtroom. ADR approaches include mediation, arbitration, and negotiation. The civil litigation process controls the order and method of the production of such evidentiary proof. The rules of evidence within civil procedure determine what types of evidence are permissible. In a civil action lawsuit where civil litigation process applies, the litigation process typically begins with the plaintiff’s complaint and summons, which are then served to the defendant. This is followed by various options on how to respond to the plaintiff’s complaint and summons, including filing of a motion to dismiss, arguing for affirmative defenses, and even ignoring the complaint and summons, each resulting in various possible subsequent legal responses. Such ADR and civil litigation options exist for various related parties to seek legal remedies and justice under the American legal system.
