1.HISTORICAL THINKING SKILLS

The AP® World History: Modern course and exam require you to understand and use six key historical thinking skills. This means that you’re being asked to think like a historian. As a result, you’re expected to approach the exam equipped 准备好 to do the following. We’ll have more to say on these skills later.

SKILL 1. Identify and explain historical developments and processes.

Why do events and processes in history happen?

SKILL 2. Analyze sourcing and situation of primary and secondary sources.

Understand the point of view (POV) of documents.

SKILL 3. Analyze arguments in primary and secondary sources.

Explain why historians have different opinions about historical events.

SKILL 4. Analyze the context of historical events, developments, or processes.

Understand that history does not happen in a vacuum 在真空中；与世隔绝地.

SKILL 5. Using historical reasoning processes (see below), analyze patterns and connections between and among historical developments and processes.

Make sense of history with connections that go beyond facts.

SKILL 6. Develop an argument.

Take a stand 表明立场 on historical issues using supporting evidence.

2.HISTORICAL REASONING PROCESSES

In reasoning like a historian, you hone 磨炼；锤炼 an approach that shapes how you think. The following are the reasoning processes historians—and AP® World History: Modern exam candidates—use.

A.Comparison: Analyze similarities and differences between historical developments and processes.

B.Causation: Describe causes and effects of historical developments and processes and their significance; explain the context of historical developments.

C.Continuity and Change: Explain the significance of historical developments in patterns of change and continuity over time.

=== KEY TERMS  关键术语


=== KEY TERMS 关键术语

BUDDHISM 佛教 -- A “reform 改革；改进” of Hinduism 印度教 begun by Prince 王子 Siddhartha Gautama 乔达摩 c. 约 500 BCE 公元前, who became the Buddha 佛陀 (“Enlightened One 觉者；佛陀”). Unlike Hinduism, Buddhism supported spiritual 精神上的 equality 平等 and missionary 传教的 activity. Its two most popular forms 形式, Mahayana 大乘 and Theravada 上座部 Buddhism, spread far from its origins 起源 in South Asia into Southeast and East Asia along trade routes 贸易路线, bringing significant 重大的 cultural effects 影响 that last to this day.

[.my2]

佛教——由悉达多·乔达摩王子于约公元前500年开创的印度教“改革”，他后来成为佛陀（“觉者”）。与印度教不同，佛教支持精神平等和传教活动。其最流行的两种形式，大乘佛教和上座部佛教，从其南亚的起源地沿着贸易路线传播到遥远的东南亚和东亚，带来了持续至今的重大文化影响。

Neo-Confucianism 新儒学 -- It included aspects 方面 of Buddhism and the ancient Chinese belief system 信仰体系 Daoism 道教, promising eternal 永恒的 reward 奖赏；回报 for faithfulness 忠诚 to Confucian 儒家的 teachings 教义. Neo-Confucianism became prominent 突出的；重要的 during the Song Dynasty 宋朝 in China and spread to Japan and Korea.

[.my2]

新儒学——它包含了佛教和中国古代信仰体系道教的元素，承诺对儒家教义的忠诚会带来永恒的回报。新儒学在中国宋朝时期变得突出，并传播到日本和朝鲜。

DAR AL-ISLAM 伊斯兰世界 -- Across Afro-Eurasia 亚非欧大陆, Dar al-Islam means “everywhere Islam 伊斯兰教 is.” In the era 时代 c. 1200–c. 1450, this term 术语 described the territory 领土 extending 延伸 from Spain and Northwest Africa all the way to 一直到 South and Southeast Asia. Dar al-Islam was not a unified 统一的 political 政治的 empire 帝国, but a large region 地区 where Islamic faith 信仰 and culture was dominant 占主导地位的.

[.my2]

伊斯兰世界——在亚非欧大陆，Dar al-Islam 意为“伊斯兰所在之处”。在大约1200年至1450年这个时期，这个术语描述的是从西班牙和西北非一直延伸到南亚和东南亚的领土。伊斯兰世界不是一个统一的政治帝国，而是一个伊斯兰信仰和文化占主导地位的广大地区。

CALIPHATE 哈里发国 -- Unlike Christianity 基督教, Islam had no clear rules of succession 继承 after Muhammad 穆罕默德. Culturally, Islam united 联合 many peoples 民族, but politically, it fragmented 分裂 into regional states 国家 called caliphates, each led by a caliph 哈里发. The Abbasid 阿拔斯 caliphate ruled from Baghdad 巴格达 until it was sacked 洗劫 by the Mongols 蒙古人 in 1258.

[.my2]

哈里发国——与基督教不同，伊斯兰教在穆罕默德之后没有明确的继承规则。在文化上，伊斯兰教联合了许多民族，但在政治上，它分裂成称为哈里发国的区域性国家，每个都由一位哈里发领导。阿拔斯哈里发国统治着巴格达，直到1258年被蒙古人洗劫。

TURKIC PEOPLES 突厥民族 — Central Asian nomadic 游牧的 peoples who had a common language family 语系 called Turkish 突厥语族. Their migrations 迁徙 into Southwest Asia beginning in the 10th century ushered in 迎来 a period of political domination 统治 by Turkish groups 群体 such as the Seljuk 塞尔柱 and later the Ottomans 奥斯曼 that lasted until the 20th century. These Turkish groups converted 皈依 to Islam and established 建立 regional states called sultanates 苏丹国, named after 以…命名 the leader of the Turkish group, a Sultan 苏丹.

[.my2]

突厥民族——中亚的游牧民族，拥有一个共同的语系，称为突厥语族。他们从10世纪开始向西南亚迁徙，迎来了一个由突厥群体（如塞尔柱人及后来的奥斯曼人）政治统治的时期，这一直持续到20世纪。这些突厥群体皈依了伊斯兰教，并建立了称为苏丹国的区域性国家，以该突厥群体的领袖苏丹的名字命名。

SYNCRETISM IN RELIGIONS 宗教融合 — A global religion 全球性宗教 must be flexible 灵活的 enough to adapt 适应 to local customs 习俗 as it spreads. Many examples of the global diffusion 传播 of religion exist: when Buddhism spread into East Asia, the Buddha became a god-like 神一般的 provider 提供者 of eternal salvation 永恒的救赎；as Islam spread into parts of South Asia, it adopted 采用 some Hindu 印度教的 features 特征 and turned toward 转向 mysticism 神秘主义 with Sufism 苏菲派；some forms 形式 of Christianity in the New World adopted traditional gods 神 and made them part of the pantheon 万神殿 of saints 圣徒.

[.my2]

宗教融合——一个全球性宗教在传播时必须足够灵活，以适应地方习俗。存在许多宗教全球传播的例子：当佛教传播到东亚时，佛陀变成了一个神一般的永恒救赎提供者；随着伊斯兰教传播到南亚部分地区，它采用了一些印度教的特征，并通过苏菲派转向了神秘主义；新世界的一些基督教形式吸纳了传统的神灵，并使他们成为圣徒万神殿的一部分。

INCA AND MEXICA (AZTEC) EMPIRES 印加和墨西加（阿兹特克）帝国 — Centered 以…为中心 in the Andes Mountains 安第斯山脉 in western South America, the Inca civilization 文明 was built on previous cultures. Their empire extended 延伸 along the western coast 海岸, a result of both conquest 征服 and diplomacy 外交. Pivotal 关键的 to Incan economic strength 经济实力 was the mit’a 米塔 system, which imposed 强制实行 forced labor 强制劳役 on indigenous 土著的 peoples. This system allowed for 使得 the creation 创造 of a massive 大规模的 mandatory 强制性的 public works 公共工程 program 项目 that made possible 使成为可能 an extensive 广泛的 road network 道路网, construction 建造 of public buildings, and later, under the Spanish, mining 采矿 (see entry No. 33). Its influence 影响 peaked 达到顶峰 in the 15th century but declined 衰落 rapidly when Spanish conquistadors 征服者 arrived in the early 16th century. Similarly, the Mexica (Aztec) empire was built upon 建立在…基础上 features of earlier cultures, like the Maya 玛雅, and fell 覆灭 after the arrival 到来 of the Spanish. Its capital 首都 was located 位于 in central Mexico.

[.my2]

印加和墨西加（阿兹特克）帝国——印加文明以南美洲西部的安第斯山脉为中心，建立在先前文化的基础上。他们的帝国沿着西海岸延伸，这是征服和外交共同作用的结果。印加经济实力的关键是米塔制，这是一种强加于土著人民的强制劳役制度。该制度使得创建一个大规模的强制性公共工程项目成为可能，从而建立了广泛的道路网，建造了公共建筑，后来在西班牙人统治下用于采矿（见条目33）。其影响力在15世纪达到顶峰，但在16世纪初西班牙征服者到来后迅速衰落。同样，墨西加（阿兹特克）帝国建立在早期文化（如玛雅）特征的基础上，并在西班牙人到来后覆灭。其首都位于墨西哥中部。

EUROPEAN EXPLORATIONS 欧洲探索 — Seeking 寻求 an increase 增加 in the trade of spices 香料, silk 丝绸, and other goods from East and Southeast Asia, kings from new European nations sent expeditions 探险队 around Africa into the Indian Ocean and also across the Atlantic Ocean. Started by Portugal, then Spain, France, Britain, and the Netherlands (a.k.a. 亦称 the Dutch 荷兰人), these explorers 探险家 initiated 发起 the first truly global contacts 联系 and ushered in 迎来 the rise 崛起 of European influence 影响 around the world.

[.my2]

欧洲探索——为了寻求增加来自东亚和东南亚的香料、丝绸和其他商品的贸易，新兴欧洲国家的国王们派遣探险队绕过非洲进入印度洋，也横渡大西洋。这些探险家由葡萄牙发起，随后是西班牙、法国、英国和荷兰（亦称 the Dutch），他们发起了第一次真正的全球联系，并迎来了欧洲在世界各地影响力的崛起。

ATLANTIC WORLD 大西洋世界 — The Atlantic World includes the people, politics, religions, goods, and ideas that crossed back and forth 来回地 on the Atlantic Ocean after Columbus’s journeys 旅程 connected 连接 Europe, Africa, and North and South America. This term is especially important in the years 1450–1900.

[.my2]

大西洋世界——大西洋世界包括在哥伦布的航行连接了欧洲、非洲和南北美洲之后，在大西洋上来回流动的人民、政治、宗教、商品和思想。这个术语在1450-1900年间尤为重要。

MERCANTILISM 重商主义 — The west European governments 政府 of Portugal, Spain, Britain, France, and the Dutch expanded 扩张 their new maritime 海上的 empires 帝国 into worldwide power 世界性力量 because of the Columbian Exchange 哥伦布大交换. Each established 建立 mercantilism as an example 例子 of economic nationalism 经济民族主义. Under mercantilist 重商主义的 policies 政策, nations developed 发展 colonies 殖民地 in the Americas and Asia and used them to provide 提供 raw materials 原材料 such as sugar 糖, furs 毛皮, silver 白银, and lumber 木材. These products 产品 were then processed 加工 and sold by companies from the owner (mercantilist) nation all over the world. Each nation competed 竞争 to amass 积累 as many colonies as possible as a sign 标志 of economic and political power. Silver mining 银矿开采 and trade were the foundation 基础 of mercantilist system.

[.my2]

重商主义——葡萄牙、西班牙、英国、法国和荷兰的西欧政府，由于哥伦布大交换，将其新的海上帝国扩张为世界性的力量。各国都将重商主义作为经济民族主义的一个范例建立起来。在重商主义政策下，各国在美洲和亚洲发展殖民地，并用它们来提供诸如糖、毛皮、白银和木材等原材料。这些产品然后由来自（重商主义）宗主国的公司加工并销往世界各地。各国竞相积累尽可能多的殖民地，作为经济和政治实力的标志。白银开采和贸易是重商主义体系的基础。

ENCOMIENDA AND MIT’A SYSTEMS 监护征赋制和米塔制 — The encomienda 监护征赋制 system was a Spanish land-use 土地使用 practice 做法 in its American colonies and the Philippines. Spanish settlers 定居者 were granted 被授予 tracts 大片 of land and permitted 被允许 to use the native 当地的 people already living on that land as indentured servants 契约仆役. The mit’a system was a form of labor (e.g., road building) required 被要求的 by the Incan government, and adopted 被采用 by the Spanish for gold mining 金矿开采.

[.my2]

监护征赋制和米塔制——监护征赋制是西班牙在其美洲殖民地和菲律宾的一种土地使用做法。西班牙定居者被授予大片土地，并获准使用已居住在该土地上的当地人作为契约仆役。米塔制是印加政府要求的一种劳役形式（例如，筑路），并被西班牙人采用来开采金矿。

INDUSTRIALIZATION 工业化 — The Industrial Revolution 工业革命 began in Britain in the mid-18th century 18世纪中叶 and was a major part 主要部分 of the West’s enormous 巨大的 social changes and economic and political expansions 扩张 in the nineteenth century. It marks 标志着 the shift 转变 from slow hand-made 手工制作的 to rapid machine-made 机器制造的 production 生产. Industrialization spread to Russia, South and East Asia, and North and South America by the end of the 19th century.

[.my2]

工业化——工业革命始于18世纪中叶的英国，是19世纪西方巨大的社会变革及经济政治扩张的主要部分。它标志着从缓慢的手工生产向快速的机器生产的转变。到19世纪末，工业化已传播到俄罗斯、南亚和东亚以及南北美洲。

THE ENLIGHTENMENT 启蒙运动 — Like the Industrial Revolution, the Enlightenment was a western European development 发展 that had tremendous 巨大的 global impact 影响. With foundations 基础 in scientific study 科学研究 and intellectual 智力的 reason 理性, its basic tenets 基本原则 included individual rights 个人权利 such as freedom of speech 言论自由 and participation 参与 in government. It greatly influenced 影响 the American and French Revolutions, which then inspired 激励 political revolutions around the world.

[.my2]

启蒙运动——与工业革命一样，启蒙运动是一场对全球产生巨大影响的西欧发展。其基础在于科学研究和智性理性，其基本原则包括个人权利，如言论自由和参政。它极大地影响了美国革命和法国革命，进而激励了世界各地的政治革命。

AGE OF REVOLUTIONS 革命时代 — During the mid-19th-century 19世纪中叶 “Age of Isms 主义时代” in western Europe (see entry Nos. 41 through 44), many revolutions seeking 寻求 political and social change occurred, inspired 受…启发 by the ideas of the Enlightenment and the French Revolution. In Latin America, most countries successfully revolted 反抗 against European political control 政治控制.

[.my2]

革命时代——在19世纪中叶西欧的“主义时代”（见条目41至44），受启蒙运动和法国革命思想的启发，发生了许多寻求政治和社会变革的革命。在拉丁美洲，大多数国家成功反抗了欧洲的政治控制。

19TH-CENTURY MIGRATIONS 19世纪移民潮 — The Industrial Revolution included improvements 改进 in transportation 交通 that made ocean travel 海上旅行 safer and cheaper. Pushed 被推动 by revolutions and poor living conditions 生活条件, and pulled 被吸引 by stories 传闻 of opportunities 机会, millions—especially Europeans but also South and East Asians—migrated 迁移 to North and South America in the 19th century.

[.my2]

19世纪移民潮——工业革命带来了交通的改善，使得海上旅行更安全、更便宜。数百万人——主要是欧洲人，也包括南亚和东亚人——在19世纪迁移到南北美洲，他们受到革命和恶劣生活条件的推动，并被关于机会的传闻所吸引。

INDENTURED SERVITUDE 契约劳工制 — A form of coercive 强制的 labor where a person exchanges 交换 his or her work for a period of time—usually a few years—for transportation 交通 and necessities 生活必需品, without other pay 报酬. After the term 期限 of indenture 契约 was completed, the laborer 劳工 was supposed 应该 to be freed. Indentured servitude was a factor 因素 in migrations to the Americas in the 17th and 18th centuries, but was replaced 被取代 by slavery 奴隶制. By the end of the 19th century, slavery in the West had ended and indentured servitude returned. In that era, thousands of South Asians migrated to South Africa and the Caribbean 加勒比海地区 as agricultural 农业的 indentured servants. East Asians were also employed 被雇用 as indentured servants in the Americas.

[.my2]

契约劳工制——一种强制性的劳动形式，个人以其工作交换一段时期（通常是几年）的交通和生活必需品，没有其他报酬。在契约期限完成后，劳工应获得自由。契约劳工制是17和18世纪向美洲移民的一个因素，但后来被奴隶制取代。到19世纪末，西方的奴隶制已经结束，契约劳工制卷土重来。在那个时代，成千上万的南亚人作为农业契约劳工迁移到南非和加勒比地区。东亚人也在美洲被雇用为契约劳工。

“SECOND” INDUSTRIAL REVOLUTION “第二次”工业革命 — The “first” Industrial Revolution involved 涉及 the mechanization 机械化 of agriculture 农业 and textiles 纺织业, but in the last half 后半叶 of the 19th century, its focus 焦点 changed to innovations 创新 in electricity 电力 (telephone 电话 and radio 无线电), chemistry 化学 (fertilizers 化肥), transportation 交通 (cars 汽车 and airplanes 飞机), and steel 钢铁 (skyscrapers 摩天大楼 and modern weapons 现代武器). These developments 发展 continued to influence 影响 the rapid social and economic changes in the West into the 20th century.

[.my2]

“第二次”工业革命——“第一次”工业革命涉及农业和纺织业的机械化，但在19世纪后半叶，其焦点转向了电力（电话和无线电）、化学（化肥）、交通（汽车和飞机）以及钢铁（摩天大楼和现代武器）方面的创新。这些发展持续影响了西方快速的社会和经济变革，直至20世纪。

THE WORLD WARS 世界大战 -- The results 结果 of the wars were the decline 衰落 of western Europe and the rise 崛起 of the power 力量 of the U.S. and Soviet Union 苏联 in the second half 后半叶 of the 20th century.

[.my2]

世界大战——战争的结果是西欧的衰落以及美国和苏联力量在20世纪后半叶的崛起。

THE GREAT DEPRESSION 大萧条 — Two major results 主要结果 were the rise 兴起 of authoritarian 威权主义的 governments and the outbreak 爆发 of World War II.

[.my2]

大萧条——两个主要结果是威权主义政府的兴起和第二次世界大战的爆发。

AUTHORITARIANISM 威权主义 — One result 结果 of the catastrophe 灾难 of World War I was a rejection 摒弃 of democratic 民主的 forms of government in parts of Europe and Asia, namely 即, Germany, Italy, Russia, and Japan. Single-party 一党 rule led by a strongman 强人 with dictatorial 独裁的 powers 权力 was thought to be a more efficient 高效的 system than democracy. Communism 共产主义 and fascism 法西斯主义 were the best-known examples 例子 of such governmental 政府的 systems. The growing military aggression 军事侵略 of the fascist 法西斯的 governments was a cause 原因 of World War II.

[.my2]

=== KEY TERMS


BUDDHISM -- A “reform” of Hinduism begun by Prince Siddhartha Gautama c. 500 BCE, who became the Buddha (“Enlightened One”). Unlike Hinduism, Buddhism supported spiritual equality and missionary activity. Its two most popular forms, Mahayana and Theravada Buddhism, spread far from its origins in South Asia into Southeast and East Asia along trade routes, bringing significant cultural effects that last to this day.


Neo-Confucianism -- It included aspects of Buddhism and the ancient Chinese belief system Daoism, promising eternal reward for faithfulness to Confucian teachings. Neo-Confucianism became prominent during the Song Dynasty in China and spread to Japan and Korea.

DAR AL-ISLAM -- Across Afro-Eurasia, Dar al-Islam means “everywhere Islam is.” In the era c. 1200–c. 1450, this term described the territory extending from Spain and Northwest Africa all the way to South and Southeast Asia. Dar al-Islam was not a unified political empire, but a large region where Islamic faith and culture was dominant.

CALIPHATE -- Unlike Christianity, Islam had no clear rules of succession after Muhammad. Culturally, Islam united many peoples, but politically, it fragmented into regional states called caliphates, each led by a caliph. The Abbasid caliphate ruled from Baghdad until it was sacked by the Mongols in 1258.


TURKIC PEOPLES — Central Asian nomadic peoples who had a common language family called Turkish. Their migrations into Southwest Asia beginning in the 10th century ushered in a period of political domination by Turkish groups such as the Seljuk and later the Ottomans that lasted until the 20th century. These Turkish groups converted to Islam and established regional states called sultanates, named after the leader of the Turkish group, a Sultan.

SYNCRETISM IN RELIGIONS — A global religion must be flexible enough to adapt to local customs as it spreads. Many examples of the global diffusion of religion exist: when Buddhism spread into East Asia, the Buddha became a god-like provider of eternal salvation; as Islam spread into parts of South Asia, it adopted some Hindu features and turned toward mysticism with Sufism; some forms of Christianity in the New World adopted traditional gods and made them part of the pantheon of saints.


INCA AND MEXICA (AZTEC) EMPIRES — Centered in the Andes Mountains in western South America, the Inca civilization was built on previous cultures. Their empire extended along the western coast, a result of both conquest and diplomacy. Pivotal to Incan economic strength was the mit’a system, which imposed forced labor on indigenous peoples. This system allowed for the creation of a massive mandatory public works program that made possible an extensive road network, construction of public buildings, and later, under the Spanish, mining (see entry No. 33). Its influence peaked in the 15th century but declined rapidly when Spanish conquistadors arrived in the early 16th century. Similarly, the Mexica (Aztec) empire was built upon features of earlier cultures, like the Maya, and fell after the arrival of the Spanish. Its capital was located in central Mexico.


EUROPEAN EXPLORATIONS — Seeking an increase in the trade of spices, silk, and other goods from East and Southeast Asia, kings from new European nations sent expeditions around Africa into the Indian Ocean and also across the Atlantic Ocean. Started by Portugal, then Spain, France, Britain, and the Netherlands (a.k.a. the Dutch), these explorers initiated the first truly global contacts and ushered in the rise of European influence around the world.


ATLANTIC WORLD — The Atlantic World includes the people, politics, religions, goods, and ideas that crossed back and forth on the Atlantic Ocean after Columbus’s journeys connected Europe, Africa, and North and South America. This term is especially important in the years 1450–1900.


MERCANTILISM — The west European governments of Portugal, Spain, Britain, France, and the Dutch expanded their new maritime empires into worldwide power because of the Columbian Exchange. Each established mercantilism as an example of economic nationalism. Under mercantilist policies, nations developed colonies in the Americas and Asia and used them to provide raw materials such as sugar, furs, silver, and lumber. These products were then processed and sold by companies from the owner (mercantilist) nation all over the world. Each nation competed to amass as many colonies as possible as a sign of economic and political power. Silver mining and trade were the foundation of mercantilist system.


ENCOMIENDA AND MIT’A SYSTEMS — The encomienda system was a Spanish land-use practice in its American colonies and the Philippines. Spanish settlers were granted tracts of land and permitted to use the native people already living on that land as indentured servants. The mit’a system was a form of labor (e.g., road building) required by the Incan government, and adopted by the Spanish for gold mining.

INDUSTRIALIZATION — The Industrial Revolution began in Britain in the mid-18th century and was a major part of the West’s enormous social changes and economic and political expansions in the nineteenth century. It marks the shift from slow hand-made to rapid machine-made production. Industrialization spread to Russia, South and East Asia, and North and South America by the end of the 19th century.


THE ENLIGHTENMENT — Like the Industrial Revolution, the Enlightenment was a western European development that had tremendous global impact. With foundations in scientific study and intellectual reason, its basic tenets included individual rights such as freedom of speech and participation in government. It greatly influenced the American and French Revolutions, which then inspired political revolutions around the world.


AGE OF REVOLUTIONS — During the mid-19th-century “Age of Isms” in western Europe (see entry Nos. 41 through 44), many revolutions seeking political and social change occurred, inspired by the ideas of the Enlightenment and the French Revolution. In Latin America, most countries successfully revolted against European political control.

19TH-CENTURY MIGRATIONS — The Industrial Revolution included improvements in transportation that made ocean travel safer and cheaper. Pushed by revolutions and poor living conditions, and pulled by stories of opportunities, millions—especially Europeans but also South and East Asians—migrated to North and South America in the 19th century.

INDENTURED SERVITUDE — A form of coercive labor where a person exchanges his or her work for a period of time—usually a few years—for transportation and necessities, without other pay. After the term of indenture was completed, the laborer was supposed to be freed. Indentured servitude was a factor in migrations to the Americas in the 17th and 18th centuries, but was replaced by slavery. By the end of the 19th century, slavery in the West had ended and indentured servitude returned. In that era, thousands of South Asians migrated to South Africa and the Caribbean as agricultural indentured servants. East Asians were also employed as indentured servants in the Americas.

“SECOND” INDUSTRIAL REVOLUTION — The “first” Industrial Revolution involved the mechanization of agriculture and textiles, but in the last half of the 19th century, its focus changed to innovations in electricity (telephone and radio), chemistry (fertilizers), transportation (cars and airplanes), and steel (skyscrapers and modern weapons). These developments continued to influence the rapid social and economic changes in the West into the 20th century.


THE WORLD WARS -- The results of the wars were the decline of western Europe and the rise of the power of the U.S. and Soviet Union in the second half of the 20th century.

THE GREAT DEPRESSION —   Two major results were the rise of authoritarian governments and the outbreak of World War II.

AUTHORITARIANISM — One result of the catastrophe of World War I was a rejection of democratic forms of government in parts of Europe and Asia, namely, Germany, Italy, Russia, and Japan. Single-party rule led by a strongman with dictatorial powers was thought to be a more efficient system than democracy. Communism and fascism were the best-known examples of such governmental systems. The growing military aggression of the fascist governments was a cause of World War II.

DECOLONIZATION — A major global development after World War II was the end of Europe’s colonial empires around the world. Colonies in South Asia, Southeast Asia, and Africa regained their independence some peacefully, others through violence. The new independent states often faced significant social, economic, and political challenges.


COLD WAR — The dominant global conflict after World War II, the Cold War was conducted between the U.S. (and its allies) and the Soviet Union (and its allies). The aim for each side was to keep the other from increasing its political and economic influence globally. It was called the Cold War because the two sides did everything to prepare for a real “hot” war (with real weapons) except actually fight each other directly. This led to proxy wars like the Korean War, the Angolan Civil War, and the Sandinista-Contras Conflict in Nicaragua. Massive accumulation of nuclear and other forms of weapons threatened mutually assured destruction, but when the Soviet Union fell apart in the late 20th century, the Cold War ended.

MULTINATIONAL OR TRANSNATIONAL CORPORATIONS — A multinational or transnational corporation does business in more than one country. The British and Dutch East India companies of the 18th century were early examples, but it was after World War II that this business model became common. Today, Exxon Mobil, Toyota, and General Electric are prominent multinational corporations.
