== THE POSTWAR ECONOMIC BOOM 战后经济繁荣

To Hong Kong officials, the American and UN embargoes  often seemed to be aimed at hurting (v.) Hong Kong’s economy, but they were “_a boon in disguise_ (伪装物,伪装) 塞翁失马；因祸得福.”  +

[.my1]
.案例
====
.boon in disguise

“*A boon in disguise*” 是一个英语习语,由更著名的习语 “a blessing (幸事,福祉) in disguise”​ 变化而来.  它的意思是 “*看似坏事,实则好事*”​ 或 “因祸得福”.

boon：恩惠,好处,非常有用的东西.    +
in disguise：伪装起来的,化装的.   +
字面直译：“伪装起来的恩惠”​ 或 “以伪装形式出现的好处”.

这个习语描述了：一件起初看起来是坏事、麻烦或挫折的事情,最终却带来了意想不到的巨大好处.

- Losing that job was _a boon in disguise_; it pushed me to start my own successful business.
（丢掉那份工作其实是因祸得福；它促使我创办了自己的成功企业.  ）
- The injury was _a blessing in disguise_​ as it gave him time to reflect and change his career path.
（这次受伤塞翁失马,因为它让他有时间反思并改变了自己的职业道路.  ）
====

The embargoes forced (v.) Hong Kong to shift *from* entrepôt 转运港,转口的；中转的 trade *to* manufacturing 制造业, without which /Hong Kong might never have attained  its level of economic prosperity  after the war.  +


This new emphasis on _light industrialization_ 工业化 was not the result of any action `后定说明` taken by the colonial  government, which in fact continued to view (v.) industry with some skepticism (n.)怀疑；质疑 while continuing to stress (v.) Hong Kong’s status  as a trading port.  +

[.my2]
这种对轻工业的新重视,并非源于殖民政府采取的任何举措.  事实上,殖民政府一方面仍对工业持一定怀疑态度,另一方面则不断强调香港作为"贸易港口"的地位.

Rather, the initiative 倡议；主动行动 #came from# local Chinese entrepreneurs 企业家；创业者, who realized that /while `主` *smuggling (v.) goods into China* under the embargoes `谓` could be highly lucrative 获利多的；赚大钱的 /it was risky and unreliable, #and from# the influx  of Chinese entrepreneurs from the mainland.

[.my1]
.案例
====
To Hong Kong officials, the American and UN embargoes often seemed to be aimed at hurting Hong Kong’s economy, but they were “a boon in disguise.” 7 The embargoes forced Hong Kong to shift from entrepôt trade to manufacturing, without which Hong Kong might never have attained its level of economic prosperity after the war.  +
This new emphasis on light industrialization was not the result of any action taken by the colonial government, which in fact continued to view industry with some skepticism while continuing to stress Hong Kong’s status as a trading port.  +
Rather, the initiative came from local Chinese entrepreneurs, who realized that while smuggling goods into China under the embargoes could be highly lucrative it was risky and unreliable, and from the influx of Chinese entrepreneurs from the mainland.

在香港官员看来,美国和联合国的禁运似乎往往是为了损害香港经济,但实际上却是“因祸得福”.   7.  +
禁运迫使香港从转口贸易转向制造业,否则香港战后或许无法达到如此繁荣的经济水平.  这种对轻工业化的新重视并非殖民政府采取的任何行动的结果,事实上,殖民政府在继续强调香港作为贸易港口地位的同时,仍然对工业抱有一定的怀疑态度.  相反,这一举措来自本地华人企业家,他们意识到,尽管在禁运期间走私货物到中国内地利润丰厚,但风险极高且不可靠；此外,来自大陆的华人企业家的涌入也起到了推波助澜的作用.   +
====


`主` The Chinese of Hong Kong, *whether* long-term residents *or* recent immigrants, `系` were most responsible for Hong Kong’s post-1949 industrial boom, *both* as producers 生产者,制造者 *and* consumers 消费者.  +

[.my2]
无论是长期居民还是新移民,香港的华人都是1949年以后香港工业繁荣的最大贡献者,他们既是生产者,也是消费者.

But Hong Kong’s colonial status  also played an important role in this remarkable economic transformation, as did some of the factors `后定说明` that had shaped (v.) Hong Kong’s economic development /since it became a British colony: its economic and political stability, deep harbor and good port, and steady supply of cheap labor from China.  +

[.my2]
但香港的殖民地位, 在这场非凡的经济转型中同样发挥了重要作用,自成为英国殖民地以来塑造香港经济发展的诸多因素, 亦是如此：经济与政治稳定、深水良港的天然优势,以及来自中国内地源源不断的廉价劳动力供给.

Whereas （表示对比）但是,然而 in 1953 only 30 percent of Hong Kong’s exports 出口产品 were produced locally, by 1959 this proportion 比例 had risen to 70 percent, #with# the colony #producing# (v.) and exporting _a wide variety 多样化,变化；种类 of_ 各种各样的 manufactured goods 制成品 such as textiles 纺织品 and clothing, plastic toys and flowers, flashlights 手电筒 and batteries, and aluminum 铝, enamel 搪瓷制品, and rattan ware 藤器.  +

[.my2]
1953年,香港仅有30%的出口商品为本地生产；而到1959年,这一比例已升至70%.  当时香港生产并出口各类制成品,包括纺织品与服装、塑料玩具和塑料花、手电筒与电池,以及铝制品、搪瓷制品和藤制品.


[.my1]
.案例
====
这里的 with ... doing 结构是 “with + 宾语 + 现在分词”​ 构成的"独立主格结构".

#*独立主格的核心特征是：拥有自己的逻辑主语,且该主语与"主句主语"不同.   +
这个结构在句中充当 伴随状语,用来补充说明主句动作（比例上升）发生的 背景、方式或伴随情况.  *#

with：引导词,表示“伴随状态”.   +
the colony：逻辑主语（即“doing”这个动作的发出者）.   +
producing and exporting：现在分词（doing）,表示主动进行的动作.

Nor could this rapid industrialization 工业化 have occurred /without _the trading and banking networks_   built up over the previous century.  +

Chinese industrialists 实业家；工业家 *relied heavily on* loans 贷款 from British banks, which enjoyed the favor 青睐；支持 of the colonial regime 政权；统治机构.  +

And whereas historians have generally assumed 假定；认为 that /Hong Kong’s rise to a regional financial center 区域金融中心 began in the late 1960s and early 1970s, economic historian Catherine Schenk argues that /this rise actually began in the 1950s, when Hong Kong *was “profoundly affected 深刻地影响” by* its position in the international monetary (a.)  system 国际货币体系 *and by* its colonial status.  +

According to Schenk, the colonial _monetary system_ 货币体系 provided _currency stability_ 货币稳定 in _the increasingly regulated 受管制的 international system_ that developed in the postwar period. +

[.my2]
申克认为,殖民时期的货币体系, 在战后逐步形成、监管日益严格的国际体系中,为货币稳定提供了保障.

Hong Kong’s status as *both* a British colony *and* an Asian entrepôt 转口港；贸易中转站 thus laid (v.) the foundation 奠定基础 for its emergence   as an international financial center, while `主` the political instability  in other parts of East Asia `谓` increased Hong Kong’s economic competitiveness  竞争力.



[.my1]
.案例
====
The Chinese of Hong Kong, whether long-term residents or recent immigrants, were most responsible for Hong Kong’s post-1949 industrial boom, both as producers and consumers.  +
But Hong Kong’s colonial status also played an important role in this remarkable economic transformation, as did some of the factors that had shaped Hong Kong’s economic development since it became a British colony: its economic and political stability, deep harbor and good port, and steady supply of cheap labor from China.  +
Whereas in 1953 only 30 percent of Hong Kong’s exports were produced locally, by 1959 this proportion had risen to 70 percent, with the colony producing and exporting a wide variety of manufactured goods such as textiles and clothing, plastic toys and flowers, flashlights and batteries, and aluminum, enamel, and rattan ware.  +
Nor could this rapid industrialization have occurred without the trading and banking networks built up over the previous century.  +
Chinese industrialists relied heavily on loans from British banks, which enjoyed the favor of the colonial regime.  +
And whereas historians have generally assumed that Hong Kong’s rise to a regional financial center began in the late 1960s and early 1970s, economic historian Catherine Schenk argues that this rise actually began in the 1950s, when Hong Kong was “profoundly affected” by its position in the international monetary system and by its colonial status.  +
According to Schenk, the colonial monetary system provided currency stability in the increasingly regulated international system that developed in the postwar period.8 Hong Kong’s status as both a British colony and an Asian entrepôt thus laid the foundation for its emergence as an international financial center, while the political instability in other parts of East Asia increased Hong Kong’s economic competitiveness.

香港华人,无论是长期居民还是新移民,都是 1949 年后香港工业繁荣的主要推动者,他们既是生产者也是消费者.  但香港的殖民地地位也在这一非凡的经济转型中发挥了重要作用,其他一些因素也同样影响着香港自成为英国殖民地以来的经济发展：经济和政治的稳定、深水良港以及来自中国内地的廉价劳动力供应.  1953 年,香港出口产品中只有 30%是本地生产的,而到 1959 年,这一比例上升至 70%,香港生产和出口的制成品种类繁多,包括纺织品和服装、塑料玩具和花卉、手电筒和电池,以及铝制品、搪瓷制品和藤制品.  如果没有上个世纪建立起来的贸易和银行网络,这种快速的工业化也不可能发生.  中国实业家严重依赖英国银行的贷款,而这些银行也深受殖民政权的青睐.  尽管历史学家普遍认为香港崛起为区域金融中心始于 20 世纪 60 年代末 70 年代初,但经济史学家凯瑟琳·申克（Catherine Schenk）认为,这一崛起实际上始于 20 世纪 50 年代,当时香港深受其在国际货币体系中的地位及其殖民地地位的影响.  申克指出,在战后时期日益规范化的国际体系中,殖民地货币体系为香港提供了货币稳定性.  8 香港作为英国殖民地和亚洲转口港的地位,为其成为国际金融中心奠定了基础,而东亚其他地区的政治不稳定则提高了香港的经济竞争力.   +
