在您句子中的具体分析

By 1964, almost 500,000 squatters were living in hillside shacks​ or rooftop huts.

- hillside shacks(山坡棚屋)：
用 shack非常精准，因为非法占地者 (squatters) 在陡峭山坡上私自搭建的居所，必然是材料廉价、结构危险、随时可能倒塌的临时建筑.  这个词强调了居住环境的极度恶劣和非法性.

- rooftop huts(天台小屋)：
用 hut也很合适.  在天台上搭建的小屋，虽然也简陋，但可能结构相对完整，有明确的墙壁和屋顶，是家庭为扩展居住空间而有意建造的.  它依然拥挤不堪，但“小屋”的意味比“破棚子”稍强一丝.

▶ DeepSeek:

是的，这是一个 “否定副词前置”​ 引起的部分倒装.


原句：#Nor#, despite..., #was it# particularly anti-British. +
正常语序：#It was not# particularly anti-British, despite...

倒装过程：
##**句首使用了否定词（或含有否定意义的词）Nor.  按照语法规则，需要将句子的谓语动词的一部分（助动词/情态动词/系动词）​ 提前到主语之前，**##构成“否定词 + 助动词/系动词 + 主语 + 其余部分”的结构.

本句中，谓语是 was（系动词），所以将 was提前到主语 it之前，形成 was it.

Nor表示“也不”，意味着前文已经陈述了一个否定情况，本句是递进的另一个否定情况.

例: +
#Nor do I# agree with you.（我也不同意你.  ） +
#Never have I# seen such a beautiful sight.（我从未见过如此美景.  ） +
#Not only was he# late, but he also forgot the documents.（他不仅迟到了，还忘了带文件.  ） +

After an official inquiry, Elsie Elliot, who was known as an advocate for the poor and for trying to expose police corruption—and whose name the hunger striker had invoked because Elliot had solicited public opinion on whether the fare increases were acceptable—was censured for provoking the demonstrators and making “unsubstantiated” allegations against the police.  +
Although the inquiry commission could offer no other explanation, it insisted that the riots had not been caused by economic, political, or social conditions.  +
Some of the youths admitted to having joined the riots mainly to protest the fare increases, but others claimed to have done so out of the excitement of the moment, for fun, or simply to let off steam.  +
One of the riot leaders later committed suicide; another ended up in a mental hospital.  +
Still, the inquiry commission conceded that overcrowding, the struggle to maintain a living, and “the underlying insecurity of life in the Colony, resulting from international political and economic conditions, create tensions which elsewhere would be more than sufficient cause for frequent disturbances.”17
