By the end of the nineteenth century, Hong Kong was the only British colony with such a limited form of self-government.  +
Although the Chinese community rarely questioned this system, many Europeans believed that they deserved a larger role in running the colony they had helped build.  +
In 1894, the Constitutional Reform Association petitioned for the right to elect British nationals as unofficial members in the Legislative Council and for more control over the colony’s finances.  +
The British government, however, rejected this as a poorly disguised attempt to gain power for Europeans.  +
Secretary for the Colonies Lord Ripon, formerly the viceroy of India, argued that because Hong Kong had “become a Chinese rather than a British community” and Chinese settlement formed the “one main element in its prosperity,” he could not sanction any representation that would exclude the Chinese.3 The Colonial Office also feared that giving in to any European demands would encourage the Chinese to make similar requests.  +
Although he raised the possibility of appointing a Chinese to the Executive Council, Lord Ripon warned that granting representation to Europeans would mean giving more representation to the Chinese.
到十九世纪末, 香港是唯一一个自治权如此有限的英国殖民地。尽管华人社群很少质疑这一制度, 但许多欧洲人认为, 他们理应在自己参与建设的殖民地管理中扮演更重要的角色。1894 年, 宪政改革协会请愿, 要求选举英国国民作为非官方成员进入立法会, 并要求对殖民地财政拥有更大的控制权。然而, 英国政府驳回了这一请愿, 认为这是欧洲人企图攫取权力的拙劣伎俩。曾任印度总督的殖民地事务大臣里彭勋爵辩称, 由于香港“已成为华人而非英国人聚居地”, 华人定居是“香港繁荣的主要因素”, 因此他不能批准任何排除华人参与的代表权。殖民地事务部也担心, 如果对任何欧洲人的要求做出让步, 都会鼓励华人提出类似的要求。尽管里彭勋爵提出了任命一名中国人进入行政委员会的可能性, 但他警告说, 给予欧洲人代表权就意味着给予中国人更多的代表权。 +

.案例
====
Ironically, the 1894 petition for constitutional reform ended up benefiting the Chinese community more than the European community.  +
The Legislative Council was increased by one official and one unofficial member, but the latter was Wei Yuk, a comprador and entrepreneur appointed in 1896.  +
In May 1896, Lord Ripon’s successor, Joseph Chamberlain, ordered that two unofficial members be added to the Executive Council.  +
They were to be selected “at the discretion of the Governor” from the unofficial members of the Legislative Council and “by consideration of personal merit” and with “no reference to the particular class or race.”4 A Chinese was not appointed to the Executive Council until 1926, but given how the 1894 movement for reform inadvertently benefited the Chinese community, the European community did not seriously mention constitutional reform again until after World War I.
具有讽刺意味的是, 1894 年的宪政改革请愿最终使华人社区比欧洲社区受益更多。立法委员会增加了一名官方成员和一名非官方成员, 但后者是魏玉, 一位买办和企业家, 于 1896 年被任命。1896 年 5 月, 里彭勋爵的继任者约瑟夫·张伯伦下令在行政委员会中增加两名非官方成员。这两名成员将“由总督酌情决定”从立法委员会的非官方成员中选出, “以个人功绩为准”, 且“不考虑特定阶级或种族”。直到 1926 年, 才有一位华人被任命为行政委员会成员。但鉴于 1894 年的改革运动无意中使华人社区受益, 欧洲社区直到第一次世界大战后才再次认真讨论宪政改革。 +
