为什么会多出一个“of”？ befitting是动词 befit的现在分词, 但常被当作形容词使用, 意为“合适的, 相称的”。

英语中许多描述“特性、属性”的形容词, 常与 “of”​ 搭配, 表示“具有...特性的”。例如： +

worthy of(值得...的) +
人们可能错误地类比这些结构, 将 befitting也接上了 of, 从而产生了 befitting of这个非标准但广泛使用的短语。 +

.案例
====
In 1891, Hong Kong observed its fiftieth anniversary under British rule with a celebration truly befitting of a proper British jubilee: ships decorated with lights, church services at the Anglican and Catholic cathedrals, a royal salute fired from ships in the harbor, a review of military forces at the horse racing course in Happy Valley, sporting contests, and a public ball at City Hall.  +
Although the jubilee reiterated the familiar, self-congratulating themes of British colonialism, it was truly a collaborative affair, for local European, Chinese, Eurasian, and Indian leaders all helped organize the festivities.  +
This collaboration was reflected in the jubilee rhetoric, which emphasized Hong Kong as a meeting point of East and West: “a curious commentary on the relative characteristics of Western and Oriental enterprise.”

1891年, 香港庆祝其在英国统治五十周年, 其庆典盛况空前, 堪称一场名副其实的英国庆典：船只灯火辉煌, 圣公会和天主教堂举行礼拜仪式, 港口船只鸣放皇家礼炮, 在跑马地赛马场检阅军队, 举办体育比赛, 并在市政厅举行公众舞会。尽管庆典重申了英国殖民主义惯用的自夸主题, 但它确实是一场合作盛会, 因为当地的欧洲、中国、欧亚混血和印度裔领袖都参与了庆典的组织工作。这种合作精神也体现在庆典的宣传语中, 强调香港是东西方文化的交汇点：“这巧妙地诠释了东西方企业各自的特点。” +

.案例
====
Jubilees and other commemorations create idealized representations of societies and their histories.  +
What the 1891 jubilee rhetoric did not explain was that the segregated Chinese and European communities often remained highly suspicious of each other, as would become evident during the bubonic plague of 1894.  +
Many disgruntled Europeans wanted more political representation and a larger role in managing the colony they had helped to build.  +
Alarmed by the rise of Chinese nationalism and the potential threats from other powers in the late 1800s, these Europeans also became increasingly worried about the colony’s defense, a fear partly assuaged by Britain’s acquisition of the New Territories in 1898.  +
And even as they became more concerned about the fate of their ancestral homeland, many Hong Kong Chinese—especially among the rising bourgeoisie—began to see themselves as a special group of Chinese.

周年庆典和其他纪念活动往往会塑造社会及其历史的理想化形象。1891年的周年庆典言辞未能解释的是, 当时隔离的华人社区和欧洲人社区之间常常存在着高度的猜忌, 这一点在1894年的黑死病疫情中暴露无遗。许多心怀不满的欧洲人渴望获得更多的政治代表权, 并在他们曾参与建设的殖民地管理中扮演更重要的角色。随着19世纪末中国民族主义的兴起以及其他列强潜在威胁的出现, 这些欧洲人也日益担忧殖民地的防御, 而英国在1898年对新界的收购在一定程度上缓解了他们的这种担忧。即便他们越来越关注祖籍国的命运, 许多香港华人——尤其是新兴资产阶级——也开始将自己视为一个特殊的华人群体。 +
